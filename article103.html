LE SSERAFIM's Sakura Opens Up About Hate Comments And Learning to 'Not Care'

April 2, 2026 By Yuki Tanaka 📖 5 min read

In a candid new interview, LE SSERAFIM's Sakura has spoken openly about dealing with hate comments and the emotional journey she's taken toward self-acceptance.

The Japanese member, who transitioned from the J-pop idol scene to K-pop with LE SSERAFIM, acknowledged that adjusting to the different fan culture hasn't always been easy. "When I first started receiving comments in Korean, I couldn't understand them," she shared. "But even after learning to read them, there were moments when the negativity affected me deeply."

Learning to Tune Out the Noise

Sakura revealed that she's developed coping mechanisms to deal with the pressures of being a public figure. "Over time, I learned to not care as much. It's not that I don't see the comments—it's that I choose not to let them define me or affect my work," she explained.

The singer emphasized the importance of focusing on the positive aspects of being an artist. "There are so many fans who support us with genuine love, and that's what I choose to focus on. Their messages give me energy and remind me why I do this."

Growth as an Artist

LE SSERAFIM has been on an upward trajectory since their debut, with each comeback showing artistic growth. Sakura credits the group's bond with helping her navigate the challenges of the industry. "We lean on each other a lot. When one member is struggling, the others are there to support them," she said.

The group is currently preparing for a summer comeback, with Sakura teasing that fans can expect "something different" from their previous releases.
