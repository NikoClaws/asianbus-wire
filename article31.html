ZEROBASEONE Split: Zhang Hao, Ricky, Gyuvin, Yujin Form ANDOUBLE For May Debut

March 18, 2026 By Sarah Kim 📖 9 min read

In a major development for the post-ZEROBASEONE landscape, YH Entertainment (formerly Yuehua Entertainment) has confirmed that four former members of the beloved project group will debut as a brand new idol group called ANDOUBLE on May 26, 2026. Zhang Hao, Ricky, Kim Gyuvin, and Han Yujin will reunite under the new banner, marking one of the most anticipated K-pop debuts of the year and providing closure to fans who have been anxiously waiting to see how the members would continue their careers after ZEROBASEONE's conclusion.

According to an exclusive report from Ilgan Sports published on March 18, the four members—all of whom were under YH Entertainment before their ZEROBASEONE days—will form the core of ANDOUBLE's lineup. However, the final member count and complete roster have yet to be revealed, with the agency confirming that additional members will be announced sequentially in the coming weeks leading up to the May debut.

The news comes just days after ZEROBASEONE wrapped up their final activities, bringing an emotional end to a project group that had captured the hearts of millions worldwide. For many fans, the announcement of ANDOUBLE represents both an ending and a new beginning—a chance to continue supporting artists they've grown to love while also embracing what promises to be a fresh creative direction.

The Road From Boys Planet To ANDOUBLE

The journey to ANDOUBLE traces back to 2023, when Mnet's survival program Boys Planet captivated audiences across Asia and beyond. Zhang Hao, Ricky, Kim Gyuvin, and Han Yujin were among the contestants who successfully made it into ZEROBASEONE, the final group composed of the show's top vote-getters. Their debut in July 2023 marked the beginning of what would become a highly successful 2.5-year project.

During their time with ZEROBASEONE, all four members distinguished themselves in unique ways. Zhang Hao emerged as a fan favorite for his vocal abilities and charismatic stage presence, while Ricky became known for his striking visuals and dance skills. Kim Gyuvin won hearts with his energetic personality and variety show appeal, and Han Yujin impressed with his all-rounder capabilities and growth throughout the group's tenure.

"The synergy between these four members was always evident," noted K-pop industry analyst Park Ji Young. "They share a history at YH Entertainment that predates their ZEROBASEONE days, and there's a natural chemistry that fans have always noticed. Debuting them together as a permanent group makes strategic sense."

ZEROBASEONE's Final Chapter

ZEROBASEONE's activities were originally scheduled to conclude on January 10, 2026, in line with the standard 2.5-year contract for Mnet survival show groups. However, in a decision that demonstrated the strong bond between all nine members, the group unanimously agreed to extend their activities by two additional months.

This extension allowed them to release a special farewell album in February and conduct their emotionally charged "Here & Now" encore concerts from March 12-15 at KSPO Dome in Seoul Olympic Park. The three-day concert series drew tens of thousands of fans and served as a fitting send-off for a group that had achieved remarkable success in a relatively short period.

"These past three years have been the most precious time of my life. ZEROBASEONE will always be a part of who I am, but I'm also excited to show you what comes next." — Kim Gyuvin, during the final encore concert

The final performances were marked by tears, heartfelt speeches, and promises to support each other in their future endeavors—promises that the ANDOUBLE announcement seems to be fulfilling for the YH Entertainment members.

What About The Other Five?

While four members move forward with ANDOUBLE, the remaining five ZEROBASEONE members—Sung Hanbin, Kim Jiwoong, Seok Matthew, Kim Taerae, and Park Gunwook—will continue activities as a reorganized five-member version of ZEROBASEONE. This decision has sparked mixed reactions among fans, with some celebrating the continuation of the ZEROBASEONE name and others feeling nostalgic for the original nine-member lineup.

The five-member ZEROBASEONE will operate under the original management structure, likely continuing their relationship with WAKEONE Entertainment, the HYBE joint venture that managed the project group. Details about their future plans, including potential new music or tours, have not yet been announced.

This split creates an interesting dynamic in K-pop—two distinct groups emerging from one, each carrying different aspects of ZEROBASEONE's legacy forward. Industry observers note that this approach could actually expand the combined fanbase, as supporters now have multiple ways to continue their journey with their favorite artists.

ANDOUBLE: What's In A Name?

Sharp-eyed fans and industry trackers had noticed YH Entertainment filing trademark applications for multiple variations of the group name in recent months, including "ANDOUBLE," "AND2BLE," and "&DOUBLE." The final stylization for English-language markets has not been officially confirmed, though "ANDOUBLE" appears to be the primary spelling based on the announcement.

The name itself has prompted speculation and analysis among fans. The "AND" prefix suggests addition, continuation, and connection—fitting themes for a group that represents the next chapter for artists fans already know. "DOUBLE" could reference the dual nature of the members' careers (pre and post-ZEROBASEONE), the bond between members, or the group's intention to deliver twice the impact.

Numerology enthusiasts have also noted that the group will debut on May 26 (5/26), with the numbers potentially being significant—though official confirmation of any symbolic meaning behind the date remains pending.

YH Entertainment's Ambitious Plans

For YH Entertainment, the formation of ANDOUBLE represents a major investment and statement of intent. The agency, which operates under the larger Yuehua Entertainment umbrella that spans operations across China and South Korea, has been working to establish a stronger foothold in the K-pop market.

The company's previous attempts at launching K-pop groups have met with varying degrees of success, but ANDOUBLE arrives with significant built-in advantages. The four confirmed members already possess substantial individual fanbases developed during their ZEROBASEONE days, providing a foundation that most rookie groups can only dream of.

"This is a calculated move by YH Entertainment," explained entertainment industry consultant Lee Min Ho. "By adding new trainees to proven talents, they get the best of both worlds—established stars who can draw immediate attention, plus fresh faces who can help define a new group identity. It's a smart strategy for maximizing debut impact."

The addition of new members to the existing four also creates opportunities for interesting group dynamics and content. Fans will likely be eager to see how the established members mentor and interact with newcomers, creating narrative hooks that can drive engagement across social media and content platforms.

Fan Reactions: Joy, Tears, And Anticipation

The announcement has sent shockwaves through fan communities, generating an outpouring of emotional responses across social media platforms. For many ZEROBASEONEfans—known as ZEROSE—the news represents the culmination of weeks of speculation and anxiety about their favorite members' futures.

Trending topics related to ANDOUBLE and the four members quickly climbed global Twitter trends within hours of the announcement. Fan accounts dedicated to the members have already begun transitioning their focus to the new group, while fan artists have started creating concept art imagining what ANDOUBLE's visual identity might look like.

"I've been preparing myself for this day since we knew ZEROBASEONE would end," wrote one fan on a popular K-pop forum. "But actually seeing the confirmation that they'll stay together... I'm crying happy tears. They deserve this. We deserve this."

However, not all reactions have been uniformly positive. Some fans have expressed concern about the decision to add new members to the group rather than keeping it as a four-member unit. Others worry about potential overshadowing of new trainees by the established stars, or conversely, that the addition of unknowns could dilute the group's appeal.

The May Countdown Begins

With just over two months until the May 26 debut date, anticipation is already building to fever pitch. YH Entertainment is expected to begin rolling out teasers, member reveals, and promotional content in the coming weeks, following the established K-pop playbook for building pre-debut hype.

Key questions remain unanswered: How many total members will ANDOUBLE have? What musical direction will they pursue? Will they maintain connections to ZEROBASEONE's sound, or forge an entirely new identity? Will there be pre-debut variety content or a reality show documenting the group's formation?

For Zhang Hao, Ricky, Kim Gyuvin, and Han Yujin, ANDOUBLE represents not just a continuation of their idol careers, but an opportunity to establish themselves outside the context of a temporary project group. Unlike ZEROBASEONE, which always had an expiration date, ANDOUBLE is presumably intended as a long-term venture—a chance to build something lasting.

As one industry insider noted: "The transition from project group to permanent group is always challenging, but these four have the talent, the experience, and the fanbase to make it work. If YH Entertainment plays their cards right, ANDOUBLE could be one of the biggest debut stories of 2026."
