BIGBANG's Coachella Set Breaks The Internet — 17 Songs, Daesung's Trot Segment, And A Refused NYT Interview

By Ji-won Lee April 13, 2026

INDIO, CALIFORNIA — BIGBANG's Coachella comeback delivered far more than fans bargained for. The K-pop legends performed 17 songs across 60 minutes at the Outdoor Theatre on April 12, and the internet has not stopped talking about it since — from Daesung's surprise trot segment to the group's reported decision to decline an interview with The New York Times.

The three-person lineup of G-Dragon, Taeyang, and Daesung opened with "Bang Bang Bang" and rolled through a setlist that read like a greatest hits marathon: "Fantastic Baby," "Mental Sober," "Loser," "Day by Day," "Lies," "Bad Boy," and "We Like 2 Party." Fan accounts documented the show in real-time, with comments like "Seeing Fantastic Baby at Coachella — 20 years of experience and I've never lost my live spark" flooding social media.

Daesung's Desert Trot Moment

The most unexpected moment of the night came when Daesung turned the middle of the American desert into a trot stage, performing "Over the Limit" and "Look at Me Defect." After the song, he asked the crowd — apparently stunned by their recognition — "How do you know this song?" The moment sent shockwaves through both the live audience and fans watching the Coachella YouTube stream.

One fan account wrote: "Exceeding the limit in Coachella??? What is the concept of Korean trot in Coachella?" Others praised Daesung for bringing an authentic Korean music tradition to one of the world's biggest festival stages.

Taeyang brought the heat with his solo performance of "Ringa Ringa," while G-Dragon exploded across the stage with "Power Power." The two also performed their collaboration "Good Boy" together, showcasing the chemistry that has defined BIGBANG for two decades.

The "Still Life" Finale and T.O.P's Shadow

The emotional peak of the set came with the finale: "Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter Still Life," the 2022 track released during a difficult period for the group. When T.O.P's verse resonated through the speakers — the first time his recorded vocals had appeared on a BIGBANG stage since his departure — fans reported being overwhelmed with emotion. Even without T.O.P physically present, the song's reference to cycles of life and change resonated deeply with audiences.

Refusing The New York Times

Perhaps the most talked-about aspect of the evening — beyond the performance itself — was BIGBANG's decision to decline an interview request from The New York Times. The refusal quickly became a trending topic in Korea and internationally, with fans praising the group for setting boundaries while others debated the optics of declining one of the world's most prominent publications.

G-Dragon reportedly told the crowd: "Tonight's Coachella stage means a lot to us," and later predicted: "The 20th anniversary of Big Bang has just begun." The group returns for Coachella's second weekend on April 19.

Meanwhile, coachella's YouTube channel saw an influx of "King of K-pop" and "#BANGCHELLA" comments in the hour leading up to the performance, reflecting the intense anticipation surrounding the group's return to the global stage.
