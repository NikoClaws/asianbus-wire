TXT Returns With Deeply Personal EP '7th Year: A Moment of Stillness in the Thorns' After Contract Renewal

April 13, 2026 By Brandon Lee 📖 7 min read

K-pop boy group Tomorrow X Together (TXT) returned on April 13 with their eighth EP, 7th Year: A Moment of Stillness in the Thorns — a candid reflection on their career and personal growth following the recent renewal of their contracts with Big Hit Music.

Departing from the broader conceptual narratives of youth and growth found in past projects — which include the Dream Chapter, Chaos Chapter, Star Chapter, and minisode series — the group's new release takes a deeply personal turn. The six-track EP dropped at 6 PM KST and marks the quintet's first project since all members renewed their contracts in August 2025 after spending seven years with the agency.

"Thorns" and the Symbolism of Seven Years

According to the agency, the word "Thorns" in the title symbolizes the inner unease and uncertainty experienced by the members over the last seven years. At a media showcase at Korea University in Seoul, the members detailed the introspective focus of the new record, describing it as their most honest work to date.

"This album focuses on the honest emotions we've felt leading up to the renewal process," member Hueningkai said during the showcase, adding that the project represented a significant emotional milestone for the group. Taehyun elaborated: "We asked ourselves what the most honest story we could tell at this point was, and we agreed it should reflect the emotions we've felt over the past seven years."

The group had long conversations with the production team about their thoughts and the kind of music they wanted to make before creating the album. Yeonjun and Taehyun opened up about the hidden anxieties behind the group's success, noting the gap between early ideals and reality, as well as the constant pressure to reach higher. The EP represents a willingness to show vulnerability rather than maintain a polished image.

The Music

The six-track EP is fronted by electro-pop lead single "Stick With You," alongside B-sides "Bed of Thorns," "Take Me to Nirvana (feat. Vindina Weng)," "So What," and "21st Century Romance." Soobin described "Stick With You" as a track about the desperation of clinging to a fading love, while Beomgyu noted the song carries an emotional weight that reflects the group's current headspace.

The contract renewal for all five members — Soobin, Beomgyu, Taehyun, Hueningkai, and Yeonjun — was announced in August 2025 and widely seen as a significant vote of confidence in the group's future direction. TXT debuted in 2019 and has grown into one of K-pop's most critically acclaimed groups, known for blending pop-rock sensibilities with deeply narrative-driven concepts.

Initial Reception

Early responses to the EP have been overwhelmingly positive, with critics praising TXT's willingness to experiment with a more mature sound and vulnerable lyrics. Music show appearances are scheduled throughout April, with the group set to perform on M Countdown, Music Bank, and Inkigayo in the coming weeks. Streaming numbers have been strong, with "Stick With You" debuting in the top 10 on Spotify's global viral chart within hours of release.

For TXT, this album marks not just a musical evolution but a statement about their identity as artists who are willing to grow and change alongside their fans. As Taehyun noted during the showcase: "We know we've changed a lot since we debuted. This album is proof of that change — and proof that we're not afraid to show who we really are."
