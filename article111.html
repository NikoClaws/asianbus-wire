BTS' 'Arirang' Makes History with Second Week at Billboard 200 #1

BTS becomes the first K-pop act ever to top the Billboard 200 for two consecutive weeks

By Sarah Chen • April 6, 2026
6 min read

BTS has achieved what no K-pop act has ever done before. Their album "Arirang" has returned to #1 on the Billboard 200 for a second consecutive week, making BTS the first K-pop artist in history to achieve this feat.

The album earned 187,000 equivalent album units in the latest tracking week, a remarkable number that exceeds even optimistic industry predictions. This achievement solidifies BTS's position as the most successful K-pop group in Billboard chart history.

A Historic Achievement

The consecutive #1 weeks mark a significant milestone not just for BTS, but for K-pop's presence in the Western market. While K-pop has achieved individual hit songs and viral moments, sustained album sales at the top of the charts represents a different level of commercial success.

Industry analysts have noted that "Arirang"'s success appears to be driven by genuine album sales rather than streaming alone, suggesting fans are purchasing physical and digital copies rather than just streaming.

Global Impact

The achievement has been celebrated internationally, with major Western media outlets highlighting BTS's continued dominance despite their military service hiatus. The group's ability to maintain this level of success while members were completing mandatory military service has impressed industry observers.

BTS is currently on their ARIRANG World Tour, which kicked off on April 9 in Seoul. The tour is expected to further boost album sales as attending fans receive exclusive physical copies.

About BTS

BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan) is a South Korean boy band consisting of RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook. They are known for their social activism and record-breaking music.
