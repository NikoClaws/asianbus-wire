ASTRO's Cha Eunwoo Returns to Public Eye Amid Tax Evasion Scandal

March 17, 2026 By Selena Jung 📖 8 min read

The "Face Genius" idol-actor Cha Eunwoo made his first public appearance today since being embroiled in tax evasion allegations that have threatened to derail his once-stellar career, addressing journalists and fans in an emotional press conference that may determine whether South Korea's most photographed face can survive its biggest scandal.

Cha Eunwoo, whose real name is Lee Dong-min, arrived at the Seoul Press Center at approximately 2:00 PM KST, flanked by representatives from his agency Fantagio Entertainment. The 29-year-old ASTRO member, visibly thinner than in recent photographs, bowed deeply before assembled media for a full ten seconds before taking his seat at the podium.

The press conference, which lasted approximately 45 minutes, marked the end of nearly three weeks of silence from the star since news of the National Tax Service investigation first broke. During that period, Cha Eunwoo had cancelled all scheduled appearances and remained entirely absent from social media, fueling speculation about the severity of the allegations.

The Allegations: What We Know

Reports first surfaced in late February alleging that Cha Eunwoo had significantly underreported income from his extensive portfolio of brand endorsements and acting projects. According to documents leaked to Korean media outlet Dispatch, the discrepancy between reported and actual income may exceed 2 billion won (approximately $1.5 million USD).

The National Tax Service launched a formal investigation following an audit that flagged irregularities in tax filings dating back to 2023. Sources familiar with the investigation suggest that the issues center primarily on income from overseas endorsement deals and appearance fees from events in China and Southeast Asia.

Cha Eunwoo has been one of K-entertainment's most prolific endorsers, with his face appearing on products ranging from luxury watches to instant noodles. Industry estimates suggest his annual endorsement income alone exceeds 10 billion won, not including his acting and music earnings.

"The complexity of international entertainment income creates genuine opportunities for error, but the amounts allegedly involved here are substantial enough to warrant serious scrutiny. This isn't a rounding error—this is a systematic underreporting over multiple years." — Tax attorney speaking to JoongAng Ilbo on condition of anonymity

Cha Eunwoo's Statement: "I Take Full Responsibility"

At today's press conference, Cha Eunwoo delivered a prepared statement before taking questions from journalists. His voice wavered noticeably at several points during the address, and he appeared to be fighting back tears throughout.

"I stand before you today with a heart full of regret and shame," the star began. "I have let down my fans, my colleagues, my agency, and the public who have supported me throughout my career. For this, I am truly and deeply sorry."

Cha Eunwoo explained that he had delegated most financial matters to his management team and personal accountants, but emphasized that he does not view this as an excuse. "As the person who earned this income, the ultimate responsibility is mine. I should have been more diligent in understanding and overseeing my financial affairs."

"I have fully cooperated with the National Tax Service investigation from the moment it began. I have paid all outstanding taxes, penalties, and interest. I have also made a substantial donation to social welfare causes as an expression of my sincere remorse. But I know that money cannot undo the damage to the trust that fans placed in me." — Cha Eunwoo, press conference statement

The star confirmed that he has settled approximately 3.2 billion won with tax authorities, including back taxes, penalties, and interest. He also revealed a 500 million won donation to underprivileged children's education programs.

Public Reaction: A Nation Divided

Cha Eunwoo's apology has generated dramatically divided reactions among the Korean public. Supporters have praised his willingness to accept responsibility and his decision to address the matter publicly rather than hiding behind corporate statements.

Fan communities, particularly ASTRO's AROHA fandom, have rallied behind the star. The hashtag #WeStandWithEunwoo trended number one in Korea following the press conference, with fans sharing messages of support and arguing that the matter should be considered resolved given his cooperation and payment.

Critics, however, argue that Cha Eunwoo's privileged position and clean-cut image made his tax evasion particularly egregious. Online communities have highlighted the contrast between his luxury lifestyle and the struggles of ordinary Koreans to meet their tax obligations.

"He's been selling himself as this perfect, hardworking, humble guy for years," wrote one highly-upvoted comment on Naver. "Now we find out he's been cheating on taxes the whole time? His whole image was a lie."

Impact on Ongoing Projects

The scandal has already had significant professional consequences for Cha Eunwoo. Three major brands—including a global luxury fashion house and a domestic skincare company—have terminated their endorsement contracts, representing an estimated loss of over 4 billion won in annual income.

More critically, questions remain about the fate of his drama projects. Cha Eunwoo was set to begin filming a highly anticipated historical drama for tvN next month. While the network has not officially cancelled his casting, sources indicate that producers are actively considering replacements.

"Advertisers and networks are extremely risk-averse when it comes to tax scandals," explained entertainment industry analyst Park Min-jung. "In Korea, tax evasion is viewed as a particularly serious moral failing. Many celebrities have recovered from dating scandals or even minor legal issues, but tax problems are career-enders for some."

The ASTRO Factor

The scandal has raised difficult questions about Cha Eunwoo's future with ASTRO, the six-member group that launched his career in 2016. The group has been on an extended hiatus, with members pursuing individual activities, but had been rumored to be planning a reunion comeback later this year.

Fellow ASTRO members have not publicly commented on the controversy, though member MJ posted a cryptic Instagram story featuring a sunset and the caption "This too shall pass" that fans interpreted as a message of support.

Industry insiders suggest that Fantagio is carefully evaluating how to proceed with ASTRO's group activities. "There's a concern that Eunwoo's individual scandal could negatively impact the other members' careers," one source explained. "But there's also recognition that ASTRO fans want to see the group together, and that group activities might actually help with Eunwoo's rehabilitation."

Industry Context: Tax Scandals in K-Entertainment

Cha Eunwoo joins a list of Korean celebrities who have faced tax-related scandals, though outcomes have varied widely. Some stars, like actor Song Hye-kyo, successfully rehabilitated their careers after resolving tax issues. Others have seen their careers effectively ended.

The severity of consequences often depends on factors including the amount involved, the perceived intentionality of the evasion, the celebrity's response, and broader public sentiment. Cha Eunwoo's proactive approach—including immediate payment and public apology—mirrors strategies employed by celebrities who successfully recovered.

Tax experts note that the Korean entertainment industry's complex payment structures create genuine challenges for accurate reporting. "When you have income flowing from multiple countries, in multiple currencies, through various corporate entities, mistakes can happen even with good faith efforts," acknowledged tax accountant Lee Jae-won. "But that's why high-earning celebrities need to invest in proper professional guidance."

What Happens Next

The National Tax Service investigation is expected to conclude within the next month. While criminal prosecution is unlikely given Cha Eunwoo's cooperation and full payment, the final determination will influence how the public and industry partners view the matter going forward.

Fantagio Entertainment has announced that Cha Eunwoo will take a "period of reflection" before resuming public activities. The length of this hiatus remains undetermined, but sources suggest a minimum of two to three months.

For Cha Eunwoo personally, the coming months will be critical in determining whether his career can survive intact. His youth, track record, and the manner of his apology all work in his favor. Against him are the substantial sums involved and Korea's particularly unforgiving attitude toward tax-related misconduct.

As one industry veteran summarized: "Korea has forgiven a lot from its celebrities—scandals, controversies, even crimes. But money matters hit different. People can relate to personal failings. Tax evasion feels like cheating everyone. Whether Eunwoo can come back depends on whether the public believes he's genuinely sorry or just sorry he got caught."
