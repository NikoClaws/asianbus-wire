Lovelyz Mijoo Sparks Backlash After Grilling On Apartment Balcony

March 17, 2026 By Grace Kim 📖 7 min read

Former Lovelyz member and variety star Lee Mijoo has found herself at the center of an unexpected controversy after posting Instagram photos and videos of herself grilling meat on her apartment balcony. The seemingly innocuous content has sparked intense debate, with critics accusing the popular entertainer of being inconsiderate to neighbors and potentially violating building regulations.

The controversy erupted when Mijoo shared a series of Instagram stories showing her enjoying a solo grilling session on what appeared to be her high-rise apartment balcony. The 28-year-old entertainer, known for her bubbly personality and comedic timing, captioned the posts with cheerful comments about enjoying the nice weather and treating herself to some quality samgyeopsal (Korean pork belly).

What Mijoo likely intended as relatable, lifestyle content quickly turned sour when Korean netizens began criticizing her for the smoke and smell that balcony grilling produces in apartment complexes. The backlash intensified as the posts spread beyond her Instagram followers to broader online communities.

The Neighbor Complaints

In South Korea, where the majority of the urban population lives in high-rise apartment complexes, balcony etiquette is a serious matter. Grilling on balconies is a particularly contentious issue, as smoke and food odors can easily drift into neighboring units, affecting residents who may be allergic, health-conscious, or simply bothered by the smell.

"This is so inconsiderate," wrote one commenter on a popular online community. "Does she not realize that her neighbors have to deal with all that smoke filling their homes? Some people have respiratory issues. Some are vegetarians who don't want to smell cooking meat. This is basic apartment living common sense."

Others pointed out that many Korean apartment buildings have explicit rules against balcony grilling, which can not only cause neighbor disputes but also trigger fire alarms and potentially violate fire safety codes. While it's unclear whether Mijoo's specific building has such rules, critics argued that a public figure should know better.

"When you live in an apartment, you have to think about your neighbors. Celebrities aren't exempt from basic courtesy. If anything, they should be more careful because they're supposed to be role models." — Comment from Naver user receiving over 10,000 upvotes

Defenders Speak Up

However, Mijoo's substantial fanbase quickly mobilized in her defense, arguing that the backlash was disproportionate to the alleged offense. Many pointed out that grilling on balconies, while potentially annoying, is hardly a serious transgression worthy of public condemnation.

"Are we really doing this? Canceling someone over grilling meat?" wrote one defender. "She wasn't hurting anyone. She wasn't doing anything illegal in most cases. This is the kind of everyday thing millions of Koreans do. Why is it suddenly a scandal when a celebrity does it?"

Supporters also noted Mijoo's general good character and the positive energy she brings to the entertainment industry. Since Lovelyz's disbandment in 2021, Mijoo has become one of Korea's most beloved variety show personalities, known for her genuine and unpretentious demeanor. Fans argued that this minor incident shouldn't overshadow her positive contributions.

Some commenters suggested that the intense reaction reflected Korea's sometimes excessive scrutiny of celebrity behavior, where public figures are held to impossibly high standards and criticized for behaviors that would pass unnoticed if done by ordinary citizens.

The Broader Context Of Celebrity Controversies

This incident arrives at a peculiar moment in K-pop and Korean celebrity culture, where the threshold for what constitutes a "controversy" seems to lower continuously. In recent years, idols and celebrities have faced backlash for everything from how they sit to what they eat, leading some observers to question whether public discourse has become excessively nitpicking.

"There's a pattern here," observed media critic Jung Min Soo. "We see celebrities being criticized for increasingly minor things—wearing certain clothes, making facial expressions people don't like, or in this case, grilling food. It raises questions about what we really expect from public figures and whether our expectations have become unreasonable."

At the same time, defenders of the criticism argue that celebrities do have social influence and that their public behavior—especially behavior they choose to broadcast to millions of followers—can normalize inconsiderate actions. From this perspective, even "minor" controversies serve the purpose of maintaining social standards.

Mijoo's Response

Following the backlash, Mijoo deleted the original Instagram stories showing the grilling session. She has not made any public statement addressing the controversy directly, a common strategy for managing minor scandals in the Korean entertainment industry where engagement often prolongs negative attention.

Her agency has similarly remained silent on the matter, apparently choosing not to escalate the situation with an official response. Industry observers note that addressing such controversies with formal statements can sometimes backfire, making the issue seem more serious than it is.

In subsequent social media posts, Mijoo has returned to her usual content without acknowledging the controversy, suggesting she and her team have opted to ride out the criticism rather than engage with it directly. This approach has worked for many celebrities facing minor backlash, allowing the news cycle to move on naturally.

The Apartment Living Debate

Beyond the celebrity angle, the controversy has reignited broader discussions about apartment living etiquette in South Korea. With roughly 60% of the Korean population living in apartments, neighbor disputes are a common source of social tension, and balcony activities are frequently at the center of conflicts.

Common points of contention include smoking on balconies (which sends smoke into neighboring units), drying laundry that drips onto lower floors, excessive noise, and yes, grilling. Online communities regularly feature posts from frustrated apartment dwellers complaining about inconsiderate neighbors.

"This isn't really about Mijoo specifically," argued one commenter. "It's about a broader social issue that many people deal with. She just became a symbol of the frustration people feel when neighbors are inconsiderate. Whether that's fair to her individually is another question."

Impact On Mijoo's Career

Entertainment industry insiders suggest that this controversy is unlikely to have any lasting impact on Mijoo's career. Unlike serious scandals involving illegal activity or major ethical violations, balcony grilling criticism falls into the category of temporary social media storms that fade quickly.

Mijoo's established reputation as a likeable, down-to-earth entertainer provides significant cushion against this type of minor backlash. Her regular appearances on popular variety shows and her substantial, loyal fanbase mean that one questionable Instagram post is unlikely to define her public image.

"Mijoo has built up a lot of goodwill with the public," noted entertainment reporter Yoon Ji Hye. "People generally like her and want to like her. That social capital protects celebrities from minor controversies. If this had happened to someone with a less positive public image, it might have blown up bigger."

Lessons And Moving Forward

If there's a lesson in this incident for both celebrities and the general public, it may be about the double-edged nature of social media sharing. What seems like relatable, everyday content can quickly become controversial when viewed through critical eyes and amplified by viral sharing.

For celebrities, the incident serves as a reminder that even mundane activities can generate unexpected backlash when broadcast to large audiences. For the public, it raises questions about the proportionality of online criticism and whether every questionable behavior warrants a pile-on.

As for Mijoo, she continues her busy schedule of variety show appearances and endorsements, apparently undeterred by the temporary controversy. Whether she'll think twice before grilling on her balcony in the future—or at least before posting about it—remains to be seen.
