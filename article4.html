ILLIT Under Fire: Double Plagiarism Accusations From BLACKPINK and NewJeans Fans

March 17, 2026 By Michael Finley 📖 8 min read

HYBE's rising girl group ILLIT finds themselves caught in an unprecedented storm of criticism as fans of both BLACKPINK and NewJeans simultaneously accuse the rookie group of plagiarizing their favorite artists, creating a multi-front controversy that threatens to derail the group's promising career and exposes deeper tensions within the K-Pop industry's creative landscape.

The controversy erupted earlier this week when ILLIT released promotional materials and performance videos for their latest comeback, "Eclipse." Within hours, eagle-eyed fans from two of K-Pop's most passionate fandoms began compiling what they claim is evidence of creative theft, setting social media ablaze with detailed comparison posts and accusatory hashtags.

The dual nature of the accusations—coming simultaneously from both YG Entertainment's BLINK fandom and HYBE's own NewJeans supporters (Bunnies)—has created a uniquely volatile situation. The hashtags #ILLITCopiedBLACKPINK and #ILLITCopiedNewJeans both trended worldwide, often appearing side by side on Twitter's trending list.

The BLACKPINK Accusations: Style, Concept, and Aesthetics

BLINKs have assembled extensive comparison documents alleging that ILLIT's recent concept bears striking similarities to BLACKPINK's signature aesthetic. The accusations span multiple elements including fashion styling, music video cinematography, and overall visual presentation.

Specifically, fans have highlighted the "Eclipse" music video's use of dark, high-fashion imagery contrasted with moments of vibrant color—a visual language that BLINKs argue was pioneered by BLACKPINK's creative director and has become synonymous with the YG group. Comparison posts showing ILLIT members in poses nearly identical to iconic BLACKPINK photoshoots have garnered millions of views.

"The styling is too similar to be coincidental. The leather harnesses, the specific shade of red they're using, the way they've staged the dance break—these are BLACKPINK signatures that have been developed over years. ILLIT debuted yesterday and they're already copying concepts that took BLACKPINK years to establish." — @BLINKArchives, fan account with 1.8M followers

The accusations have extended beyond visuals to include claims about ILLIT's musical direction. Some BLINKs argue that the bass-heavy production style and the structure of certain verses mirror BLACKPINK's approach too closely to be attributed to general industry trends.

The NewJeans Accusations: Choreography and Creative Direction

Perhaps even more damaging to ILLIT's reputation are the accusations coming from within HYBE's own family of fandoms. NewJeans supporters have compiled frame-by-frame video comparisons showing what they claim are directly copied dance moves and formations.

The allegations center on ILLIT's choreography for "Eclipse," with Bunnies identifying at least twelve specific sequences they say replicate NewJeans' signature moves. Particularly contentious is a hand gesture sequence that appears nearly identical to choreography from NewJeans' hit "Super Shy."

Video compilations showing the parallel movements have accumulated over 25 million views across platforms. Several professional choreographers have weighed in on the controversy, with opinions divided on whether the similarities constitute plagiarism or simply reflect common contemporary dance vocabulary.

"As someone who has studied both groups' choreographies extensively, I can say that several of these moves are distinctive enough to NewJeans that seeing them performed by ILLIT without any acknowledgment feels wrong. Choreography may not be copyrightable in the legal sense, but there are ethical standards in the dance community." — Professional choreographer commenting on TheQoo

HYBE's Complicated Position

The situation is particularly awkward for HYBE, the entertainment conglomerate that houses both ILLIT (through sublabel Belift Lab) and NewJeans (through ADOR). The company now faces the delicate task of addressing accusations that pit two of its own artist groups against each other.

Industry observers note that this controversy illuminates ongoing tensions within HYBE's multi-label structure. Since acquiring multiple subsidiaries and launching new groups, HYBE has faced periodic criticism about creative overlap and the potential for cannibalization among its own artists.

The NewJeans controversy is further complicated by the well-publicized tensions between ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin and HYBE's central management. Some industry watchers speculate that the vociferous defense of NewJeans by Bunnies may be fueled by broader concerns about the group's treatment within the HYBE ecosystem.

The Young Members Caught in the Crossfire

Lost in much of the online discourse is the fact that ILLIT's members are remarkably young, with the average age of the group being just 18. The five members—Yunah, Minju, Moka, Wonhee, and Iroha—have found their social media accounts flooded with hostile comments despite having likely had little input into the creative decisions being criticized.

Mental health advocates have expressed concern about the intensity of the backlash directed at the young performers. "These girls probably showed up to record a song and dance that was created by adult professionals," noted one commentator. "They're now bearing the brunt of hatred for decisions that were almost certainly made above their level."

ILLIT's Yunah, the group's leader at just 19 years old, reportedly broke down in tears during a recent fan event when asked about the controversy, though the moment was quickly cut from official streams.

Industry Context: K-Pop's Plagiarism Problem

The ILLIT controversy is hardly the first time plagiarism accusations have rocked the K-Pop industry. The highly derivative nature of pop music production, combined with the rapid pace of comebacks and the global reach of fandoms, creates fertile ground for such disputes.

Industry experts point out that concepts, styling, and even choreography often follow trends that make distinguishing inspiration from imitation difficult. "K-Pop operates within a relatively narrow aesthetic bandwidth," explained music industry analyst Choi Sung-ho. "Multiple groups are trying to appeal to similar demographics with similar concepts. Overlap is inevitable."

However, the same experts acknowledge that the ILLIT case may represent something beyond typical trend-following. "When you have two major fandoms independently reaching similar conclusions, that suggests the similarities may be more substantive than coincidental," Choi added.

Official Responses—Or Lack Thereof

As of publication, neither Belift Lab nor HYBE has issued an official statement addressing the plagiarism allegations. This silence has frustrated fans on all sides, who have demanded transparency about the creative process behind ILLIT's recent work.

YG Entertainment, BLACKPINK's agency, has also declined to comment publicly, though sources suggest the company is "monitoring the situation." The lack of official engagement has allowed speculation to flourish unchecked, with increasingly extreme claims gaining traction online.

Music industry attorneys suggest that legal action is unlikely regardless of the accusations' validity. "Plagiarism in music styling and choreography is notoriously difficult to prosecute," explained entertainment lawyer Kim Tae-ho. "Unless there's direct copying of copyrighted music or clearly protected intellectual property, these cases rarely make it to court."

Fan Behavior Under Scrutiny

The controversy has also sparked meta-discussions about fan behavior in the K-Pop space. While many participants in the discourse have maintained respectful criticism, others have crossed into harassment, sending threatening messages to ILLIT members and their families.

Some voices within both the BLINK and Bunny fandoms have called for measured responses, arguing that holding creative decision-makers accountable should not involve targeting the young performers. However, these moderating voices have struggled to be heard amid the outrage.

What Happens Next

The coming days will be critical for ILLIT's future trajectory. Industry experts suggest several possible outcomes: HYBE could issue a statement defending the group's creative originality; the company could acknowledge the similarities and announce changes to future creative direction; or management may simply wait for the controversy to burn itself out.

For ILLIT themselves, the immediate future likely involves continuing their scheduled promotions while attempting to weather the storm. Their upcoming music show appearances will be closely watched for signs of how the controversy is affecting the young performers.

The broader implications for HYBE's multi-label strategy remain to be seen. If the company cannot effectively manage creative differentiation among its numerous girl groups, similar controversies may become recurring events.
