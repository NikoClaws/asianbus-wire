ILLIT Under Fire: Double Plagiarism Accusations From BLACKPINK and NewJeans Fans

March 17, 2026 By AsianBus Wire Staff

HYBE's rookie girl group ILLIT has found themselves in hot water after fans accused them of plagiarizing both BLACKPINK's album concept and NewJeans' choreography for their latest comeback.

The accusations come at a particularly sensitive time, as ILLIT has already faced comparisons to NewJeans since their debut — comparisons that have often sparked heated debates between fandoms.

Album Teaser Controversy

The first wave of criticism emerged when ILLIT released teasers for their new album. Sharp-eyed BLINKs (BLACKPINK's fandom) immediately pointed out similarities between the visual concept and previous BLACKPINK releases.

"The color palette, the styling, even the poses — it's too similar to be coincidental," argued one fan on social media, posting side-by-side comparisons that quickly went viral.

Choreography Similarities

Adding to the group's troubles, NewJeans fans (Bunnies) have accused ILLIT of copying choreography elements from NewJeans' catalog. Specific dance moves have been highlighted and dissected across fan communities, with many demanding an explanation from HYBE.

The choreography accusations are particularly damaging given the ongoing tensions between HYBE's various sub-labels and the very public disputes that have played out over the past year involving NewJeans and their management.

Industry Experts Weigh In

K-Pop industry analysts have noted that plagiarism accusations are increasingly common in the highly competitive idol landscape. "With so many groups debuting and so much content being produced, some overlap is inevitable," said one industry insider. "But the question is whether these similarities cross the line from inspiration to imitation."

HYBE's Response

As of publication, HYBE has not issued an official statement regarding the accusations. The agency has historically been cautious in responding to fan-driven controversies, often choosing to let the situation cool down rather than adding fuel to the fire.

ILLIT's fans (GLLIT) have rallied to defend the group, arguing that the accusations are overblown and driven by inter-fandom rivalry rather than legitimate concerns. The debate continues to rage across social media platforms.
