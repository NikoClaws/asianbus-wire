'KPop Demon Hunters' Directors Return To Seoul After Historic Oscar Sweep — First K-Pop Song To Win Academy Award

By Ji-won Lee April 2, 2026

Directors Maggie Kang and Chris Appelhans touched down in Seoul on April 1 for a press conference celebrating the extraordinary awards run of "KPop Demon Hunters" — a Netflix action-fantasy animation about K-pop superstars who secretly protect the world from supernatural threats.

The film swept the 98th Academy Awards with wins for both Best Animated Feature and Best Original Song ("Golden"), adding to an already historic trophy cabinet that includes the same categories at the 83rd Golden Globe Awards and the 31st Critics' Choice Awards. "Golden," performed by the fictional idol group Huntrix, became the first K-pop song to ever win an Oscar — and the first to win a Grammy Award after taking home Best Song Written for Visual Media at the 68th Grammys.

A Cultural Breakthrough

The film's impact extends far beyond the awards circuit. "KPop Demon Hunters" has surpassed 500 million global views on Netflix, becoming one of the platform's most-watched animated features of all time. Time magazine named it the 2025 Breakthrough of the Year, cementing the film as a rare crossover success that merged K-pop culture with mainstream Western animation.

"When we started this project, people told us K-pop and animation was a niche combination," Kang said at the Seoul press conference. "But the audience proved that K-pop's storytelling power — the worldbuilding, the lore, the emotional connection fans have with idol narratives — is a perfect foundation for animation."

EJAE Cashes In

South Korean entertainment company EJAE, which holds key licensing rights to the project, is anticipating significant royalties from the film's continued success. With "Golden" charting worldwide and the Huntrix soundtrack generating streams in the billions, the financial impact is expected to ripple across K-pop's animation ambitions for years to come.

At the 68th Grammy Awards, "Golden" received four nominations — including Song of the Year — and won Best Song Written for Visual Media, making it the first K-pop track to win in any Grammy category. The song's success has sparked conversations about K-pop's expanding footprint in Hollywood scoring.

What's Next

Netflix has not confirmed a sequel, but industry analysts expect an announcement before the end of 2026 given the film's commercial performance. Meanwhile, the "KPop Demon Hunters" franchise has spawned a webtoon adaptation, a sold-out concert tour featuring the Huntrix soundtrack performed live, and merchandise collaborations with major K-pop agencies.

For an industry that spent years fighting for Western recognition, the Oscar sweep represents a full-circle moment: K-pop didn't just show up at the Academy Awards — it dominated.
