BIGBANG Launches 20th Anniversary With Coachella Showcase — Justin Bieber Makes Surprise Appearance

April 13, 2026 By Ji-won Lee 📖 6 min read

BIGBANG returned to the Coachella Valley on Saturday night for the first time since 2017, launching their 20th debut anniversary with a headline set at the Outdoor Theatre that drew a massive crowd and earned some unexpected reinforcement on stage — global pop star Justin Bieber joining them for an impromptu collaboration.

The K-pop pioneers — G-Dragon, Taeyang, Daesung, and Seungri — took the stage from 10:30 PM to 11:30 PM PST, delivering a high-energy set packed with their signature party anthems. According to the Los Angeles Times, BIGBANG "drew a huge crowd with a set long on sexed-up party jams and exposed abdominal muscles — one more sign of the festival's increasing internationalization in its own 25th year of business."

Justin Bieber's Surprise Appearance

The night's biggest surprise came mid-set when Justin Bieber joined BIGBANG on stage for an impromptu collaboration. The Canadian pop star, who has previously worked with BTS and other K-pop artists, performed his hit single "Baby" alongside the K-pop group, creating a historic crossover moment that had both audiences roaring. Fan-recorded clips of the collaboration immediately went viral across social media platforms, with hashtags like #CoachellaBIGBANG and #JustinXBIGBANG trending within minutes.

The appearance was completely unannounced — no hint had been dropped about Bieber joining BIGBANG's set, making the cameo a genuine surprise for the packed Outdoor Theatre crowd. For many in attendance, it was a once-in-a-lifetime moment to see two of pop's biggest acts share a stage.

20 Years In The Making

BIGBANG debuted under YG Entertainment in 2006 and went on to become one of the most influential acts in modern pop music. Their Coachella return marks their first major international festival appearance as a group since 2017, when they performed at the Empire Polo Club in Indio, California. That 2017 show itself was historic, making BIGBANG one of the first K-pop groups to play a major US festival at that scale.

The 2026 return reinforces their continued global relevance and sets the stage for what YG Entertainment has described as a major anniversary year. The group performed a career-spanning setlist that included fan favorites across multiple eras, from early hits like "Haru Haru" to more recent releases, demonstrating the breadth of their catalog and the loyalty of their fandom.

This performance was also notable for being the group's first time back at Coachella without former member T.O.P, who recently released his debut solo album Another Dimension. Despite T.O.P's absence, the four remaining members delivered a show that many fans called one of BIGBANG's most electrifying performances in years.

What Comes Next

BIGBANG is scheduled to return for Coachella's second weekend on April 19, closing out the festival's Outdoor Theatre once again. With Weekend 1 setting such a high bar — both musically and in terms of surprise moments — all eyes will be on what BIGBANG has in store for fans attending the second show.

The Coachella appearance is just the beginning of what promises to be a significant anniversary year for BIGBANG. YG Entertainment has hinted at additional celebrations and releases throughout 2026 to mark two decades since the group's debut, though specifics have yet to be announced.
