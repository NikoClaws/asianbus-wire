BTS Makes Historic Comeback: 260,000 Fans Flood Seoul For ARIRANG Concert

March 21, 2026 10 min read By Andrew Lewter

They're finally back. On Saturday, March 21, 2026, all seven members of BTS took to the stage together for the first time since October 2022, delivering an emotionally-charged performance that shut down central Seoul and brought millions of fans worldwide to tears.

The "BTS The Comeback Live: ARIRANG" concert at Gwanghwamun Square drew an estimated 260,000 people to Seoul's historic district, according to HYBE Corporation. With 104,000 inside the venue and over 150,000 watching from surrounding areas, authorities called it the largest public concert in South Korean history.

The Wait Is Over

For ARMY, the global BTS fanbase, this moment was nearly four years in the making. The group went on hiatus in June 2022, with members subsequently completing South Korea's mandatory military service. Jin enlisted first in December 2022, followed by J-Hope in April 2023. RM, V, Jimin, and Jungkook enlisted together in December 2023, while Suga completed his alternative military service in June 2025.

"The past few years have been long," RM told the crowd, his voice cracking with emotion. "But knowing you were waiting made everything possible. We're home."

The one-hour set featured songs from their new album "ARIRANG," which dropped on Friday to massive commercial success, selling 3.98 million copies on day one alone. The album's title track borrows from Korea's most beloved folk song—a centuries-old anthem about moving through hardship toward something better.

A Nation Stops For BTS

The production was nothing short of spectacular. Seoul deployed 7,000 police officers, including SWAT units with anti-drone systems. Three subway stations were closed, dozens of buildings restricted access, and metal detectors were installed at 31 entry points.

The iconic Gwanghwamun Gate, a 14th-century gateway to the royal palace, provided a breathtaking backdrop. Purple lights—BTS's signature color—illuminated the historic structure as RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook delivered performances that reminded the world why they became the biggest band of their generation.

"BTS is the reason I'm here," said Ami Ostrovskaia, a 23-year-old Russian student who moved to Seoul specifically because of her love for the group. "It's why I started learning about Korean history, culture, food, and the language."

Global Impact

Netflix live-streamed the concert worldwide as part of an exclusive deal that includes a documentary on the band's reunion. The streaming giant has invested billions into Korean entertainment, and Saturday's event represented a major marketing triumph.

The concert is just the beginning. BTS announced an 82-date world tour that will take them to 34 cities across the globe, including stops in Singapore, Tokyo, Munich, and Los Angeles. Industry analysts estimate the tour could generate over $1 billion in revenue.

"They've always exceeded my expectations," said Park Joo-young, a longtime fan in her 20s. "They might feel the pressure, but I believe they'll do their best."

What's Next For BTS

The world tour kicks off next month, and the stakes couldn't be higher. BTS returns to a K-pop industry that has grown more competitive during their absence. New generation groups like ENHYPEN, NewJeans, and Stray Kids have claimed significant market share.

But if Saturday's concert proved anything, it's that the BTS phenomenon transcends trends. As V declared from the stage: "This isn't a comeback—this is just the beginning of BTS 2.0."

The seven members ended the show with "Arirang," the title track, as the crowd of hundreds of thousands sang along to the folk melody that has represented Korean resilience for generations. It was a perfect metaphor: after years of separation, BTS and ARMY are together again, walking that figurative mountain pass toward whatever comes next.

The "ARIRANG" album is now available on all streaming platforms. BTS's world tour begins April 15, 2026, in Seoul.
