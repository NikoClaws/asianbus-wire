BTS Jungkook And aespa Winter's "Favorite Date Spot" Exposed As Fans Debate Mounting Evidence

March 17, 2026 By Min-seo Lee, Dating & Rumors Correspondent

The rumored relationship between BTS's Jungkook and aespa's Winter has exploded back into public discourse following new claims from Korean netizens who allege they have identified a private restaurant in Seoul where the two idols have been spotted together on multiple occasions.

The latest wave of speculation began when a post on the Korean online community Theqoo claimed to reveal the location of what the poster called "the couple's favorite date spot"—a high-end private dining establishment in the upscale Cheongdam-dong neighborhood known for its celebrity clientele and strict privacy policies.

The post, which has since been viewed over 5 million times, included what the poster claimed were testimonies from restaurant staff and screenshots of reservation systems allegedly showing bookings under names associated with the idols' management companies. AsianBus Wire has not been able to independently verify these claims.

The Evidence: What Fans Are Claiming

Supporters of the dating theory have compiled what they consider to be a substantial body of circumstantial evidence over the past several months. The latest restaurant claims add to a growing list of alleged "proof" that the two fourth-generation icons are in a relationship.

Previous evidence cited by fans includes:

  • Matching jewelry spotted on both idols during separate public appearances
  • Similar Instagram story backgrounds posted within hours of each other
  • A blurry photograph allegedly showing two figures resembling the idols entering a building together
  • Overlapping schedules that fans claim would have allowed for private meetings
  • Alleged "couple items" including matching phone cases and clothing

The restaurant revelation has added fuel to these existing claims. According to the Theqoo post, the establishment—a Japanese omakase restaurant with only six seats and a two-month waiting list for regular customers—has allegedly hosted the pair on at least four separate occasions over the past three months.

"My friend works there and they've seen them together multiple times. They always book the entire restaurant and arrive separately, but they leave together in the same car. Everyone who works there knows but they're all bound by NDAs." — Anonymous poster on Theqoo (translated)

The Skeptics Fight Back

Not everyone is convinced by the mounting claims. A significant portion of both fandoms—ARMY for BTS and MY for aespa—have pushed back against what they characterize as baseless speculation built on coincidences and fabricated evidence.

"Every single piece of 'evidence' can be easily explained," wrote one ARMY on Twitter. "Idols wear similar jewelry because they're styled by the same brands. They post similar backgrounds because they're both in Seoul. This is a witch hunt based on nothing."

Skeptics have also pointed out inconsistencies in the various claims. The blurry photograph that allegedly shows the couple has been analyzed by fans who argue the figures could be anyone, while the "matching items" have been identified as popular products owned by thousands of people.

The restaurant claims have faced particular scrutiny. Critics note that the original poster provided no photographic evidence of the alleged sightings and that the screenshots of reservation systems could easily be fabricated. The restaurant itself has not responded to inquiries.

Agency Silence Fuels Speculation

Both HYBE (which manages BTS through its subsidiary Big Hit Music) and SM Entertainment (which manages aespa) have maintained complete silence on the dating rumors despite repeated media inquiries. This silence has been interpreted differently by different camps.

Those who believe the rumors argue that the agencies' refusal to deny the relationship is tacit confirmation. In the K-Pop industry, agencies typically issue swift denials when false dating rumors emerge, particularly for artists of Jungkook and Winter's caliber.

"If it wasn't true, they would have denied it by now," argued one fan. "The fact that both companies are staying quiet speaks volumes."

However, others point out that agencies sometimes choose not to dignify rumors with responses, particularly when they view the claims as too absurd or poorly substantiated to warrant official comment.

The Impact on Both Artists

Regardless of whether the rumors are true, the persistent speculation has had tangible effects on both artists' public perception and fan dynamics.

For Jungkook, who completed his mandatory military service last year and is preparing for BTS's highly anticipated group activities, the rumors have created division among ARMYs. Some fans have expressed support for his personal life choices, while others have reacted negatively to the possibility of a relationship.

Winter, as one of aespa's most popular members, has faced similar scrutiny. The 24-year-old vocalist has seen increased attention on her personal life, with some fans expressing concern about the impact of the rumors on her mental health and career.

"These idols are human beings who deserve privacy," wrote a fan account that has been advocating against the rumor-spreading. "Whether they're dating or not is none of our business. The obsession with their personal lives is unhealthy for everyone involved."

K-Pop's Dating Culture Problem

The Jungkook-Winter saga highlights the broader issues surrounding dating in the K-Pop industry. Despite gradual shifts in attitudes, many idol contracts still include dating restrictions, and public relationships remain relatively rare among top-tier artists.

When relationships are confirmed—whether voluntarily or through media exposure—the consequences can range from supportive fan reactions to career-damaging backlash. This creates an environment where idols often feel compelled to hide their personal lives entirely.

"The industry has created this monster," explained pop culture critic Park Sung-ho. "By selling the fantasy of accessibility and availability, companies have fostered fan cultures that feel entitled to control idols' personal lives. Until that fundamental dynamic changes, we'll keep seeing these invasive investigations into private matters."

What Happens Next

For now, the rumors show no signs of abating. New "evidence" continues to emerge regularly on Korean and international fan forums, while dedicated accounts track the idols' movements and public appearances for any signs of connection.

Both Jungkook and Winter have upcoming schedules that will bring them back into the public eye. BTS is preparing for group activities following the completion of all members' military service, while aespa continues to promote their latest album. Whether the increased public exposure will lead to more speculation—or finally put the rumors to rest—remains to be seen.

Until either agency issues an official statement or the idols themselves choose to address the speculation, the debate will likely continue in the passionate, polarized fashion that has become characteristic of K-Pop fan culture.

AsianBus Wire has reached out to both HYBE and SM Entertainment for comment. This story will be updated if responses are received.
