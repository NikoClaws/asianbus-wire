BTS Concert Faces Bomb Threats As Police Announce Unprecedented Security Measures

March 17, 2026 By AsianBus Wire Staff

BTS's highly anticipated comeback concert in Seoul is facing unprecedented security challenges after police received multiple bomb threats, leading to demands from some quarters for the event's cancellation.

Korean National Police announced today that metal detectors will be installed at all 30 entrances to the venue, with additional security personnel deployed throughout the concert grounds. The decision comes after a series of anonymous threats were received over the past week.

Enhanced Security Protocols

In a press conference, police officials detailed the extensive security measures being implemented. "We are taking every precaution to ensure the safety of all attendees," stated a Seoul Metropolitan Police spokesperson. "This includes K-9 units, increased surveillance, and coordination with international security agencies."

The security measures have sparked their own controversy, with some fans expressing frustration at the additional time required for entry. Concert organizers have advised ARMYs to arrive at least three hours before the scheduled start time.

Cancellation Demands

Despite assurances from both police and Big Hit Music, some civic groups have called for the concert to be postponed. "The safety of tens of thousands of fans should not be gambled with," argued one petition circulating online.

However, the majority of ARMYs have rallied behind BTS, expressing their determination to attend regardless of the threats. "We've waited years for this comeback," wrote one fan. "No anonymous coward will stop us from supporting our boys."

BTS's Response

While BTS members have not directly addressed the threats, the group's official social media accounts have continued promoting the concert and thanking fans for their support. Big Hit Music released a statement expressing confidence in the security arrangements.

"The safety of ARMYs is our top priority," the statement read. "We are working closely with authorities to ensure a safe and memorable experience for all attendees."

The concert, marking BTS's first full-group performance since completing their military service obligations, remains scheduled as planned.
