BTS Arirang Tour Kicks Off April 9 — 82 Dates Across 34 Cities Could Eclipse Taylor Swift's Eras Tour

April 2, 2026 6 min read By Daniel Yoo

SEOUL — In exactly one week, BTS will step onto the stage at Goyang Sports Complex for the opening night of what could become the highest-grossing concert tour in history. The Arirang World Tour, spanning 82 dates across 34 cities in 23 countries through 2027, is being positioned by industry analysts as the tour most likely to surpass Taylor Swift's Eras Tour, which grossed over $2 billion.

The opening dates — April 9, 11, and 12 in Goyang — are expected to draw a combined audience of roughly 129,000 fans. But that's just the beginning of a touring schedule that reads like a geography textbook: Japan, Mexico, the United States, Spain, France, the UK, Belgium, Canada, Colombia, Peru, Chile, Argentina, Brazil, Taiwan, Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Hong Kong, Indonesia, the Philippines, and Australia are all on the itinerary.

Years of pent-up demand

The math behind the "bigger than Swift" projections is straightforward: BTS hasn't toured since 2022, when the group began their military service hiatus. Jin was the first to enlist in late 2022, and Suga, the last to be discharged, walked free in June 2025. That's roughly three years of global demand building up with no outlet — and an ARMY fanbase that, if anything, only grew during the silence.

Their March 20 comeback album ARIRANG debuted at No. 1 on both the Billboard 200 and the Hot 100 (with title track "SWIM"), making BTS the first group in history to simultaneously debut an album and single at the top of both charts on multiple occasions. All 13 vocal tracks from ARIRANG entered the Billboard charts.

The Gwanghwamun preview

Fans got their first taste of the comeback show at the free Gwanghwamun Square concert in Seoul, which drew over 100,000 in-person attendees and 18.4 million Netflix live-stream viewers for the hour-long performance. But the Arirang tour shows will be a different beast entirely — three-hour productions with a 360-degree stage setup designed for maximum immersion.

Netflix followed up the concert with BTS: The Return, a 90-minute documentary tracking the group's reunion and the recording of ARIRANG in Los Angeles. The film gave viewers rare studio footage, though fans were understandably more captivated by the candid moments of all seven members simply being together again.

Australia dates confirmed for 2027

For ARMY in the Southern Hemisphere, the wait extends a bit longer. BTS will play four concerts across Sydney and Melbourne in February 2027 as part of the tour's final leg. While that's nearly a year away, tickets for the Australian dates are expected to be among the most competitive in the entire run.

The $2 billion question

Whether BTS can actually eclipse the Eras Tour's staggering $2 billion gross remains to be seen. Swift's tour ran over 149 shows across nearly two years. BTS has 82 dates confirmed so far, though additional shows have been rumored for markets with particularly high demand. The key variable is venue size — many of the Arirang tour stops are at stadiums with capacities north of 50,000.

What's not in question is the cultural moment. BTS's return from military service has been treated as a national event in South Korea, with the Gwanghwamun concert area set against a 14th-century royal palace gateway. The group's members, now mostly in their early 30s (the youngest, Jungkook, is 28), are stepping back into the spotlight not as the scrappy underdogs who debuted in 2013, but as the most commercially successful act in K-pop history.

Tickets for the opening Seoul dates sold out within minutes. The Arirang era, it seems, is just getting started.
