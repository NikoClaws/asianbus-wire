BTS ARIRANG World Tour Launches With Record-Breaking Seoul Shows — $1 Billion Projected

April 10, 2026 By Julian Choi 📖 6 min read

BTS returned to the stage they once swore would never end — and the world felt it. The K-pop supergroup officially launched their ARIRANG World Tour at Goyang Stadium on April 9, delivering a 2.5-hour concert that generated 2 million mentions and 10 million engagements on X (formerly Twitter) in a single night — making it one of the most-discussed single concerts in music history.

Industry projections now estimate the tour could gross over $1 billion across its global run, making it the highest-grossing tour by any K-pop artist and potentially the highest-grossing tour of all time. The tour is being organized by Live Nation and is projected to be one of the most lucrative touring events of the decade.

Rain, Gratitude, and Seven Members Together Again

Despite pouring rain that soaked the stage and limited the tightly synchronized choreography the group is famous for, all seven members — RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook — performed with an energy that BBC News described as "emotion over perfection." Throughout the show, the members repeatedly expressed gratitude for the chance to perform together again.

"We're still here because we love each other so much," leader RM told an Apple Music Radio programme. "With fans and no reason to quit this precious brand and family, these people are my youth itself." The emotional declaration drew some of the loudest cheers of the evening from the Goyang Stadium crowd.

Concertgoer Park Su-bin told the BBC: "The 360-degree stage was so refreshing and brilliant. It's a shame it rained, but I think it'll still be a special memory." The immersive stage design featured rotating platforms and LED installations that transformed the venue into an immersive concert experience despite the weather conditions.

First Full Group Tour Since 2022

BTS's last full group concert was at Las Vegas's Allegiant Stadium on April 16, 2022 — the final date of the Permission to Dance on Stage tour. What followed was a combination of mandatory military service for all members and a period of solo pursuits that saw each member branch into individual projects, from RM's acclaimed Right Place, Wrong Person to J-Hope's historic Lollapalooza headline set.

The ARIRANG album, released March 20, has now spent three consecutive weeks at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 — making BTS the first K-pop act ever to achieve that feat. The achievement is even more remarkable given the album's deeply traditional Korean identity, featuring arrangements rooted in folk music while reaching global audiences through contemporary pop production.

The tour's massive scale reflects that ambition. With 85 dates planned across five continents and a stage production that reportedly cost over $50 million to mount, ARIRANG is designed to be a once-in-a-generation touring event. The stage itself features a 360-degree catwalk, pyrotechnic arrays, and an LED screen spanning over 20,000 square feet.

Netflix Broadcast Extends The Reach

For fans who couldn't secure tickets, Netflix's BTS THE COMEBACK LIVE | ARIRANG broadcast has already drawn over 18 million global viewers, making it one of the most-watched concert broadcasts in the platform's history. The stream was available in over 190 countries, with subtitles available in 12 languages.

The tour continues with additional Seoul dates on April 11 and 12 before moving to Tokyo Dome on April 17 and 18. North American and European dates will follow, with the full 85-date schedule available on the official tour website. BTS ARIRANG is not just a concert — it's a statement about the enduring power of K-pop to connect audiences across languages, borders, and cultures.
