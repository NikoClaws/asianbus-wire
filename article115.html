BTS ARIRANG World Tour Begins Today: 34 Cities, Record-Breaking K-Pop Tour

April 9, 2026 By Selena Jung 📖 8 min read

The moment millions of ARMY have been waiting for is finally here. BTS kicks off their monumental ARIRANG World Tour tonight in Goyang, South Korea, beginning a 34-city journey that will span 2026 and 2027—a record-setting run that industry analysts are already calling the most ambitious tour in K-pop history.

The three-night stand at the Goyang Stadium (April 9, 11, and 12) marks the official launch of a tour that will take BTS to stadium venues across Asia, North America, Europe, Latin America, and Oceania. According to Live Nation, the tour is projected to become the highest-grossing tour by any K-pop artist in history, with preliminary estimates suggesting total revenues could exceed $800 million.

"This isn't just a concert—it's a statement," said one tour industry analyst who spoke to AsianBus on condition of anonymity. "When you factor in the production scale, the global reach, and the sheer number of shows, BTS is essentially operating their own traveling festival. No one in K-pop has done anything like this."

A Tour Unlike Any Other

The ARIRANG World Tour represents BTS's first full-scale group tour since their military hiatus began in 2022. While members have maintained solo projects and international appearances during that period, tonight's concert marks the first time all available members will perform together on stage since their "Permission to Dance" concerts in Las Vegas.

Jungkook, the group's youngest member, completed his military service in March, meaning all seven BTS members will be present for the tour's opening night—a fact that has fans around the world counting down the hours. The group has not performed as a full seven-member unit since November 2022.

The tour takes its name from BTS's latest album "ARIRANG," which translates roughly to a Korean folk song symbolizing the enduring Korean spirit. The album, released March 20, debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and has maintained that position for two consecutive weeks—a historic achievement that makes BTS the first K-pop act to achieve back-to-back chart-topping weeks.

The 34-City Itinerary

According to information released by Big Hit Music, the tour will cover the following regions over its 18-month run:

Asia (April–June 2026): Goyang, Tokyo, Osaka, Manila, Bangkok, Singapore, Jakarta, Kuala Lumpur, Taipei, Hong Kong, Shanghai, Beijing, Seoul (additional dates)

North America (July–September 2026): Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle, Denver, Dallas, Chicago, New York, Boston, Atlanta, Miami, Mexico City, Toronto, Vancouver

Europe (October–December 2026): London, Paris, Amsterdam, Berlin, Madrid, Milan, Stockholm, Warsaw, Prague, Vienna

Latin America (2027): São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Santiago, Lima, Bogotá, Mexico City (additional)

Oceania (2027): Sydney, Melbourne, Auckland, Brisbane

The tour's scale has required unprecedented coordination from Live Nation and local promoters in each market. Sources tell AsianBus that some stadium venues have been booked up to 18 months in advance, with production trucks moving between cities in carefully orchestrated logistics.

Tickets and Demand

Ticket sales for the initial North American dates broke records when they went live in March. The Los Angeles shows at SoFi Stadium sold out in under 10 minutes, prompting additional dates to be added in multiple cities. The New York area shows at MetLife Stadium sold out so quickly that Ticketmaster's Verified Fan queue crashed for approximately 30 minutes during the presale.

"We've never seen demand like this for a K-pop tour," said one ticket broker who spoke to AsianBus. "Secondary market prices for floor seats are already running 400 to 600 percent above face value. BTS could sell out any venue they announce, anywhere in the world, at this point."

Fans who couldn't secure tickets through official channels have organized viewing parties in their respective cities, with over 200 official ARMY viewing events confirmed across 40 countries for tonight's opening show. Big Hit Music has not confirmed whether the Goyang concerts will be streamed, but industry sources suggest a Netflix broadcast deal is "in advanced negotiations."

The Fan Experience

BTS concerts are known for their elaborate production, and the ARIRANG tour appears to be setting new standards. Multiple fan accounts who attended tonight's soundcheck reported seeing "massive LED screens," "pyrotechnics unlike anything from previous tours," and "a stage design that transforms throughout the show."

"The soundcheck was already better than most artists' actual concerts," wrote one fan on X (formerly Twitter). "I can't imagine what the actual show is going to be like."

Security at the venue has been heightened following concerns about crowd management at previous K-pop events. Goyang city officials announced special transportation arrangements, including extended subway service and additional shuttle buses, to accommodate the expected tens of thousands of fans converging on the stadium district.

What This Means for K-Pop

The ARIRANG tour's success or failure will set precedents for the entire K-pop touring industry, according to industry analysts. If BTS achieves its projected numbers, it could trigger a wave of expanded tour productions from other major acts.

"BTS is essentially stress-testing the global touring infrastructure for K-pop," one analyst noted. "If they can successfully execute 34 cities across multiple continents over 18 months, it proves that K-pop can sustain arena-level touring at a scale we haven't seen before."

The tour also comes at a pivotal moment for the K-pop industry's post-pandemic expansion. While many groups have returned to touring, industry observers will be watching to see whether BTS's numbers represent a peak or a new baseline for the genre's touring potential.

Tonight's Setlist

Based on fan reports from soundcheck and information from multiple sources, tonight's setlist appears to heavily feature tracks from the ARIRANG album alongside BTS's greatest hits. Reports indicate the show opens with "Body to Body," the album's high-energy opener, followed by a mix of new tracks and classics including "Dynamite," "Butter," and "Permission to Dance."

The show is expected to run approximately three hours, with a brief intermission before the encore. Sources suggest the final segment will include "Arirang (Reimagined)" and "Into the Sun" as the closing numbers.

For those unable to attend in person, fan live-tweets and updates will be available throughout the evening using the hashtag #ARIRANGTour on all major social platforms.
