K-Drama Fans Boycott "Misogynistic" Lead Actor Yoo Yeon Seok In New Drama "Phantom Lawyer"

March 17, 2026 By Chloe Nam-Davis 📖 7 min read

Actor Yoo Yeon Seok is facing an unprecedented fan boycott ahead of the premiere of his highly anticipated new drama "Phantom Lawyer," as resurfaced comments from past interviews have sparked accusations of misogyny. The controversy has ignited a fierce debate about accountability in the Korean entertainment industry and whether past statements should continue to haunt celebrities years later.

The boycott movement gained momentum over the past week when Korean netizens began circulating compilations of Yoo Yeon Seok's past interview clips and variety show appearances. In these clips, the actor made several comments that critics describe as dismissive toward women, including remarks about his "ideal type" that many found objectifying and statements about gender roles that struck viewers as outdated.

One particularly controversial clip shows Yoo Yeon Seok during a 2018 interview discussing his expectations for a romantic partner. "I think women should know how to take care of their appearance," he said at the time. "A woman who lets herself go after marriage is being disrespectful to her husband." The comment has been viewed millions of times in the past week, with many expressing outrage at what they perceive as blatant sexism.

The Boycott Movement Takes Shape

What began as scattered criticism on online communities has evolved into an organized boycott movement with significant reach. The hashtag #BoycottPhantomLawyer has trended on Korean social media platforms including Naver, Pann, and X (formerly Twitter), with thousands of users pledging not to watch the new drama.

Korean feminist communities have been particularly vocal in their criticism, creating detailed documents cataloging what they describe as a pattern of problematic behavior and statements from the actor over the years. These communities have also reached out to the drama's sponsors, urging them to reconsider their association with the production.

"This isn't about canceling someone over a single mistake. This is about a pattern of behavior that shows a fundamental lack of respect for women. We deserve better from our leading men, both on and off screen." — Statement from Korean Women's Media Watch

The boycott has had measurable effects. Several prominent K-drama fan accounts with hundreds of thousands of followers have announced they will not be covering "Phantom Lawyer," depriving the drama of valuable grassroots promotion. International fan communities, which often drive significant viewership for Korean dramas, have similarly declared their intention to skip the series.

Defenders Rally Behind The Actor

However, Yoo Yeon Seok is not without his defenders. A counter-movement has emerged arguing that the actor is being unfairly judged for comments made years ago and that people should be allowed to grow and change. Supporters have pointed to more recent interviews where Yoo Yeon Seok expressed progressive views on gender equality as evidence of his evolution.

"People can change," wrote one fan on an online community. "Those clips are from 6-8 years ago. Are we really going to condemn someone forever for things they said in a different era? He's never been accused of actually mistreating anyone—these are just words from old interviews."

Some industry observers have also questioned the timing of the controversy, noting that it conveniently emerged just as "Phantom Lawyer" was beginning its promotional campaign. Speculation about whether competing productions or anti-fans might be behind the resurfacing of old clips has circulated, though no evidence of coordinated attacks has been presented.

Fellow actors and industry colleagues have notably remained silent on the controversy, a common response in the Korean entertainment industry where speaking out on controversial topics can be professionally risky.

The Drama Production's Response

SBS, the network broadcasting "Phantom Lawyer," released a carefully worded statement acknowledging the controversy while stopping short of addressing the specific allegations. "We believe in the artistic vision of this production and the talented cast and crew who have worked hard to bring this story to life," the statement read. "We hope viewers will give the drama a fair chance."

The drama's production company has reportedly been in crisis management mode, with meetings held to discuss how to handle the backlash without alienating either side of the debate. Sources suggest that Yoo Yeon Seok's agency is preparing a statement, though the content and timing remain unclear.

"Phantom Lawyer" tells the story of a brilliant but morally ambiguous attorney who takes on cases involving the supernatural. The drama features a star-studded cast and has been heavily promoted as one of the most anticipated releases of the spring season. The controversy threatens to overshadow what was expected to be a triumphant return to television for Yoo Yeon Seok.

A Broader Conversation About Accountability

The controversy has sparked wider discussions about how Korean society handles allegations of past problematic behavior by public figures. Unlike some Western entertainment industries, Korea has historically been less forgiving of celebrity scandals, with careers often ending over controversies that might be survivable elsewhere.

However, the nature of this particular controversy—involving opinions expressed years ago rather than concrete actions—has created a more nuanced debate. Some argue that holding people accountable for past views is essential for societal progress, while others worry about creating an environment where no one can ever be forgiven for past mistakes.

Gender dynamics in Korean entertainment have been under increased scrutiny in recent years, particularly following the #MeToo movement's impact on the industry. Several high-profile actors and directors have seen their careers end following allegations of misconduct, creating heightened sensitivity around issues of misogyny and sexism.

Potential Impact On Ratings And Career

Industry analysts are divided on how significantly the boycott will impact "Phantom Lawyer's" performance. While organized fan boycotts have successfully damaged productions in the past, they have also sometimes backfired, generating controversy-driven viewership from curious audiences.

"It's impossible to predict how this will play out," said entertainment industry analyst Kim Hyun Woo. "The boycott is significant, but so is the star power attached to this drama. Many viewers may simply not care about controversies involving old interview clips and watch anyway."

For Yoo Yeon Seok personally, the stakes are high. The 42-year-old actor has enjoyed a successful career spanning film and television, with memorable roles in "Reply 1994," "Mr. Sunshine," and "Hospital Playlist." A significant ratings failure could damage his standing in an industry where success is often measured by numbers.

What Happens Next

As "Phantom Lawyer" prepares for its premiere, all eyes will be on the initial ratings and public response. The first few episodes will likely determine whether the boycott has teeth or whether the drama can overcome the controversy through strong storytelling and performances.

Yoo Yeon Seok's response—or lack thereof—will also be closely watched. A sincere apology acknowledging past insensitivity could potentially defuse some of the anger, though it risks also validating criticisms that his defenders argue are overblown. Remaining silent, conversely, may be seen as dismissive of legitimate concerns.

Whatever the outcome, the controversy surrounding "Phantom Lawyer" represents another chapter in the ongoing negotiation between Korean entertainment and evolving social values—a negotiation that shows no signs of reaching a conclusion anytime soon.
