500 Wristbands Stolen At BTS Concert Venue In Goyang — Police On The Hunt

By Min-ji Park April 12, 2026

GOYANG, GYEONGGI — About 500 admission wristbands were stolen from a ticket booth at BTS's concert venue in Goyang, Gyeonggi Province on Saturday, police confirmed, prompting the group's agency to warn fans against purchasing unofficial tickets.

A report was received at around 3:20 PM KST that a man had fled with the wristbands from a ticket booth at Goyang Stadium, where BTS's Arirang World Tour is currently underway, according to the Ilsan West Police Station.

Police said they are actively tracking the suspect following the theft. The wristbands, which are required for entry to the concert venue, are typically distributed at official ticket booths to verified ticket holders.

Fans Warned Against Unofficial Tickets

BTS's agency, Big Hit Music, issued a warning to fans following the incident: "Please be cautious of tickets or wristbands obtained through unofficial channels. Entry will only be permitted with wristbands distributed through official booths."

The theft has raised concerns about the potential for unauthorized entry and the safety of concertgoers at one of the world's most anticipated touring events. Security at the venue has reportedly been heightened following the incident.

BTS Tour Continues Despite Incident

The theft occurred during the second night of BTS's three-night stand in Goyang. The tour continues on Sunday evening before the group moves to Tokyo Dome on April 17 and 18.

The Arirang World Tour has already sold 2.4 million tickets across 41 North American and European dates, with 46 shows completely sold out according to Live Nation. Notable future stops include four nights each at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles, MetLife Stadium in New Jersey, and Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in London.

This is the second security-related incident involving BTS's tour in recent days. On April 9, the opening night at Goyang Stadium saw 2 million mentions and 10 million engagements on X (formerly Twitter), generating one of the biggest single-concert social media reactions in music history.
