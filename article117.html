500 Wristbands Stolen At BTS Concert Venue In Goyang — Police On The Hunt

April 12, 2026 By Min-ji Park 📖 5 min read

About 500 admission wristbands were stolen from a ticket booth at BTS's concert venue in Goyang, Gyeonggi Province on Saturday, police confirmed, prompting the group's agency to warn fans against purchasing unofficial tickets.

A report was received at around 3:20 PM KST that a man had fled with the wristbands from a ticket booth at Goyang Stadium, where BTS's Arirang World Tour is currently underway, according to the Ilsan West Police Station. Officers responded to the scene within minutes and have since been reviewing security footage to identify the suspect.

Fans Warned Against Unofficial Tickets

BTS's agency, Big Hit Music, issued a warning to fans following the incident: "Please be cautious of tickets or wristbands obtained through unofficial channels. Entry will only be permitted with wristbands distributed through official booths." The statement urged fans who may have already purchased unofficial wristbands to contact local authorities rather than attempting to use them at the venue.

The theft has raised concerns about the potential for unauthorized entry and the safety of concertgoers at one of the world's most anticipated touring events. Security at the venue has reportedly been heightened following the incident, with additional personnel deployed at all entry points for the remaining shows at Goyang Stadium.

Concert wristbands have become a common security measure at major K-pop concerts in South Korea, replacing traditional paper tickets. Each wristband is typically linked to a fan's identity during the purchase process, making unauthorized use easier to detect but also making stolen wristbands potentially valuable on secondary markets.

BTS Tour Continues Despite Incident

The theft occurred during the second night of BTS's three-night stand in Goyang. The tour continues on Sunday evening before the group moves to Tokyo Dome on April 17 and 18. The group performed their second show on April 11 without disruption, with the wristband theft apparently occurring before the venue opened for the evening session.

The Arirang World Tour has already sold 2.4 million tickets across 41 North American and European dates, with 46 shows completely sold out according to Live Nation. Notable future stops include four nights each at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles, MetLife Stadium in New Jersey, and Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in London — all venues where wristband security is expected to be particularly tight following this incident.

This is the second security-related incident involving BTS's tour in recent days. On April 9, the opening night at Goyang Stadium saw 2 million mentions and 10 million engagements on X (formerly Twitter), generating one of the biggest single-concert social media reactions in music history. Despite the theft, attendance for the remaining Goyang dates remains unaffected, with fans encouraged to arrive early to account for increased security screenings.
