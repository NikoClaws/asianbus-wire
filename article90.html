BTS Achieves Rare Billboard Sweep — ARIRANG No. 1 On Billboard 200, 'SWIM' Tops Hot 100

April 1, 2026 · 3 min read

BTS has pulled off a rare Billboard double crown, with their comeback album ARIRANG debuting at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and lead single "SWIM" simultaneously topping the Hot 100 — the biggest chart week of 2026 by any artist.

The album moved a staggering 641,000 equivalent album units in its first week, including roughly 532,000 in pure sales — with 208,000 of those on vinyl alone. It marks the largest first-week figure since Taylor Swift's The Life of a Showgirl last fall, and the biggest sales week for a group since One Direction's Midnight Memories in 2013.

Seventh Hot 100 No. 1

"SWIM" logged 15.3 million streams, 25.8 million radio impressions, and 154,000 combined sales for the tracking week. The achievement makes BTS the fifth act in history with seven or more Hot 100 chart-toppers, joining The Beatles, The Supremes, Bee Gees, and The Rolling Stones.

It's also the group's first Hot 100 No. 1 since 2021's "Butter," though members Jimin and Jung Kook each scored solo No. 1 hits during BTS's hiatus.

ARIRANG Dominates The Charts

The album's reach extends well beyond its title track — 13 of ARIRANG's 14 tracks entered the Hot 100 simultaneously. "Body to Body" charted at No. 25, "Hooligan" at No. 35, "FYA" at No. 36, and "Normal" at No. 41. The only track that missed was "No. 29," a 98-second instrumental featuring the historic Seongdeok Bell.

The comeback comes after a three-year, nine-month hiatus during which all seven members completed mandatory military service and pursued solo projects. Despite the extended absence — or perhaps because of it — demand for the group's return has been extraordinary.

World Tour Starts April 9

BTS will kick off their world tour April 9-12 at Goyang Stadium in Gyeonggi Province, South Korea, with 82 concerts planned across approximately 34 cities worldwide.

Leader RM shared the Billboard news on Instagram, writing simply: "Sending my love and thanks." Through Big Hit Music, the group added: "We hope this song has provided even a small measure of comfort and courage to listeners across borders."

The K-pop landscape shifted significantly during BTS's absence — Stray Kids alone scored seven Billboard 200 No. 1s in that time — but ARIRANG's record-breaking debut proves the group's grip on the global charts hasn't loosened one bit.
