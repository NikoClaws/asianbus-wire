Mark Lee Leaves NCT & SM Entertainment After 10 Years — Contract Ends April 8

Mark Lee Leaves NCT & SM Entertainment After 10 Years — Contract Ends April 8

By Staff Writer • April 2026
6 min read

A Decade Of NCT

Mark debuted with NCT U on April 9, 2016 — meaning his departure comes just one day before what would have been his 10-year anniversary. He went on to become one of the most versatile members in the NCT system, promoting actively across NCT 127, NCT DREAM, and various NCT U configurations.

In April 2025, he launched his solo career with the album "The Firstfruit," which was well-received by fans and critics alike. SM acknowledged his contributions, calling him "a remarkable artist over the last 10 years" who showed "outstanding abilities both as a group member and as a solo artist."

Mark's Heartfelt Letter To Fans

In a handwritten letter posted to Instagram, Mark opened up about his decision with characteristic honesty. "I know this may feel very sudden to everyone," he wrote, "but in fact, ever since my trainee days — or maybe even before that — I've always carried a dream in my heart."

He described childhood dreams of busking with an acoustic guitar and writing in English, explaining that while he loved his time with NCT, ten years of growth led him to ask bigger questions about his purpose. "After spending 10 years seeing and experiencing the world at its fullest and going on the best journey, I think I naturally began to wonder: what is the best dream I can possibly dream?"

The letter painted a picture of an artist who cherishes his past but feels called to something new — not a departure born from conflict, but from personal evolution.

What's Next For NCT

SM confirmed that NCT 127 will continue as a seven-member group with Johnny, Taeyong, Yuta, Doyoung, Jaehyun, Jungwoo, and Haechan. NCT DREAM will move forward with six members: Renjun, Jeno, Haechan, Jaemin, Chenle, and Jisung.

Mark's departure marks a significant shift for both sub-units. As a founding member who participated in nearly every NCT project since debut, his absence will be deeply felt — particularly in NCT DREAM, where he served as the de facto leader and lead rapper.

Fans React

The news has sent shockwaves through the NCT fandom. "Mark IS NCT" began trending on X within minutes of the announcement, along with "Thank You Mark" and emotional tributes to his decade with the group. While many fans expressed sadness, others voiced support for his decision to pursue his own path.

Mark has not yet announced which agency he will sign with or what his next steps will be. Given his songwriting skills, fluency in English and Korean, and massive global fanbase, the K-pop industry is watching closely.

"After a long period of in-depth discussions with Mark regarding the direction of his future activities and following adequate conversations between both parties, we came to a mutual agreement to conclude his exclusive contract as of April 8," the agency said in an official statement.
