Mark Lee Leaves NCT & SM Entertainment After 10 Years — Contract Ends April 8

The Canadian-American rapper departs after a decade, marking the end of an era for NCT

By Soo-yeon Kim • April 8, 2026
7 min read

Mark Lee is leaving NCT and SM Entertainment. The agency confirmed on April 8 that Mark's exclusive contract would terminate that same day, ending a decade-long relationship that saw the Canadian-American rapper become one of NCT's most prominent members.

Mark debuted as part of NCT in 2016 and quickly became a cornerstone of the group's identity, serving as a captain of NCT 127 while also being an integral member of NCT DREAM. His departure marks the end of an era for the rotational idol project that defined a generation of K-pop.

A Decade of Success

During his ten years with SM Entertainment, Mark became one of the best-selling artists in the industry's history. He participated in numerous hit tracks including "Cherry Bomb," "Regular," and "Kick It," helping establish NCT 127 as one of K-pop's premier boy groups.

The announcement arrives just days after Mark's birthday, which fans celebrated extensively online. Many had speculated about his future with the group following various hints he dropped during recent performances.

What's Next for Mark

While neither Mark nor SM Entertainment has confirmed his future plans, industry insiders suggest he may pursue solo projects or acting opportunities. Mark has previously expressed interest in both directions during various interviews.

"Mark has been an incredible asset to our company and the NCT family," SM Entertainment said in their official statement. "We respect his decision and thank him for everything he's contributed over the past decade."

About Mark Lee

Mark Lee is a Canadian-American rapper who debuted with NCT in 2016. He was a member of NCT 127 and NCT DREAM, known for his rapping skills and stage presence.
