BIGBANG Returns To Coachella Stage April 19 — "The 20th Anniversary Has Just Begun"

April 14, 2026 5 min read By Ji-won Lee

SEOUL — BIGBANG is not done with Coachella yet. After their explosive first performance on April 12 drew praise from fans worldwide, the K-pop legends confirmed they will return to the Outdoor Theatre stage on April 19 for the festival's second weekend — and G-Dragon is already promising the best is yet to come.

"The 20th anniversary of Big Bang in 2026 has just begun," G-Dragon told the Coachella crowd during their Weekend 1 set, which ran for 67 minutes and featured a setlist spanning their entire 20-year career.

The April 12 performance drew explosive reactions before the first note even sounded. When the opening bars of "BANG BANG BANG" rang out across the Outdoor Theatre, the crowd erupted — and the energy never let up through 17 songs, including solo stages from each member and a surprise K-trot segment from Daesung.

What Made Weekend 1 Special

Coverage from STARNEWS detailed the magnitude of the comeback: "As it was the moment when they finally gathered under the name of Big Bang and stood in front of music fans around the world, an explosive heat filled the audience even before the start." The report described the performance as a "thrilling" display of "overwhelming aura and the essence of their long experience."

Each member's solo stage left its own impression. Taeyang brought the heat with "RINGA LINGA," G-Dragon commanded the stage with "PO ₩" (Power), and their unit song "GOOD BOY" showcased the chemistry that has defined BIGBANG for two decades. Daesung, meanwhile, turned the desert into a trot stage — performing "Over the Limit" and "Look at Me, Defect" for a global audience that may never have experienced K-trot before.

"Thank you for being with me in this moment today," Daesung told the crowd. "I think it will be an unforgettable memory."

Weekend 2 Awaits

With BIGBANG confirmed for April 19, Coachella's second weekend promises to be just as historic. The group is expected to deliver a similarly ambitious setlist, though it's unclear whether any surprises are in store for those catching Weekend 2.

BIGBANG debuted in 2006 and grew to become one of the defining acts of second-generation K-pop. Their Coachella return marks their first major international festival appearance as a trio since their 2017 tour — and the beginning of what promises to be a significant anniversary year.
