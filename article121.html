BIGBANG Returns To Coachella Stage April 19 — "The 20th Anniversary Has Just Begun"

April 14, 2026 By Julian Choi 📖 6 min read

BIGBANG is not done with Coachella yet. After their explosive first performance on April 12 drew praise from fans worldwide, the K-pop legends confirmed they will return to the Outdoor Theatre stage on April 19 for the festival's second weekend — and G-Dragon is already promising the best is yet to come.

"The 20th anniversary of Big Bang in 2026 has just begun," G-Dragon told the Coachella crowd during their Weekend 1 set, which ran for 50 minutes and featured a setlist spanning their entire 20-year career. The declaration has since become one of the most-quoted lines from the weekend's performances, resonating with fans who have watched the group navigate lineup changes, military service, and the departure of former member T.O.P.

What Made Weekend 1 Special

Coverage from STARNEWS detailed the magnitude of the comeback: "As it was the moment when they finally gathered under the name of Big Bang and stood in front of music fans around the world, an explosive heat filled the audience even before the start." The report described the performance as a "thrilling" display of "overwhelming aura and the essence of their long experience." From the opening bars of "Bang Bang Bang" to the emotional finale of "Still Life," the show left no doubt that BIGBANG remains a live force.

Each member's solo stage left its own impression. Taeyang brought the heat with "Ringa Ringa," G-Dragon commanded the stage with "Power," and their unit song "Good Boy" showcased the chemistry that has defined BIGBANG for two decades. Daesung, meanwhile, turned the desert into a trot stage — performing "Over the Limit" and "Look at Me, Defect" for a global audience that may never have experienced K-trot before. "Thank you for being with me in this moment today," Daesung told the crowd. "I think it will be an unforgettable memory."

Justin Bieber's Surprise Cameo

The night also delivered an unexpected pop culture moment when Justin Bieber appeared on stage to perform "Baby" with BIGBANG. The collaboration was entirely unannounced and caught even those at the venue by surprise. Fan footage of the moment immediately went viral, with the clip accumulating millions of views within hours. The crossover moment was seen as a testament to BIGBANG's enduring influence on global pop music, bridging K-pop with Western pop in a way that few K-pop groups have achieved.

Weekend 2 Awaits

With BIGBANG confirmed for April 19, Coachella's second weekend promises to be just as historic. The group is expected to deliver a similarly ambitious setlist, though it's unclear whether any surprises are in store for those catching Weekend 2. Coachella's YouTube channel saw an influx of "King of K-pop" and "#BANGCHELLA" comments in the hour leading up to the Weekend 1 performance, reflecting the intense anticipation.

BIGBANG debuted in 2006 and grew to become one of the defining acts of second-generation K-pop. Their Coachella return marks their first major international festival appearance as a trio since their 2017 tour — and the beginning of what promises to be a significant anniversary year. The question on everyone's mind: if Weekend 1 was this explosive, what does BIGBANG have left in store for the final show?
