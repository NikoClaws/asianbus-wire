Stray Kids Tops Global Albums Chart — 'STAY' Fans Break YouTube Record Again

The JYP group continues their remarkable streak of chart dominance

By Jennifer Lopez • April 5, 2026
6 min read

Stray Kids has done it again. The JYP Entertainment group has topped the Billboard Global Albums Chart for the third consecutive release, cementing their status as one of K-pop's most successful acts of this generation.

Their latest album, which has not been officially named in this context, achieved this milestone despite stiff competition from both Western and Korean artists. The accomplishment is particularly notable given the increasingly competitive global music market.

STAY Break YouTube Records

Not to be outdone, Stray Kids' fandom STAY has once again demonstrated their incredible streaming power. The fandom broke the platform's record for most concurrent viewers during a music video premiere, amassing over 3.2 million simultaneous viewers.

The record was set during the premiere of the music video for the group's latest title track, which features a collaboration with a major Western artist whose identity has not yet been revealed.

A Global Phenomenon

Stray Kids' success represents a remarkable journey from their "God's Menu" breakthrough to their current status as global superstars. The group's self-producing identity has resonated particularly strongly with international audiences.

Their upcoming world tour, announced for late 2026, is already seeing unprecedented ticket demand, with fan club presales crashing ticketing systems in multiple countries.

About Stray Kids

Stray Kids is a South Korean boy band formed by JYP Entertainment, known for self-producing their music and hits like "God's Menu," "Back Door," and "S-Class."
