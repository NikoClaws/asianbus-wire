ZEROBASEONE Gyuvin's Song Choice During Last Concert Sparks Divided Reactions

March 17, 2026 By Brandon Lee

ZEROBASEONE member Park Gyuvin has become the center of intense fan debate following his unexpected song selection during the group's emotional final concert before their scheduled hiatus. The choice—a cover of a song closely associated with another artist—has split the ZB1 fanbase between those praising his artistic courage and those questioning his judgment at such a significant moment.

The incident occurred during the solo performance segment of ZEROBASEONE's "FOREVER NINE" concert, billed as their last group performance before members pursue individual activities during the hiatus period. Each member was given the opportunity to perform a song of their choice, with most selecting either original material or covers of iconic K-Pop tracks.

Gyuvin surprised attendees by performing a cover of "Good Night," a song originally performed by the artist COLDE and closely associated with emotional, introspective themes. The performance was technically accomplished, with Gyuvin displaying vocal abilities that impressed many who had primarily seen him in his dancer role within the group.

However, the choice immediately generated controversy for several reasons, splitting the fanbase in ways that have continued to reverberate days after the concert concluded.

The Controversy Explained

The primary source of contention centers on the song's association with melancholy and endings. "Good Night," with its themes of farewell and moving on, struck some fans as an inappropriately somber choice for what was meant to be a celebration of ZEROBASEONE's journey and a promise of future reunion.

"We came to celebrate ZB1, not to be reminded that they're leaving," one fan wrote in a widely-shared post. "The song choice felt like Gyuvin had already moved on emotionally. It made the whole event sadder than it needed to be."

"I was crying before, but after Gyuvin's stage, I was sobbing uncontrollably. The song choice broke me. I don't know if I'm angry or grateful for that, honestly." — Concert attendee's post-event reflection

Others took issue with the fact that the song isn't a K-Pop track in the traditional sense, viewing the choice as Gyuvin positioning himself for a different artistic direction during the hiatus. Some interpreted this as signaling that he sees his future outside the idol framework—a reading that created anxiety among fans worried about the group's long-term cohesion.

A smaller contingent focused on the specific artist association, noting that COLDE has collaborated with figures some fans consider controversial, and arguing that any association—even through a cover—was inappropriate for an idol at a group event.

Defenders Praise Artistic Integrity

Conversely, many fans rushed to defend Gyuvin's choice as a meaningful artistic statement that added emotional depth to the concert experience. They argue that the hiatus is inherently bittersweet and that acknowledging that reality through song choice was honest rather than inappropriate.

"Gyuvin chose a song that reflected how he actually feels about this moment—sad but hopeful, saying goodbye but believing in tomorrow," one defense post argued. "Would you rather he performed something generic and pretended this isn't hard? I respect his authenticity."

Supporters also highlighted the performance quality itself, noting that Gyuvin's vocal abilities in the lower, more emotional register required by "Good Night" showed a side of him rarely seen in ZEROBASEONE's typically high-energy discography. For fans who want him to have solo opportunities, the performance served as compelling evidence of his potential.

"Everyone is talking about the song choice, but can we discuss how GOOD he was? Gyuvin showed range we didn't know he had. If this is what his solo music sounds like, I'm here for it." — Fan account focusing on the performance

The artistic defense extends to the thematic appropriateness of "Good Night" specifically. Unlike a generic sad song, defenders note, "Good Night" contains undertones of hope and the promise of new beginnings—making it arguably perfect for a temporary farewell where reunion is anticipated.

The Group Dynamic Question

Underlying much of the controversy is anxiety about what the hiatus means for ZEROBASEONE's future. As a project group formed through the survival show "Boys Planet," ZB1 has a predetermined contract length, and fans are acutely aware that temporary breaks can sometimes become permanent.

In this context, Gyuvin's song choice has been analyzed for what it might signal about his personal intentions. Some fans worry that selecting a song associated with artistic reinvention indicates he's preparing to distance himself from the idol image. Others counter that reading career intentions into a single cover song is overanalysis bordering on paranoia.

The other eight members' reactions during the performance have also been scrutinized. Fan-captured footage shows varied responses—some members appeared moved, while others seemed surprised. Whether this indicates prior knowledge of the song choice or genuine reaction to an unexpected selection is debated.

Wake One Entertainment, ZB1's management company, has not commented on the controversy directly. The company's social media accounts posted standard concert recap content without addressing the specific debate around Gyuvin's performance.

Fan Community Tensions

The divided reaction has created visible tensions within the ZE_ROSE (ZEROBASEONE's fandom) community. Discussions that were initially focused on concert memories have devolved into arguments about whether criticizing a member's artistic choice is ever acceptable and where the line between feedback and hate lies.

Some Gyuvin solo stans have accused critics of targeting their bias unfairly, noting that other members' solo stages received less scrutiny despite also being unconventional. They argue that Gyuvin's higher profile within the group makes him a more visible target for nitpicking.

Meanwhile, some group stans argue that prioritizing group cohesion over individual expression is essential for project groups with limited lifespans, and that members should be mindful of choices that might create discord or signal divided loyalties.

The debate has spilled onto multiple platforms, with the tone varying significantly across communities. Korean fans have expressed concerns differently than international fans, and the linguistic divide has led to some misunderstandings about what exactly is being criticized.

Gyuvin's Response

Gyuvin himself has addressed the controversy only briefly, through a Weverse post the day after the concert. Without directly referencing the debate, he thanked fans for attending and shared that he chose his solo song "because it says what my heart feels right now—grateful, a little sad, but looking forward to tomorrow."

The post received millions of likes and thousands of comments, both supportive and critical. Some fans viewed it as a classy way to address the controversy without engaging in defensive justification. Others felt it was insufficient and wanted more direct acknowledgment of why the choice upset some fans.

Notably, other ZEROBASEONE members have posted supportive messages about Gyuvin since the concert, though none have directly referenced the specific controversy. The show of member solidarity has been interpreted positively by most fans, though skeptics suggest it may have been coordinated by management.

The Bigger Picture

Beyond the immediate controversy, the incident highlights ongoing tensions in K-Pop around artistic autonomy versus fan expectations. Idols are simultaneously expected to express genuine emotion and carefully manage fan perceptions—requirements that often conflict.

The fact that a song choice at a concert can generate days of debate reflects the intense scrutiny K-Pop artists face for every public decision. For young performers like Gyuvin, who is still in his early twenties, navigating these expectations while maintaining authentic self-expression presents an ongoing challenge.

Industry observers note that the controversy, while uncomfortable, also demonstrates the depth of investment fans have in ZEROBASEONE's future. Indifference would be more concerning than passionate debate. The fact that fans care so much about the signals members send suggests a fanbase that intends to remain engaged through the hiatus.

Looking Ahead

As ZEROBASEONE enters their hiatus period, the "Good Night" controversy will likely fade from active discussion. Members will pursue individual activities—solo music, acting, variety appearances—and attention will shift to their individual journeys.

For Gyuvin specifically, the performance has established that he can handle emotional, vocally challenging material outside ZB1's typical style. Whether he pursues this direction in solo work will be closely watched by both supporters and critics.

The "FOREVER NINE" concert has been announced for eventual release as a DVD/Blu-ray product, meaning Gyuvin's "Good Night" performance will be preserved and continue to be discussed by fans who weren't able to attend in person. How the performance is received by a wider audience may reshape the ongoing debate.

For now, ZE_ROSEs are left to sit with their complicated feelings about a concert that was meant to be a celebration but ended up being more bittersweet than many expected. Perhaps, in that way, Gyuvin's song choice was more appropriate than his critics want to admit—a musical acknowledgment that saying goodbye, even temporarily, is never simple.
