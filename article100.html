April 2026 K-Pop Comeback Calendar Is Stacked — TXT, KISS OF LIFE, NCT WISH, CORTIS, ILLIT & More

By Mina Park April 2, 2026

With BTS dominating headlines through ARIRANG and the FIFA World Cup set to absorb global attention starting in June, April has emerged as K-pop's last open runway for 2026's first half — and everyone from rookie powerhouses to 13-year veterans is fighting for the same window.

The result is one of the most stacked comeback calendars in recent memory. At least a dozen major acts are releasing new music across the month, creating a high-stakes four-week sprint where chart performance is only part of the equation.

The HYBE Wave

HYBE is leading the charge with an unprecedented six artists returning in April. TOMORROW X TOGETHER kicks things off April 13 with their eighth mini album "7TH YEAR: A Moment of Stillness in the Thorns," marking their first release since contract renewals with BigHit Music and what the label is calling their "second chapter."

BigHit's youngest boy group CORTIS will prerelease the title track from its second mini album "GREENGREEN" on April 20, building on a rookie year that saw their debut EP crack the Billboard 200 at No. 15 and top 2 million cumulative sales. Belift Lab's ILLIT returns April 30 with "MAMIHLAPINATAPAI," their fourth mini album.

Additional comebacks from LE SSERAFIM and TWS are confirmed for April though exact dates remain under wraps — making this the single biggest month of HYBE releases in the company's history.

Beyond the Big Three

JYP Entertainment is staking its own claim with KickFlip returning April 6 with "My First Kick" and band unit Xdinary Heroes following on April 17. Over at SM Entertainment, NCT WISH will unveil their first full-length album "Ode to Love" on April 20 — a 10-track set that broadens the group's youth-pop identity.

Four-member girl group KISS OF LIFE fires first on April 6 with the single album "Who is she," ending a 10-month domestic hiatus. Rookie girl group ifeye follows April 15 with "As If," while virtual boy band PLAVE continues its genre-bending run with "Caligo Pt.2" on April 13.

Solo Returns

The solo lane is just as crowded. Former BIGBANG member T.O.P returns tomorrow (April 3) with his first full-length solo album in 13 years. WJSN's Dayoung drops her second digital single "What's a girl to do?" on April 7, and former Wanna One member Park Ji-hoon is also preparing new music.

The Full April Timeline

  • April 3: T.O.P — Solo Album
  • April 6: KISS OF LIFE — "Who is she" | KickFlip — "My First Kick"
  • April 7: Dayoung (WJSN) — "What's a girl to do?"
  • April 9: BTS ARIRANG World Tour Begins (Seoul)
  • April 13: TXT — "7TH YEAR" | PLAVE — "Caligo Pt.2"
  • April 15: ifeye — "As If"
  • April 17: Xdinary Heroes — New Release
  • April 20: NCT WISH — "Ode to Love" | CORTIS — "GREENGREEN" Pre-Release
  • April 30: ILLIT — "MAMIHLAPINATAPAI"
  • TBD: LE SSERAFIM | TWS

In a market this crowded, the bigger question isn't who charts — it's who breaks through the noise and turns timing into lasting impact. April 2026 will separate the heavyweights from the contenders.
