"Phantom Lawyer" Overtakes BTS To Claim Netflix Korea's #1 Spot

March 24, 2026 9 min read By AsianBus News Staff

In a stunning display of K-drama's enduring appeal, the supernatural legal series "Phantom Lawyer" has surged past "BTS: The Comeback Live" to claim the top spot on Netflix Korea's content chart. The achievement comes just four episodes into the SBS drama's run, signaling that 2026's hottest new show may have arrived.

As of Monday morning, "Phantom Lawyer" ranked #1 on Netflix Korea's Top 10 series list, overtaking the BTS concert special that had dominated the platform since its Saturday premiere. The drama has also climbed to #7 on Netflix's global non-English series chart—a remarkable achievement for a show that only premiered two weeks ago.

The performance is particularly striking given the competition. BTS's comeback concert, one of the most anticipated K-pop events in years, had been expected to dominate Korean streaming platforms throughout the week. That a drama could dethrone it speaks to both "Phantom Lawyer's" quality and the unique moment it's having with Korean audiences.

The Rise of "Phantom Lawyer"

"Phantom Lawyer" (Korean title: "신이랑 변호사") premiered on SBS on March 14 to solid but unspectacular ratings of 6.3% for its first episode. The supernatural legal drama follows Shin I-rang (played by Yoo Yeon-seok), a talented but introverted attorney who discovers he can see and communicate with ghosts.

The premise allows for a unique blend of courtroom drama, supernatural mystery, and emotional storytelling. Each episode sees Shin I-rang taking on cases involving spirits with unresolved grievances, using his abilities to uncover truths that conventional investigation could never reveal.

By its fourth episode this past weekend, "Phantom Lawyer" had climbed to a 9.1% viewership rating—a remarkable 44% increase from its premiere. The upward trajectory suggests strong word-of-mouth is driving new viewers to discover the show, a pattern typical of breakout K-drama hits.

The drama also stars Lee Som as Han Na-hyun, a skeptical elite attorney who becomes Shin I-rang's reluctant partner after witnessing his abilities firsthand. Their dynamic—mixing professional rivalry with grudging mutual respect—has become a highlight for viewers who appreciate strong ensemble chemistry.

Yoo Yeon-seok's Triumphant Return

For star Yoo Yeon-seok, "Phantom Lawyer" represents a significant comeback after a period of relative quiet. The 42-year-old actor, best known for his roles in "Reply 1994," "Mr. Sunshine," and the global phenomenon "Hospital Playlist," had taken a step back from leading roles in recent years.

His portrayal of Shin I-rang has drawn particular praise for its subtlety. The character is not a typical drama hero—he's awkward, anxious in social situations, and more comfortable speaking with the dead than the living. Yoo plays these qualities with a gentleness that makes the character immediately endearing.

"What I love about I-rang is that he's not trying to be a hero," Yoo said in a recent interview. "He just sees people—whether living or dead—who need help, and he can't look away. There's something very human about that, even when the situations are supernatural."

The role has also generated renewed discussion about Yoo's earlier controversies. In 2024, resurfaced comments from his past led to accusations of misogyny and calls for boycotts. While "Phantom Lawyer's" success suggests many viewers have moved past these concerns, some critics continue to call attention to the issue.

Why It's Working

Industry observers point to several factors behind "Phantom Lawyer's" rapid rise. First, the show fills a gap in the current drama landscape—offering supernatural elements without the dark intensity of horror, and legal proceedings without the dryness of pure courtroom drama.

The tonal balance has proven especially appealing. While the show deals with death and injustice, its treatment is fundamentally warm. Ghosts aren't presented as frightening but as people with unfinished business—often more sad than scary. This approach creates emotional catharsis that has resonated strongly with viewers.

Second, the episodic structure allows easy entry for new viewers. Each episode features a relatively self-contained case while building longer-term mysteries about Shin I-rang's abilities and past. Viewers can enjoy individual episodes without feeling lost if they've missed previous installments.

Third, the production values are exceptional. SBS has clearly invested heavily in the series, with atmospheric cinematography, convincing supernatural effects, and detailed set design that brings both the legal world and the spirit realm to vivid life.

Beating BTS: A Surprise Victory

That "Phantom Lawyer" managed to overtake BTS content on Netflix Korea underscores just how well the drama is performing. "BTS: The Comeback Live" had been dominating streaming discussions since its Saturday premiere, with the global fanbase driving massive concurrent viewership.

The BTS special remains a phenomenon in its own right—it ranked #1 in 77 countries upon release and continues to dominate globally. But on the domestic Korean chart, the drama's steady rise has proven more sustainable than the concert's explosive premiere-day numbers.

Industry analysts note that this isn't unprecedented. K-dramas often demonstrate stronger long-term performance than one-time music specials, even very popular ones. The weekly episodic structure keeps viewers returning, while concerts typically see a large initial spike followed by gradual decline.

Still, the symbolic victory is notable. BTS is the biggest K-pop act of the generation; any content bearing their name commands automatic attention. For a new drama to surpass that, even briefly, speaks volumes about its appeal.

What's Next

With 12 episodes remaining in its 16-episode run, "Phantom Lawyer" has ample room to build on its success. If current trends continue, the show could become one of 2026's highest-rated Korean dramas and a significant international hit on Netflix.

The next episode, airing this Friday, promises to escalate the stakes. Promotional materials tease that Shin I-rang will face his most challenging case yet—defending a woman accused of murdering her husband, with the husband's ghost insisting she's innocent. Meanwhile, Han Na-hyun takes the prosecution's side, setting up a courtroom confrontation between the two leads.

SBS has not yet announced whether a second season is being considered, but the show's performance makes such discussions likely. The supernatural premise allows for unlimited case possibilities, and viewer enthusiasm suggests strong demand for more.

For now, "Phantom Lawyer" stands as proof that even in an era of global K-pop dominance, K-drama's hold on Korean hearts remains unshakeable.

"Phantom Lawyer" airs Friday and Saturday at 9:50 PM KST on SBS and streams on Netflix Korea.
