Taemin Makes History As First Korean Male Solo Artist To Perform At Coachella

April 2, 2026 4 min read By Jihye Kim

LOS ANGELES — SHINee's Taemin is about to etch his name into festival history. The K-pop icon will become the first Korean male solo artist to perform at Coachella when he takes the stage on April 11 and 18, a milestone he discussed during an appearance on The Jennifer Hudson Show that aired April 1.

"There is a saying in Korea: 'Big opportunities come three times in life,'" Taemin told Hudson when asked how it felt to make history. "And this is one of them."

While Jackson Wang — who is Chinese — was the first male K-pop soloist to perform at the annual California festival in 2024, Taemin holds the distinction of being the first male Korean K-pop solo artist to take the Coachella stage.

Grammy Museum opens first-ever K-pop soloist exhibit

Coachella isn't the only record Taemin is setting this spring. Billboard exclusively reported in March that the Grammy Museum in Los Angeles opened a dedicated exhibit titled "TAEMIN: Performer. Artist. Icon" on April 1 — making him the first K-pop soloist to receive such recognition from the prestigious institution.

The exhibit, running through May 25, features multiple performance looks from across Taemin's nearly two-decade career, including costumes from his Ephemeral Gaze World Tour. Fans can also view his official lightstick and a handwritten note addressed to his supporters.

'I am still evolving'

During his Jennifer Hudson appearance, Taemin also confirmed that new solo music is on the way. When asked about future releases, he replied simply: "Oh, yes. I am still evolving."

He performed his new single "Long Way Home" on the show, delivering a characteristically polished solo performance with his rhinestone microphone — a reminder of why he's earned the nickname "the Idol's Idol" among fellow K-pop artists.

A breakout spring for K-pop at Western festivals

Taemin's Coachella booking comes during an extraordinary moment for K-pop's global crossover. BTS is preparing for a world tour that industry analysts say could rival Taylor Swift's Eras Tour, while acts like aespa, ENHYPEN, and SEVENTEEN continue to land on major Western soundtracks and festival lineups.

For Taemin, who debuted with SHINee in 2008 at age 14, the Coachella milestone caps a remarkable second act. After completing military service and signing with AI entertainment, the 32-year-old has proven that longevity and relevance in K-pop aren't mutually exclusive — and that the biggest stages in the world are still within reach.

His Coachella sets on April 11 and 18 are expected to be among the most-watched performances of the festival weekend.
