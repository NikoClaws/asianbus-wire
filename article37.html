Actor Lee Dong Hwi Publicly Cuts Ties With WINNER Mino Amid Military Scandal

March 18, 2026 By James Cho

In a dramatic turn that has shocked fans of both celebrities, actor Lee Dong Hwi has publicly distanced himself from longtime best friend and WINNER member Mino following the idol's controversial appearance at Lee's movie premiere. The actor's pointed statement that he "did not invite" Mino and was "completely caught off guard" by his presence effectively severs one of the entertainment industry's most celebrated friendships, as Lee Dong Hwi moves to protect his reputation from association with Mino's ongoing military service scandal.

The fallout began when Mino was spotted at the VIP screening event for Lee Dong Hwi's new film "Method Acting." Given their well-documented close friendship—the two have been publicly affectionate about their bond for years—many assumed Mino attended at his friend's invitation. This assumption sparked immediate backlash, with netizens criticizing both as "birds of a feather" and questioning Lee Dong Hwi's judgment in associating with someone facing serious legal troubles.

Lee Dong Hwi's subsequent clarification during a press interview represents a stark break from the celebrity code that typically sees friends stand by each other through scandals. His decision to publicly deny involvement and emphasize his surprise at Mino's presence sends an unmistakable message about the boundaries of their relationship going forward.

Mino's Military Scandal: The Background

For those unfamiliar with the controversy, WINNER's Mino has been embroiled in serious allegations regarding his military service. The idol began serving as a public service worker in March 2023, an alternative to active duty military service typically assigned to those with health conditions that prevent full military participation.

However, Mino was accused of violating the Military Service Act by failing to properly report to duty. Reports indicate he skipped approximately 102 days of service, allegedly leaving his assigned workplace during working hours and failing to maintain proper attendance. He was referred to prosecutors in May of last year, and on December 30, the Seoul Western District Prosecutors' Office indicted him without detention on charges related to negligent military service.

The allegations have severely damaged Mino's public image. In South Korea, where military service is mandatory for able-bodied men, any perceived attempt to shirk or minimize one's service obligations is viewed extremely negatively. The public's patience for celebrity military exemptions or scandals is notoriously limited, and Mino's reported behavior—allegedly playing games during work hours and "skipping shifts whenever he wanted"—has generated intense criticism.

"I did not invite him. I only found out he was there at the venue that day. It was completely unexpected." — Lee Dong Hwi, distancing himself from Mino

A Friendship Under Scrutiny

Lee Dong Hwi and Mino's friendship has been a fixture of entertainment media for years. The two have spoken warmly about each other in interviews, appeared together at public events, and been spotted socializing privately. Their bond seemed genuine and was often held up as an example of authentic friendship in an industry often characterized by calculated relationships.

This history made Mino's appearance at the "Method Acting" premiere seem natural to observers—of course a best friend would show up to support his friend's big moment. But it also meant that Lee Dong Hwi was immediately associated with whatever judgment applied to Mino, a guilt-by-association dynamic that celebrities typically try to avoid.

The speed and directness of Lee Dong Hwi's denial suggests his team recognized the reputational risk immediately. Rather than offering a diplomatic non-comment or waiting for the story to fade, he chose to address the matter head-on, even at the cost of publicly embarrassing a longtime friend.

Public Reaction: Relief And Speculation

Online reaction to Lee Dong Hwi's clarification has been largely supportive, with many expressing relief that the actor "cleared his name." Comments on Korean forums and social media reflect appreciation for his transparency:

"Damn, Mino got cut off LOL," wrote one commenter, capturing the stark nature of the public distancing.

"Lee Dong Hwi must've been so thrown off having his name dragged into this like that," noted another. "I'm glad he cleared his name."

However, some have raised questions about the situation that Lee Dong Hwi's statement doesn't fully answer. If he didn't invite Mino, who did? How did Mino gain access to the VIP premiere without the star's knowledge? These mysteries have prompted speculation about event management practices and whether someone else in Lee Dong Hwi's circle might have extended the invitation.

The Cost Of Association

The incident illustrates the precarious nature of celebrity friendships when one party becomes embroiled in scandal. While loyal friendship is generally admired, the entertainment industry operates on public perception, and association with controversial figures can have tangible career consequences.

Lee Dong Hwi, who has built a successful career including roles in "Reply 1988" and various films, has his own reputation to protect. Endorsement deals, casting opportunities, and public goodwill can all be affected by perceived associations with scandal-plagued figures. The calculus that led to his public distancing, while perhaps painful on a personal level, reflects professional realities.

For Mino, the incident represents another blow to his already damaged public standing. Being publicly disowned by a best friend adds a personal dimension to what was previously primarily a legal and public relations problem. The message sent—that even close friends will distance themselves to protect their careers—underscores his current isolation.

What Happens To Mino Now?

Mino's legal situation continues to develop. Having been indicted without detention, he faces a trial process that will determine the legal consequences of his alleged service violations. The specific charges and potential penalties have not been fully detailed in public reporting, but convictions under the Military Service Act can carry significant penalties including additional service requirements or even imprisonment in serious cases.

Beyond the legal process, Mino's career prospects remain uncertain. YG Entertainment, his agency, has not announced any plans for his activities, and it's unclear whether he will attempt to resume his entertainment career after resolving his legal issues. The intense public backlash suggests that any comeback attempt would face significant headwinds.

During police questioning, Mino reportedly "largely admitted to leaving his assigned workplace during working hours," according to reports. This admission, if accurate, limits his legal defense options and confirms the basic facts underlying the public's negative perception.

Industry Implications

The Lee Dong Hwi-Mino situation offers a case study in how the entertainment industry handles association risk. The rapid, public nature of Lee Dong Hwi's distancing reflects the heightened sensitivity around military service scandals specifically, but the dynamics apply more broadly to any situation where one celebrity's problems might contaminate another's image.

Industry observers note that such public breaks are relatively rare—more often, celebrities quietly reduce contact with problematic associates without explicit statements. Lee Dong Hwi's choice to address the matter directly in a press interview suggests his team calculated that silence would be interpreted as tacit acceptance of Mino's presence and implicit support.

For other celebrities maintaining friendships with figures facing scandals, the incident serves as a reminder of the potential costs of association and the difficult choices that may be required to protect one's career.
