'XO, Kitty' Season 3 Drops Today With BTS V, aespa, ENHYPEN & More On K-Pop-Packed Soundtrack

April 2, 2026 5 min read By Hana Kim

Kitty Song Covey is back — and she brought the entire K-pop industry with her. Netflix's XO, Kitty drops its third and final season today, April 2, and the soundtrack reads like a dream lineup from a MAMA Awards stage. BTS's V (Kim Taehyung), aespa, ENHYPEN, NMIXX, SEVENTEEN's The8, and a half-dozen more K-pop acts fill out what might be the most stacked soundtrack in Netflix original series history.

V and UMI Lead A Star-Studded Track List

The biggest headline is the inclusion of "wherever u r," a collaboration between BTS's V and Japanese-American R&B artist UMI. The dreamy track, which originally appeared on V's solo work, fits the romantic coming-of-age tone of the series perfectly. For ARMY, it's another reminder of V's growing presence as a solo artist even while BTS dominates with their ARIRANG comeback.

But V is far from the only draw. aespa contributes "Whiplash," their high-energy hit that became one of 2025's most-streamed K-pop tracks. ENHYPEN brings the anthemic "Go Big or Go Home," while NMIXX adds their signature genre-bending sound with "Soñar (Breaker)." SEVENTEEN's The8 rounds out the K-pop heavyweights with his solo track "Skyfall."

Rolling Stone revealed the full track list on March 31, and the response from K-pop fans has been overwhelming. Multiple acts trended on X within hours of the announcement, with fans praising Netflix for going all-in on authentic Korean music representation.

A K-Drama That Changed The Game For K-Pop Soundtracks

Since its debut, XO, Kitty has distinguished itself from other K-drama-adjacent Netflix shows by deeply integrating K-pop into its DNA. While shows like Squid Game and The Glory captured global audiences through storytelling, XO, Kitty specifically targeted the overlap between K-drama viewers and K-pop fans — and it worked.

Season 1 featured tracks from ENHYPEN, XG, and other rising acts. Season 2 expanded the roster further. But Season 3's lineup represents a significant escalation, pulling in top-tier names like V and aespa alongside newer groups like MEOVV and MAKAMAKA, effectively bridging generations of K-pop in a single soundtrack.

"The music is as much a character in this show as Kitty herself," creator Jenny Han told Rolling Stone. "We wanted Season 3 to feel like a love letter to K-pop — because that's what this show has always been."

Kitty's Senior Year: What To Expect

Season 3 follows Kitty Song Covey as she returns to the Korean Independent School of Seoul (KISS) for her senior year. The spin-off of Jenny Han's To All the Boys I've Loved Before franchise has built a loyal following across three seasons, blending teen romance, identity exploration, and Korean culture into a bingeable package.

While Netflix hasn't officially confirmed this is the final season, multiple reports suggest Season 3 will wrap up Kitty's story at KISS, making the loaded soundtrack feel like a fitting farewell.

The timing couldn't be better for K-pop visibility on mainstream Western platforms. With BTS's ARIRANG dominating charts, BLACKPINK's Lisa announcing a Las Vegas residency, and now a Netflix series dropping with an all-star K-pop soundtrack, April 2026 is shaping up as a landmark month for Korean pop culture's global reach.

XO, Kitty Season 3 is streaming now on Netflix worldwide.
