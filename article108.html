BTS Wins Major Honor From Arirang TV — "Arirang" Album Sells 3.98M Copies Day 1

BTS kicks off world tour with record-breaking album sales and 18.4M Netflix viewers

By Sarah Chen • April 8, 2026
7 min read

BTS continues to redefine what boy bands can achieve. The Korean Broadcasting System's Arirang TV has awarded BTS a special honor for their contributions to Korean culture, just as their "Arirang" album breaks day-one sales records worldwide.

The album sold an unprecedented 3.98 million copies in its first day, shattering previous records and demonstrating BTS's unmatched commercial power even after military service hiatus. The achievement is particularly remarkable given the current state of the music industry.

Arirang TV Honor

Arirang TV, an English-language network operated by KBS, recognized BTS with their Cultural Contribution Award, citing the group's role in popularizing Korean culture globally. The award ceremony will take place during the opening ceremony of BTS's ARIRANG World Tour on April 9.

"This album represents our journey home, our connection to Korean heritage, and our gratitude to fans worldwide." — RM

The "Arirang" album has received critical acclaim for blending traditional Korean musical elements with contemporary pop, creating a sound that resonates both domestically and internationally.

Netflix Documentary Success

Meanwhile, the BTS documentary "Yet To Come: The Tour" premiered on Netflix, amassing 18.4 million viewers in its first week. The film follows the group through their preparation for and execution of their record-breaking tour.

About BTS

BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan) is a South Korean boy band consisting of RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook. They are known for their social activism and record-breaking music.
