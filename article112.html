Mark, Ten & Heeseung: Why K-Pop's Biggest Stars Are Walking Away

Industry insiders explain what's driving the wave of K-pop star departures

By Soo-yeon Kim • April 7, 2026
8 min read

The K-pop industry is experiencing an unprecedented wave of departures. In the past month alone, Mark Lee, Ten, and Heeseung have all announced significant career changes, prompting industry-wide conversations about the pressures facing idol culture.

Mark's exit from SM Entertainment and NCT after a decade-long tenure marks the most high-profile departure, but he's far from alone. Ten announced he would leave SM Entertainment while maintaining his position within NCT, and Heeseung's recent announcement suggests more changes may be coming to ENHYPEN.

Changing Industry Dynamics

Industry insiders point to several factors driving these departures. Long-term contract disputes, desire for creative freedom, and the grueling schedules of idol life are all contributing factors. "Idols are increasingly aware of their value and are demanding more control over their careers," one entertainment executive explained.

The rise of social media has also empowered idols to build personal brands independently of their agencies, making the traditional idol contract structure less appealing to established artists.

Contract Length Disputes

Many of the departing stars have cited contract length as a major issue. The standard seven-year contract, originally designed for rookie artists, often doesn't serve established idols who have outgrown the structure.

Legal experts note that Korean law has been evolving to better protect entertainers, with newer regulations providing more leverage for artists seeking contract modifications or terminations.

Fans React

Fans have responded with a mixture of support and anxiety. While many celebrate artists' freedom to pursue their desired paths, others worry about the implications for their favorite groups and the broader K-pop ecosystem.

About the Artists

Mark Lee, Ten, and Heeseung are among the most talented and popular idols in K-pop, representing different generations and groups within the NCT and ENHYPEN systems.
