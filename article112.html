Mark, Ten & Heeseung: Why K-Pop's Biggest Stars Are Walking Away

Mark, Ten & Heeseung: Why K-Pop's Biggest Stars Are Walking Away

By Staff Writer • April 3, 2026
6 min read

SEOUL — The K-pop industry is experiencing an unprecedented wave of high-profile departures. Within the span of a month, Mark and Ten of NCT, plus ENHYPEN's Heeseung, have all announced major career changes — and fans are scrambling to make sense of it all.

Mark Leaves NCT and SM Entertainment

On April 3, 2026, SM Entertainment confirmed that Mark would depart from both NCT and the agency after exactly 10 years. The Canadian star, who debuted as part of NCT U in 2016, was a foundational member of NCT 127 and NCT DOJAEJUNG. His service through the fan platform Bubble was terminated in April, with his final official activities wrapping up in the days following the announcement.

Ten Leaves SM — But Stays With NCT

Just days after Mark's announcement, Ten revealed he would not renew his contract with SM Entertainment. However, unlike Mark, Ten will continue group activities with NCT and sub-unit WayV through individual agreements — a model similar to arrangements seen with Taemin of SHINee and D.O. of EXO.

Ten, who celebrated his 10-year anniversary with SM in 2026, has been a core member of NCT since his debut in 2016. His future activities are expected to be announced separately.

Heeseung Left ENHYPEN Back in March

The departure wave started earlier than many realized. On March 10, 2026, Heeseung announced he would leave ENHYPEN and transition to a solo career through Belift Lab. The agency said the decision followed "deep discussions" about the group's direction and each member's future.

Just weeks after announcing his exit, Heeseung hinted at an upcoming solo album — fueling speculation that artistic ambition played a central role in his decision.

What's Really Driving These Departures?

Industry insiders say the timing comes down to contract cycles. Most K-pop groups operate under seven-year contracts, but NCT members are believed to have signed longer agreements due to the group's project-based structure. With Mark and Ten both hitting their 10-year mark in 2026, renewal periods became the pivotal moment for weighing group versus solo paths.

The executive added that creative limitations within group structures often drive these decisions. "Management ultimately has to focus on profitability, which often means aligning with what fans want. But not every member will be satisfied with that direction. Younger artists may accept it, but those with over 10 years of experience find it harder to compromise."

A Bigger Trend?

Music critic Lim Hee-yun pointed to structural changes in the entertainment landscape. The rise of digital platforms has fundamentally altered how idols can build and monetize their solo careers — making independent pursuits more viable than ever before.

For now, fans of NCT and ENHYPEN are processing the losses while keeping a close eye on what comes next. Mark's complete departure from SM marks the end of a decade-long chapter, while Ten's split suggests his NCT journey may not be over just yet.

"Mark's departure marks the end of an era for NCT," one fan wrote on X. "He's been there from the very beginning."
"There are always members who have a clear sense of what they want to pursue musically," an executive at a major K-pop agency told The Straits Times. "Before their debut, they prioritize making it into a group and often compromise. But over time — especially after completing military service — many begin to seek individual careers."
