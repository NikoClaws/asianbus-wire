ENHYPEN Crisis: Heeseung's Departure Sparks Massive "ENHYPEN Is 7" Fan Movement

March 17, 2026 By AsianBus Wire Staff

The K-Pop world has been rocked by the sudden announcement that ENHYPEN's Heeseung will no longer be part of the beloved boy group, triggering one of the largest fan protests in recent memory.

Belift Lab, the agency managing ENHYPEN, confirmed in a statement that Heeseung's departure is permanent, stating unequivocally that "Heeseung will not return to ENHYPEN." The announcement has left ENGENEs (ENHYPEN's fanbase) devastated and demanding answers.

Fan Protests Go Viral

Within hours of the announcement, the hashtag #ENHYPENIs7 began trending worldwide. Fans have organized coordinated protests across social media platforms, with some going to extreme lengths to voice their displeasure. A dedicated website calling for Heeseung's return has garnered millions of visits, demonstrating the intensity of fan sentiment.

"We refuse to accept this decision. ENHYPEN was formed as seven, and seven they should remain." — ENGENE Fan Coalition Statement

What Led to This Decision?

While the official statement from Belift Lab remained vague, citing "personal circumstances," industry insiders suggest the decision may be related to scheduling conflicts and creative differences. Heeseung, known for his exceptional vocals and stage presence, had reportedly expressed interest in pursuing solo activities.

The remaining six members — Jungwon, Jay, Jake, Sunghoon, Sunoo, and Ni-ki — have not yet made public statements about their bandmate's departure.

Impact on Upcoming Activities

ENHYPEN's scheduled world tour is expected to proceed as planned, though setlists and formations will need to be restructured. The group's upcoming album release date remains unchanged, raising questions about whether Heeseung's contributions were already recorded.

Fans have noted that concert tickets, many of which were purchased with the expectation of seeing all seven members, may need to be refunded. Several fan sites have organized boycotts of upcoming releases until clearer communication is provided.
