ENHYPEN Crisis: Heeseung's Departure Sparks Massive "ENHYPEN Is 7" Fan Movement

March 17, 2026 By AsianBus Wire Staff

The K-Pop world has been rocked by the sudden announcement that ENHYPEN's Heeseung will no longer be part of the beloved boy group, triggering one of the largest fan protests in recent memory.

Belift Lab, the agency managing ENHYPEN, confirmed in a statement that Heeseung's departure is permanent, stating unequivocally that "Heeseung will not return to ENHYPEN." The announcement has left ENGENEs (ENHYPEN's fanbase) devastated and demanding answers.

Fan Protests Go Viral

Within hours of the announcement, the hashtag #ENHYPENIs7 began trending worldwide. Fans have organized coordinated protests across social media platforms, with some going to extreme lengths to voice their displeasure. A dedicated website calling for Heeseung's return has garnered millions of visits, demonstrating the intensity of fan sentiment.

"We will not accept a 6-member ENHYPEN," wrote one fan on social media. "They debuted as 7, and they should remain as 7."

Members' Reactions

Perhaps most heartbreaking for fans has been the alleged reactions from the remaining members themselves. Reports suggest that the members were emotional upon learning the news, with some allegedly being caught off-guard by the timing of the announcement.

Member Jungwon has faced intense scrutiny after responding to an "OT6" comment on social media, with fans analyzing his every word for hints about the group's future direction. Meanwhile, Sunghoon's recent comments about solo activities have taken on new meaning in light of Heeseung's departure, with fans expressing concern about the group's long-term stability.

What's Next for ENHYPEN?

The situation remains fluid, with Belift Lab yet to announce concrete plans for ENHYPEN's future activities. Fans remain divided on whether to continue supporting the group or to boycott until Heeseung's return is secured.

As one long-time fan noted, "This is a defining moment for ENHYPEN. How the agency handles this situation will determine whether they can maintain their position as one of K-Pop's leading groups."
