ENHYPEN Crisis: Heeseung's Departure Sparks Massive "ENHYPEN Is 7" Fan Movement

March 17, 2026 By Selena Jung

The K-Pop industry was sent into shockwaves yesterday when Belift Lab officially announced that Lee Heeseung, one of ENHYPEN's founding members and the group's eldest, would be departing from the seven-member boy group effective immediately. The announcement has triggered one of the largest coordinated fan protests in recent K-Pop history.

In a statement released through ENHYPEN's official social media channels at 6:00 PM KST, Belift Lab—the joint venture between HYBE and CJ ENM that manages the group—confirmed what many fans had feared following days of speculation. "After extensive discussions with all parties involved, we regret to inform fans that Heeseung will no longer continue activities as a member of ENHYPEN," the statement read.

The agency's statement notably declined to provide specific reasons for the departure, citing only "various circumstances" and "mutual agreement between the artist and the company." This vague explanation has done little to quell the mounting frustration among ENGENEs, ENHYPEN's dedicated fanbase, who have demanded transparency from the agency.

The Birth of "ENHYPEN Is 7"

Within hours of the announcement, the hashtag #ENHYPENIs7 began trending worldwide on Twitter, reaching the number one spot in South Korea, Japan, the United States, and 47 other countries. The movement, which takes its name from the group's original seven-member formation on the 2020 survival show I-LAND, has become a rallying cry for fans refusing to accept the departure.

Fan accounts that previously focused on promoting ENHYPEN's music have transformed into organizational hubs for the protest movement. A dedicated website, enhypenis7.com, was launched within 12 hours of the announcement and has already received over 3 million unique visitors. The site features a petition demanding Heeseung's reinstatement, which has gathered more than 800,000 signatures at the time of publication.

"We watched these seven boys grow together from trainees to global superstars. ENHYPEN was formed as seven through the votes of global fans, and seven they must remain. We will not rest until Heeseung is back where he belongs." — Official statement from the Global ENGENE Coalition

Physical protests have also begun materializing outside Belift Lab's headquarters in Seoul. Approximately 200 fans gathered on the first day, holding signs and chanting slogans. The protest has remained peaceful but shows no signs of dispersing, with organizers stating they plan to maintain a presence "for as long as it takes."

Industry Insiders Weigh In

Sources within the K-Pop industry, speaking on condition of anonymity, have offered conflicting accounts of what led to Heeseung's departure. One insider suggested that creative differences played a significant role, claiming that Heeseung had expressed interest in pursuing a different musical direction than the group's current concept.

"There were discussions about solo activities," the source revealed. "Heeseung is incredibly talented and wanted to explore opportunities that may not have aligned with ENHYPEN's group schedule. Whether this was the primary cause or just a contributing factor, I can't say for certain."

Another industry source pointed to scheduling conflicts and the intense pressure of maintaining ENHYPEN's grueling promotional schedule. "These idols work incredibly hard," the source noted. "Sometimes the physical and mental toll becomes too much. It's possible this was a health-related decision that the company is choosing not to publicize out of respect for Heeseung's privacy."

Belift Lab has not responded to requests for additional comment beyond their initial statement.

Impact on Upcoming Activities

ENHYPEN's highly anticipated world tour, "FATE PLUS," which was scheduled to begin next month, remains officially on track according to the agency. However, significant restructuring will be required. Heeseung held several key positions within the group's performances, including main vocalist duties and center positions in multiple choreographies.

The group's upcoming studio album, tentatively scheduled for release in May, also faces uncertainty. Industry analysts speculate that songs featuring Heeseung's vocals may need to be re-recorded or removed from the tracklist entirely, potentially delaying the release.

Concert ticket holders have begun demanding refunds, arguing that they purchased tickets expecting to see all seven members perform. A class action lawsuit is reportedly being organized by fan groups in South Korea, though legal experts suggest such action faces significant hurdles given the standard terms and conditions of K-Pop concert tickets.

The Remaining Members' Silence

Perhaps most notable in the aftermath of the announcement has been the complete silence from ENHYPEN's remaining six members: Jungwon, Heeseung's co-leader Jay, Jake, Sunghoon, Sunoo, and the group's youngest member Ni-ki. None have posted on personal social media accounts or made any public statements regarding their bandmate's departure.

This silence has been interpreted differently by various fan factions. Some believe the members are bound by contractual obligations that prevent them from speaking freely. Others hope the silence indicates the members themselves disagree with the decision and may be working behind the scenes to reverse it.

A source close to one of the members suggested that the remaining six are "devastated" by the situation but have been advised by management to refrain from public comment until the situation stabilizes.

Heeseung's Legacy With ENHYPEN

Lee Heeseung, 23, was the first trainee to be announced as a member of ENHYPEN during the finale of I-LAND on September 18, 2020. As the group's eldest member and one of its most skilled vocalists and dancers, he played a crucial role in establishing ENHYPEN's identity and sound.

Throughout the group's career, Heeseung contributed to numerous hit songs and was known for his exceptional stage presence and technical abilities. His departure leaves a significant void in the group's vocal line and performance formations.

Prior to joining ENHYPEN, Heeseung trained under Big Hit Entertainment (now HYBE) for approximately three years, during which he appeared in several BTS-related content as a backup dancer, leading to early comparisons to the senior group's members.

What Happens Next

As of press time, Heeseung has not made any personal statements regarding his departure or future plans. His personal social media accounts remain active but have not been updated since before the announcement.

The K-Pop industry will be watching closely in the coming weeks to see whether fan pressure can influence Belift Lab's decision—a rare occurrence in the traditionally company-controlled idol system—or whether this marks the permanent end of ENHYPEN as fans have known it.

For now, the "ENHYPEN Is 7" movement shows no signs of slowing down. Fans have announced plans for coordinated streaming events, billboard advertisements in major cities, and continued protests until their demands are addressed.
