SEVENTEEN's Vernon and The8 Announce Historic New Unit With June Album Release

Pledis Entertainment confirms the group's sixth official sub-unit will debut this summer, with both members co-producing the album

Photo: SEVENTEEN performing | Credit: CJ ENM / MAMA Awards

SEOUL, SOUTH KOREA — In an announcement that has sent shockwaves through the K-pop industry, Pledis Entertainment has confirmed that SEVENTEEN members Vernon and The8 will debut as the group's sixth official sub-unit this summer, with an album planned for June 2026.

The news, which broke on March 12, 2026, has ignited massive excitement among CARATs (SEVENTEEN's official fandom), who have long speculated about the artistic chemistry between the two members. This marks a significant milestone for SEVENTEEN, expanding their already impressive roster of sub-units that includes the Vocal Unit, Performance Unit, Hip-Hop Unit, BSS (BooSeokSoon), and Jeonghan x Wonwoo.

An Unexpected But Perfect Pairing

At first glance, Vernon and The8 might seem like an unlikely duo. Vernon, the Korean-American rapper known for his alternative hip-hop influences and laid-back demeanor, comes from SEVENTEEN's Hip-Hop Unit. The8 (born Xu Minghao), the Chinese member celebrated for his avant-garde fashion sense and contemporary dance skills, anchors the Performance Unit.

However, fans and industry insiders have noted the two members share a deeper connection: both are known as the "quiet creatives" of SEVENTEEN, preferring thoughtful artistry over flashy performances. Their previous collaboration on the track "Orbit" showcased a unique musical synergy that had fans clamoring for more.

🎵 Vernon & The8 Unit: Key Facts

  • Debut: June 2026 (exact date TBA)
  • Format: Full album release
  • Production: Co-written by Vernon; Visual direction by The8
  • Sub-unit Number: SEVENTEEN's 6th official unit
  • Possible Names: Fans suggest "V8" or "VIII"

Both Members Taking Creative Control

What sets this unit apart from previous SEVENTEEN sub-units is the level of creative involvement from both members. According to Pledis Entertainment, Vernon will co-write and lead production on multiple tracks. This comes as no surprise to industry watchers, as Vernon became a voting member of the Recording Academy in recent years, cementing his credentials as a serious music producer.

Meanwhile, The8 is expected to take the helm on the visual direction of the album, ensuring that the aesthetic matches the musical vision. The8 has previously directed photography exhibitions and is known for his distinctive artistic eye, often blending Eastern and Western visual elements in his personal projects.

"Vernon's alternative hip-hop influences paired with The8's avant-garde artistic flair could create something we've never seen from SEVENTEEN before. This is the duo we didn't know we needed." — Korean music critic at IZM Magazine

Strategic Timing During World Tour

The announcement comes as SEVENTEEN continues their massively successful "New_" world tour, which has already drawn over 840,000 fans across multiple continents. The tour recently made stops in the Philippines, where the group performed at the Philippine Sports Stadium in Bulacan to sold-out crowds.

Industry analysts view the unit debut as a strategic move by Pledis Entertainment to maintain momentum while the full group is on tour. It also provides Vernon and The8 an opportunity to showcase their individual artistry outside of SEVENTEEN's high-energy group performances.

Fan Reaction: Euphoric Anticipation

Social media exploded following the announcement, with "Vernon" and "The8" trending worldwide on X (formerly Twitter). CARATs have already begun speculating about potential unit names, with "V8" and "VIII" (the Roman numeral for 8, playing on The8's stage name) emerging as fan favorites.

"I've been waiting for this combination since 'Orbit' dropped," wrote one fan account with over 500,000 followers. "Vernon's chill, thoughtful lyrics with The8's artistic vision? This is going to be iconic."

The announcement also sparked discussions about SEVENTEEN's unique approach to sub-units. Unlike many K-pop groups that rarely explore member combinations outside their assigned units, SEVENTEEN has consistently experimented with different pairings, allowing members to explore diverse artistic directions.

What We Know So Far

While specific details about the album remain under wraps, several key points have been confirmed:

Release Window: June 2026, providing approximately three months of preparation time. This timeline suggests the project has been in development for some time before the official announcement.

Album Format: A full album release rather than a single or EP, indicating significant musical content and a major investment from Pledis Entertainment.

Production Involvement: Both members will have hands-on roles, with Vernon focusing on music production and The8 on visual direction.

Global Release: The album will have a global release, reflecting SEVENTEEN's international fanbase and the members' appeal across different markets.

The8's Busy 2026 Schedule

The unit announcement adds to what's already shaping up to be a packed year for The8. The Chinese member was recently announced as a headline performer at the Midou Music Festival in China, demonstrating his strong solo appeal in the Chinese market.

Despite his busy schedule, The8 has consistently emphasized his commitment to group activities. In a recent Allure Korea interview alongside Vernon, both members credited CARATs as their primary motivation, expressing gratitude for the fans' continued support throughout SEVENTEEN's nearly decade-long career.

Looking Ahead

As SEVENTEEN approaches their 11th anniversary in May 2026, the Vernon x The8 unit represents the group's continued evolution and willingness to push creative boundaries. For a group that has consistently defied expectations in the K-pop industry, this unexpected pairing feels perfectly on brand.

Fans can expect more details about the unit debut in the coming weeks, with teasers and concept photos likely to drop as June approaches. Until then, CARATs worldwide will be counting down the days until Vernon and The8 unveil what promises to be one of the year's most anticipated K-pop releases.
