'Siren's Kiss' Complete Guide: Park Min-young & Wi Ha-joon's New Romance Drama

March 19, 2026 By Grace Kim

Park Min-young returns to the romance drama genre that made her a household name, this time paired with the charismatic Wi Ha-joon in "Siren's Kiss." The tvN production, currently airing its first season, has already captured audiences with its blend of fantasy elements, romantic tension, and the undeniable chemistry between its leads. Here's everything you need to know about one of Spring 2026's must-watch K-Dramas.

The Premise

"Siren's Kiss" offers a modern twist on mermaid mythology. Park Min-young plays Han Seol-a, a mysterious woman with a secret—she's a siren who has lived among humans for centuries, using her abilities to survive while avoiding emotional connections that could expose her true nature. Her powers include the ability to influence human emotions through her voice, though using them comes at a personal cost.

Wi Ha-joon portrays Cha U-seok, a marine biologist researching unexplained phenomena in Korean coastal waters. His investigation leads him to Seol-a, and despite her efforts to keep him at distance, their lives become increasingly intertwined. As he gets closer to discovering her secret, she faces an impossible choice between the connection she's avoided for centuries and the protection of her kind.

The drama explores themes of identity, belonging, and the courage to be vulnerable. Seol-a's journey is not just about romance but about accepting herself and deciding whether to continue hiding or finally reveal her true nature to someone she trusts.

The Cast

Park Min-young as Han Seol-a: The actress brings her signature warmth and emotional depth to a character who has spent centuries building walls around her heart. Park has noted in interviews that playing a non-human character offered unique challenges, requiring her to convey alienation and longing simultaneously.

Wi Ha-joon as Cha U-seok: Fresh off his "Squid Game" and "The Worst of Evil" success, Wi Ha-joon takes on a warmer, more romantic role. His character's scientific skepticism gradually gives way to acceptance as he falls for a woman who defies everything he thought he knew about the world.

Kim Jung-hyun as Baek Jun-beom: Playing Seol-a's longtime protector—another siren who has watched over her for centuries—Kim Jung-hyun brings complexity to what could have been a simple second-lead role. His character's motives are layered, and his relationship with Seol-a raises questions about whether protection can become possession.

Han Joon-woo as Do Eun-hyeok: U-seok's research partner and best friend, providing both comic relief and emotional support. His character serves as the audience's perspective, reacting to supernatural revelations with relatable disbelief.

Gong Sung-ha as Kong Ju-yeong: A young woman who works at a seaside cafe and becomes Seol-a's first human friend. Her innocent acceptance of Seol-a contrasts with the danger that threatens them both.

Lee Elijah as Kim Yun-ji: An antagonist figure—a siren hunter whose family has tracked these creatures for generations. Her obsession with destroying sirens stems from personal tragedy, making her more than a simple villain.

Early Reception

The first four episodes have garnered positive reviews and solid ratings. The premiere achieved 6.8% viewership nationally—a strong start for a cable drama—with subsequent episodes maintaining similar numbers. Online communities have praised the cinematography, which makes extensive use of coastal locations and underwater sequences.

Chemistry between Park Min-young and Wi Ha-joon has been the most discussed aspect. Their scenes together have generated significant social media engagement, with several moments becoming viral clips. Industry observers note that their pairing succeeds because both actors bring intensity while maintaining the lighthearted moments that define successful romance dramas.

Critics have also highlighted the drama's handling of its fantasy elements. Rather than overwhelming the narrative, the supernatural aspects enhance the romance, creating stakes that feel genuine and consequences that matter.

Production Notes

Director Hwang In-hyuk, known for "The King's Affection," brings a visually distinctive style to the series. Underwater sequences were filmed using a combination of practical water work and advanced CGI, with Park Min-young undergoing swim training for several months before filming began.

The drama's coastal setting utilizes locations in Gangwon Province, including Sokcho and the Eastern Sea coast. Several scenes were filmed at actual marine research facilities, lending authenticity to Wi Ha-joon's character's professional environment.

The costume design has received particular attention, with Seol-a's wardrobe incorporating oceanic colors and flowing fabrics that subtly suggest her aquatic nature without being obvious. As the series progresses, her clothing choices evolve to reflect her emotional journey.

OST Highlights

The soundtrack features contributions from several notable artists:

• "Deep Blue" by Taeyeon (opening theme) - A haunting ballad that plays over sequences of Seol-a's memories

• "Surface" by Paul Kim - A romantic track that accompanies the leads' developing relationship

• "Centuries" by Gummy - An emotional song that reflects Seol-a's experience of watching centuries pass

The background score, composed by Nam Hye-seung, blends orchestral elements with oceanic soundscapes, creating an immersive audio experience that enhances the drama's fantasy atmosphere.

Why You Should Watch

"Siren's Kiss" offers a refreshing take on several familiar genres. For romance fans, the slow-burn relationship between leads who are literally from different worlds provides satisfying emotional development. For fantasy enthusiasts, the mythology is carefully constructed and internally consistent. For those who simply enjoy quality K-Drama, the production values, performances, and writing deliver on all fronts.

Park Min-young, after years of playing relatable, contemporary characters, brings something different to Seol-a—a sense of timelessness and melancholy that underlies even her lightest moments. Wi Ha-joon demonstrates his range by playing sincerity without falling into naivety, making U-seok's gradual acceptance of the impossible feel earned rather than convenient.

With twelve episodes remaining, "Siren's Kiss" has established its world and relationships effectively. The question now is whether the drama can maintain its momentum through to a satisfying conclusion—but early indications suggest viewers who start this journey will want to see it through.
