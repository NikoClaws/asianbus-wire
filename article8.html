LE SSERAFIM Members Made "Uncomfortable" During Fan Event — Agency Response Sparks Fury

March 17, 2026 By AsianBus Wire Staff

LE SSERAFIM members were visibly distressed during a recent fan signing event after attendees engaged in behavior that fans are calling "completely unacceptable."

Videos from the event, which quickly went viral on social media, show members appearing uncomfortable and exchanging concerned glances with each other. The footage has sparked intense debate about fan behavior and the responsibilities of event organizers.

What Happened

According to eyewitness accounts, several attendees made inappropriate requests and comments during their one-on-one interactions with the members. While specific details have been kept private out of respect for the idols, those present described the atmosphere as "tense" and "uncomfortable."

"You could see on their faces that something was wrong," shared one fan who attended the event. "The staff seemed unprepared to handle the situation."

Fan Community Response

FEARNOTs (LE SSERAFIM's fanbase) have rallied to support the members while condemning the behavior. A trending hashtag calling for better security protocols at fan events garnered millions of interactions within hours.

"These events are supposed to be positive experiences," wrote one fan. "The agency needs to implement stricter guidelines and actually enforce them."

HYBE's Statement

Source Music, LE SSERAFIM's agency under HYBE, released a brief statement acknowledging the incident. "We are reviewing our event protocols and will take appropriate measures to ensure the comfort and safety of our artists at future events."

Critics have called the response insufficient, arguing that it "encourages this unacceptable behavior" by not taking stronger immediate action. Calls for blacklisting problematic attendees and implementing stricter vetting processes have gained significant traction online.

The incident has reignited broader conversations about parasocial relationships and the boundaries between K-Pop idols and their fans.
