BTS Drops 'Oldboy'-Inspired '2.0' Music Video — Second ARIRANG Visual In Two Weeks

April 2, 2026 7 min read By Selena Jung

BTS isn't slowing down. Just two weeks after the release of their comeback album ARIRANG and its lead single "SWIM," the septet has dropped a second music video — this time for the B-side track "2.0" — and it's a complete tonal shift that's already sending ARMY into a frenzy. Released on April 2 at midnight KST, the cinematic visual pays direct homage to Park Chan-wook's iconic 2003 film Oldboy, swapping the original's brutal hammer fight for something only BTS could pull off: a hallway dance battle in sharp suits.

From Elevator to Corridor: The Oldboy Connection

The music video opens with all seven BTS members — RM, Jin, SUGA, j-hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook — riding an elevator in tailored suits and leather jackets. When the doors open, they step into a dim, grungy corridor packed with thugs, recreating the claustrophobic tension of Oldboy's legendary one-take hallway fight scene.

But instead of wielding a hammer like Oh Dae-su, BTS employs a very different weapon: choreography. The members launch into a high-energy dance routine that sends the thugs scattering, turning what could have been a violent confrontation into a display of synchronized precision. One member is visibly holding a back scratcher. Another is sporting a fake beard. It's ridiculous and brilliant in equal measure.

The visual peaks when RM lowers a newspaper to reveal himself in a floppy wig and oversized sunglasses — a direct nod to Choi Min-sik's iconic portrayal of Oh Dae-su. The styling drew immediate comparisons not just to Oldboy but also to actor Park Seo-joon, with fans flooding social media with side-by-side comparisons that quickly went viral.

BIGHIT MUSIC confirmed in a press release that the video includes a "special thanks" credit to director Park Chan-wook himself, suggesting the filmmaker gave his blessing to the homage.

A Song About Reinvention

"2.0" carries a very different energy from the soulful, contemplative "SWIM." Where the lead single dealt with resilience and moving forward after years of separation, "2.0" is pure swagger — a hip-hop and trap-driven track built on an unconventional rhythm that calls back to BTS's earlier, more aggressive sound.

The song features lyrics penned by RM, j-hope, V, Jungkook, and SUGA, and is described by BIGHIT MUSIC as "a song about the BTS of right now, having entered a new phase following the change and growth of their past." The title itself is a meta-reference — this is BTS 2.0, the group rebooted and upgraded after their nearly four-year hiatus.

The visual underscores the reinvention theme with a clever wardrobe change. After the corridor sequence, the members return to the elevator and emerge in completely new, modern outfits — calling back to the lyric "We on that brand new." It's a literal transformation, old BTS stepping out and new BTS walking in.

The Video's Key Moments

Beyond the Oldboy homage, the music video packs several standout sequences. After the hallway battle, BTS transitions to a second corridor where they perform a more polished choreography without opponents, showcasing the kind of synchronized dancing that made them famous in the first place.

The group then moves to a grimy bathroom set piece, dancing in front of mirrors while singing the chorus — a scene that manages to feel both cinematic and oddly intimate. The transition from dark corridors to a sleek high-rise building for the final sequence provides a visual metaphor for the group's journey from underground beginnings to global dominance.

Fans have also noted several Easter eggs throughout the video, including nods to previous BTS music videos and what appears to be a recreation of the room from Oldboy where Oh Dae-su learns the film's devastating twist.

ARMY Reacts: 'Gangster Bangtan' Trends Worldwide

The reaction from fans has been electric. Within hours of the video's release, "Gangster Bangtan," "2.0 MV," and "Oldboy BTS" were trending worldwide on X (formerly Twitter). ARMY praised the choreography, the humor, and the cinematic quality, with many calling it one of BTS's most creative music videos to date.

"They went from making you cry with SWIM to making you scream with 2.0 in two weeks," one popular fan account posted. "Only BTS can do that range."

The video's tone — playful, self-aware, and physically impressive — has also drawn praise from film critics and K-pop commentators who appreciated the respectful but comedic nod to Park Chan-wook's work. Several noted that the "special thanks" credit to the director elevated the homage from fan tribute to genuine artistic collaboration.

Run BTS 2.0 Returns April 7

The "2.0" MV arrives just days before another major BTS event: the return of their beloved variety show, now titled Run BTS 2.0, which premieres April 7 with all seven members. The timing feels intentional — the song, the show, and the upcoming world tour (starting April 9 at Goyang Stadium) are all part of a coordinated ARIRANG rollout that has kept BTS in the global conversation for weeks straight.

With an 82-date world tour spanning 34 cities about to launch, a Netflix documentary now streaming, and now two hit music videos from a single album, BTS's ARIRANG era is shaping up to be the most comprehensive comeback in K-pop history.

"2.0" is streaming now on all major platforms. The full music video is available on the HYBE Labels YouTube channel. BTS's world tour begins April 9 at Goyang Stadium.
