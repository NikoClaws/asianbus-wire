NewJeans Reveals 'Tomorrow' Release Date — Hanni and Minji Tease 'Most Personal Album Yet'

The Gen Z icons promise their most vulnerable and authentic work to date

By Ji-won Lee • April 6, 2026
5 min read

NewJeans has finally revealed the details of their much-anticipated comeback. The group announced during a surprise livestream that their new album "Tomorrow" will drop on May 15, 2026, accompanied by Hanni and Minji describing it as their "most personal album yet."

The announcement sent shockwaves through the K-pop community, with fans immediately flooding social media with excitement. The livestream, which was meant to be a casual Q&A session, turned into a major reveal as the members shared their excitement about the upcoming project.

A New Direction

Hanni spoke about the creative process behind "Tomorrow," explaining that this album represents a significant artistic evolution. "We've always wanted to make music that feels true to us, but this time we went even deeper. Every song has a piece of our hearts in it," she said.

Minji elaborated on the themes explored in the album, hinting at more personal lyrics than previous releases. "There are songs about growing up, facing challenges, and staying true to yourself. We hope our fans can see themselves in these stories," she added.

Visual Concept

The group also teased a completely new visual direction that breaks from their signature Y2K aesthetic. While keeping some nostalgic elements, "Tomorrow" will introduce fresh styling and concepts that reflect the members' growth as artists.

ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin praised the group's creative involvement, noting that the members had unprecedented input in both the music and visual direction. "They're not just performing the songs—they're co-creating their artistic identity," she said.

About NewJeans

NewJeans is a South Korean girl group formed by ADOR, known for their fresh Y2K-inspired concept and hits like "Attention," "Hype Boy," and "Super Shy."
