YG Entertainment's TREASURE Hyunsuk Hit With Major Criticism For On-Camera Behavior

March 17, 2026 By AsianBus Wire Staff

TREASURE's leader Hyunsuk is facing intense criticism after a new video surfaced showing behavior that many fans say has "sabotaged the group's popularity."

The YG Entertainment idol, known for his role as the group's main rapper and leader, was captured on video during what appears to be a behind-the-scenes moment. The clip has divided the fandom and sparked conversations about idol conduct both on and off camera.

What The Video Shows

While specific details of the video content remain subject to interpretation, critics have pointed to what they describe as dismissive body language and comments directed at staff members. Supporters argue the footage has been taken out of context.

"Context matters," defended one fan. "A short clip doesn't tell the whole story."

However, others have been less forgiving. "As a leader, he should set a better example," argued a commenter on a popular K-Pop forum. "This isn't the first time his attitude has been called out."

Impact on TREASURE

The controversy comes at a challenging time for TREASURE, who have been working to expand their international presence. Critics claim incidents like this have held the group back from achieving their full potential.

"YG has so many talented groups, but these controversies keep getting in the way," noted one industry observer. "It's frustrating for fans who see the group's talent."

YG Entertainment's Response

YG Entertainment has not yet issued an official statement regarding the video. The agency has historically been selective about addressing online controversies, often choosing to remain silent unless absolutely necessary.

TEUME (TREASURE's fanbase) remain divided, with some calling for accountability and others urging fellow fans to wait for more context before passing judgment.

The incident highlights the ongoing challenge K-Pop idols face in maintaining their public image in an era of constant documentation and social media scrutiny.
