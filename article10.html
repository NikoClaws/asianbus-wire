YG Entertainment's TREASURE Hyunsuk Hit With Major Criticism For His Behavior In New Video

March 17, 2026 By Andrew Lewter

TREASURE member Choi Hyunsuk has become the center of a growing controversy after a video from a recent fan meeting surfaced online, showing behavior that critics are calling disrespectful to both fans and fellow group members. The backlash has put YG Entertainment in a difficult position as the company weighs its response.

The video, which was recorded by an attendee at TREASURE's fan meeting event in Osaka, Japan last weekend, shows the 25-year-old rapper and leader during what appears to be a segment where members were supposed to perform individual talents. What exactly happened has become the subject of intense debate, with interpretations varying widely based on fan allegiances.

In the approximately 90-second clip that has now been viewed over 8 million times across various platforms, Hyunsuk can be seen making gestures and comments that some viewers have interpreted as dismissive toward a fan's question, while others argue he was simply joking in a manner consistent with his known personality.

What the Video Shows

The controversial segment began when a Japanese fan asked Hyunsuk, through a translator, about his creative process for writing rap lyrics. According to fan accounts, this should have been a straightforward question-and-answer moment typical of fan meeting events.

Instead, Hyunsuk's response—delivered partly in Korean with Japanese translation—appeared to some viewers as flippant and condescending. He reportedly made a comment that roughly translated to "You wouldn't understand anyway" before laughing and moving on to another topic without providing a substantive answer.

What has drawn additional criticism is Hyunsuk's apparent eye roll and dismissive hand wave captured in the video. These gestures, combined with his verbal response, have been characterized by critics as disrespectful to fans who paid significant money to attend the exclusive event.

"I was there. It was humiliating to watch. That fan was so excited to ask her question and he just... dismissed her. Like she wasn't worth his time. I've been a Teume since debut but this is making me reconsider everything." — Fan account from event attendee on Twitter

However, other attendees have offered different perspectives. Some argue that the video lacks crucial context, claiming that Hyunsuk had been joking with the same fan earlier in the event and that his response was part of an ongoing playful exchange that doesn't translate well in short clips.

The Fandom Divide

The controversy has created a significant rift within TREASURE's fandom, known as Teumes. Long-time fans who have followed Hyunsuk since his pre-debut days on the survival show YG Treasure Box have largely come to his defense, arguing that his personality has always included a sarcastic edge that new or casual fans might misinterpret.

"Anyone who actually knows Hyunsuk knows this is just how he jokes," wrote one defender on the fan community platform Weverse. "He's been like this for six years. The people criticizing him are either new fans or antis looking for reasons to hate."

On the other side, critics—including some self-identified long-time fans—argue that regardless of his personality, a leader and senior idol should know better than to behave dismissively toward paying fans, particularly at an international event where cultural misunderstandings are already a risk.

The debate has spilled over into discussions about idol behavior more broadly. Some commenters have pointed to this incident as evidence of what they call "privilege" among YG Entertainment artists, arguing that the agency's historically permissive approach to artist behavior has created a culture of entitlement.

YG Entertainment's Response

As of publication, YG Entertainment has not released an official statement regarding the controversy. This silence has frustrated both supporters and critics, though for different reasons.

Those defending Hyunsuk argue that a statement would dignify what they view as an overblown reaction to a minor moment. Those calling for accountability, however, point out that YG's silence allows the controversy to fester and grow, potentially causing more damage to both Hyunsuk's reputation and TREASURE's brand.

Industry observers note that YG Entertainment has historically taken a hands-off approach to managing artist controversies, often waiting for situations to resolve naturally rather than issuing statements. This approach has had mixed results over the years.

Impact on Group Activities

The timing of the controversy is particularly challenging for TREASURE, who are currently in the midst of their Japanese promotional cycle for their latest single. The group has several scheduled fan meeting and concert events in Japan over the coming weeks, raising questions about how the controversy might affect attendance and fan reception.

Some fans have announced on social media that they will be selling their tickets to upcoming events, though it remains unclear whether this represents a significant portion of ticket holders or a vocal minority.

Within the group dynamic, the incident has also raised questions about Hyunsuk's role as leader. K-Pop group leaders typically serve as the face of the group in formal settings and are expected to model appropriate behavior for younger members. Critics argue that the Osaka incident represents a failure of this leadership responsibility.

Broader Context: Idol Behavior Standards

The Hyunsuk controversy touches on ongoing debates within K-Pop fan culture about the standards to which idols should be held. Some argue that idols are entertainers who should be given latitude to express their personalities authentically, including moments of sarcasm or edginess.

Others counter that the idol-fan relationship involves an implicit contract: fans provide financial and emotional support, and in exchange, idols maintain a baseline level of respect and professionalism in their interactions. From this perspective, dismissive behavior toward fans represents a breach of that contract.

"The power dynamic is inherently unequal," explained cultural critic Lee So-young. "Fans travel long distances, spend significant money, and invest emotional energy into these interactions. When an idol appears to treat that investment as worthless, it's not just rude—it's a betrayal of the relationship that makes K-Pop function as an industry."

What Happens Next

The controversy shows no signs of immediate resolution. Hashtags calling for Hyunsuk to apologize continue to trend periodically on Korean and Japanese social media, while counter-hashtags defending him maintain equally strong momentum.

TREASURE's next scheduled public appearance is a television performance later this week. Whether Hyunsuk will address the controversy, whether he'll be present at all, and how fans will respond remain open questions.

For now, the incident serves as yet another reminder of the intense scrutiny under which K-Pop idols operate and the speed at which moments captured on camera can transform into full-blown controversies in the age of social media.
