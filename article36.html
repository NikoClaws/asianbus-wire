TWICE Jeongyeon Sparks Mistreatment Allegations After Candid Fan Conversation

March 18, 2026 By Kevin Park 📖 7 min read

TWICE member Jeongyeon has inadvertently ignited a firestorm of criticism against JYP Entertainment after candidly sharing her feelings about a mysteriously canceled schedule during a recent fan interaction. Her honest admission that she doesn't understand why "these things only happen to me" has galvanized ONCEs to speak out about what they perceive as years of mistreatment and neglect from the company, with the controversy highlighting long-simmering frustrations about the unequal treatment of TWICE members.

The conversation occurred during a fan event where an ONCE asked Jeongyeon directly why the company had canceled her schedule. Her response, while delivered with her characteristic self-deprecating humor, revealed genuine hurt and confusion that resonated deeply with fans who have watched her navigate various challenges throughout her career.

"I guess they don't like me LOL. I'm just kidding. I'll work harder," Jeongyeon initially joked before becoming more sincere. "I know what ONCEs are talking about, but I really don't know why these things only happen to me. But I don't think they did it on purpose."

The Canceled Schedule Mystery

The specific schedule that was canceled has been identified by fans as Jeongyeon's first and only brand deal in her 10+ year career with TWICE. For context, this distinction alone has been a sore point among fans, who note that other members have accumulated numerous individual endorsement deals over the years while Jeongyeon—despite her popularity and talent—has been seemingly overlooked.

The fact that even this rare opportunity was mishandled by the company has intensified existing frustrations. Fans argue that the cancelation represents a pattern rather than an isolated incident, pointing to what they see as years of evidence that JYPE has failed to properly support and promote Jeongyeon's individual activities.

"This is Jeongyeon's first and only brand deal in her 10+ years career btw and JYPE couldn't even arrange a schedule properly, yet we'll see these company stans saying Jeongyeon is not mistreated. Okay sure." — ONCE on social media

Jeongyeon's Journey: Context Matters

To understand why this incident has provoked such an intense reaction, it's important to understand Jeongyeon's journey with TWICE and the challenges she has overcome. The idol has been remarkably open about her struggles with anxiety and panic disorder, taking two separate hiatuses from group activities to focus on her mental and physical health.

Her return to full activities after her second hiatus was celebrated by fans as a triumph—proof of her determination and love for performing. That she "worked so hard so she could come back," as one fan put it, makes the company's apparent failure to support her all the more galling to supporters.

Throughout her career, Jeongyeon has also dealt with physical health issues, including a neck injury that affected her ability to perform choreography at full intensity. She has handled these challenges with grace and humor, earning admiration from fans who appreciate her authenticity and resilience.

The Inequality Question

The controversy has brought renewed attention to perceived inequalities in how JYPE manages the nine members of TWICE. While the group operates under a collective brand, individual members have had vastly different opportunities for solo activities, brand endorsements, and career development outside the group context.

Some members—particularly those perceived as more popular in certain markets—have accumulated substantial individual portfolios, including acting roles, variety show appearances, solo music releases, and lucrative endorsement deals. Others, including Jeongyeon, have had far fewer such opportunities, leading to questions about how these decisions are made and whether all members are being fairly supported.

Fans argue that these disparities aren't simply the result of market forces but reflect active choices by the company about which members to promote individually. The lack of transparency around these decisions has fueled speculation and resentment, with the current incident serving as a flashpoint for long-accumulated frustrations.

Fan Mobilization

In the wake of Jeongyeon's comments, ONCEs have organized across social media platforms to express their dissatisfaction with JYPE. Hashtags calling out the company's treatment of Jeongyeon have trended, while fan accounts have compiled evidence of what they characterize as a pattern of neglect.

"JYPE's treatment of Jeongyeon is f***ing disgraceful at this point," wrote one frustrated fan. "The fact that she doesn't even have an explanation for her own schedule is insane. How the hell do you treat your own artist like this and think it's acceptable?"

Others have taken a more measured approach, expressing frustration while acknowledging that the full story may be more complicated than it appears. Some fans have noted that Jeongyeon herself said she doesn't think the cancelation was intentional, suggesting there may be factors not visible to outside observers.

Jeongyeon's Emotional Wisdom

Perhaps the most poignant aspect of the situation has been Jeongyeon's mature response to her own disappointment. In her message to fans, she shared a perspective that reflects hard-won wisdom: "After living for 30 years, I've realized you just need to care about the people who like you."

She also addressed fans directly: "I'm really upset about it, too. Just like ONCE, I feel the same way. Let's just stay quiet! I'm so sorry and thankful that you all are upset because you like me so much! I will work harder!"

This response has been praised by fans as evidence of Jeongyeon's character—her ability to find gratitude and connection even in disappointing circumstances, and her instinct to comfort fans rather than dwelling on her own frustration. At the same time, some have argued that her graciousness is being taken advantage of by a company that faces no consequences for its failures.

JYPE's Silence

As of this writing, JYP Entertainment has not publicly responded to the controversy. This silence is consistent with the company's general approach to fan-driven criticism, which typically involves waiting for situations to cool down rather than engaging directly with complaints.

However, fans argue that this pattern of non-response is itself part of the problem. Without accountability or explanation, the same issues repeat, and artists like Jeongyeon are left to manage fan expectations and disappointment on their own. The lack of communication creates a vacuum that speculation and resentment fill.

Industry observers note that K-pop companies rarely win when they engage with fan criticism directly, as any response can be parsed, criticized, and weaponized. But this doesn't make the current situation sustainable, particularly as TWICE enters a new phase of their career following recent contract renewals.

The Bigger Picture

The Jeongyeon situation touches on broader questions about the K-pop industry's treatment of idols and the power dynamics between artists, companies, and fans. While fans have become increasingly vocal advocates for their favorite artists, their ability to effect actual change remains limited.

Companies control access to work opportunities, and idols who push back against company decisions risk being seen as difficult or ungrateful—labels that can damage careers. This power imbalance means that even when fans mobilize, the pressure they can exert is indirect and often ineffective.

For Jeongyeon, navigating this situation requires balancing her own legitimate frustrations against the practical realities of her career. Her choice to acknowledge her feelings while also defusing fan anger shows a sophisticated understanding of these dynamics—but it also raises the question of whether she should have to manage this burden at all.
