Kim Seon-ho and Go Youn-jung's "Can This Love Be Translated?" Dominates Korean Drama Charts

March 22, 2026 9 min read By Grace Kim

Lightning has struck twice for Kim Seon-ho. Following his career-defining role in "Hometown Cha-Cha-Cha" and his triumphant return in "When Life Gives You Tangerines," the beloved actor has once again captured the hearts of audiences worldwide with Netflix's "Can This Love Be Translated?"

The romantic comedy, which premiered on January 16, 2026, has dominated domestic buzz charts for three consecutive weeks, with both Kim Seon-ho and co-star Go Youn-jung leading cast rankings during the same period. The achievement marks another milestone in what has become one of K-drama's most celebrated comebacks.

Chart Dominance and Critical Acclaim

According to data from Good Data Corporation, "Can This Love Be Translated?" has maintained its number-one position in the integrated TV-OTT drama category for the third, fourth, and fifth weeks of January. The consistency of its performance demonstrates genuine viewer engagement rather than mere premiere curiosity.

Kim Seon-ho has simultaneously held the top position in cast buzzworthiness, with Go Youn-jung in second place—a remarkable achievement given the competitive landscape of early 2026 K-dramas. Their combined appeal has driven conversation across social media platforms and entertainment news outlets.

Critics have praised the drama's sophisticated approach to the romantic comedy genre. Writing for Harper's Bazaar Korea, entertainment journalist Lee Soo-jin noted: "What strikes most about this drama is its maturity. The humor lands because it emerges from character rather than contrivance, and the romance builds through genuine connection rather than manufactured obstacles."

The Story: Love Lost in Translation

"Can This Love Be Translated?" tells the story of Joo Ho-jin (Kim Seon-ho), a brilliant multilingual interpreter known for his professional precision and emotional reserve. His life takes an unexpected turn when he encounters Cha Mu-hee (Go Youn-jung), a rising actress whose career is just beginning to take off.

When Ho-jin is hired to work exclusively with Mu-hee for an international project in Japan, their contrasting personalities create friction—and, inevitably, attraction. The premise allows for exploration of communication, misunderstanding, and the ways that true connection transcends language barriers.

The Hong Sisters, known for their work on beloved dramas like "Hotel Del Luna" and "My Girlfriend is a Gumiho," penned the script. Their signature wit and emotional depth are evident throughout, though viewers have noted the drama takes a more grounded approach than some of their previous fantasy-infused works.

"Looking back, every moment felt like a journey," Kim Seon-ho shared in a recent interview. "It was a project that I found hard to let go of, to the point that my eyes welled up slightly on the final day of filming."

Chemistry That Sells

Central to the drama's success is the undeniable chemistry between Kim Seon-ho and Go Youn-jung. Their on-screen dynamic balances playful banter with genuine emotional depth, creating a romance that viewers find both entertaining and moving.

Go Youn-jung, whose previous work includes acclaimed performances in "Moving" and "Sweet Home," has demonstrated her range as Mu-hee—portraying a character who is publicly confident but privately vulnerable. Her scenes with Kim Seon-ho crackle with tension and tenderness in equal measure.

"Working with Go Youn-jung-ssi was a gift," Kim Seon-ho said. "She brings such honesty to her performance. It made my job easier because I could simply react to what she gave me."

The sentiment appears mutual. In separate interviews, Go Youn-jung has praised Kim Seon-ho's professionalism and commitment to creating authentic moments between their characters.

A Redemption Arc Complete

For Kim Seon-ho, the success of "Can This Love Be Translated?" represents the culmination of a remarkable professional redemption. After facing controversy in late 2021, the actor took time away from the spotlight before returning with careful, quality-focused project choices.

"When Life Gives You Tangerines" in 2025 signaled his return to leading man status, earning both ratings success and critical acclaim. "Can This Love Be Translated?" builds on that foundation, confirming that audiences have welcomed him back wholeheartedly.

Industry observers have noted how Kim Seon-ho's approach to his comeback—choosing meaningful projects, giving thoughtful interviews, and letting his work speak for itself—offers a template for navigating public challenges in the entertainment industry.

Global Appeal and Netflix Strategy

As a Netflix original, "Can This Love Be Translated?" was designed from inception for global audiences. The drama's international setting—spanning Korea and Japan—provides visual variety and cultural texture that appeals to viewers worldwide.

Netflix's global release strategy has paid dividends, with the drama ranking in the platform's Top 10 across multiple regions. The success validates Netflix's continued investment in Korean content following monster hits like "Squid Game" and "The Glory."

Japanese actor Sota Fukushi's supporting role adds another dimension, potentially driving viewership in Japan while contributing to the drama's international flavor. The casting choice reflects Netflix's understanding of how to build cross-regional appeal.

What Viewers Are Saying

Audience reactions have been overwhelmingly positive, with viewers praising everything from the writing to the performances to the production quality. Social media has been flooded with scene compilations, fan art, and enthusiastic recommendations.

"I didn't expect to cry this much watching a rom-com," one viewer posted on X. "Kim Seon-ho and Go Youn-jung have such natural chemistry. Every scene feels real."

The drama has also sparked discussion about language, communication, and cultural exchange—themes that resonate with international viewers who often experience K-dramas through subtitles. The meta-commentary on translation has not been lost on thoughtful viewers.

Some have noted that the marketing didn't fully capture the drama's emotional depth, with early promotional materials emphasizing lighter romantic comedy elements. Viewers who expected a breezy watch have found something more substantial.

Looking Ahead

With episodes still releasing, "Can This Love Be Translated?" has plenty of runway to maintain its dominance. Early success often predicts strong overall performance, and the drama appears positioned to become one of the defining K-dramas of early 2026.

For Kim Seon-ho and Go Youn-jung, the success opens doors to even more high-profile opportunities. Both are now firmly established as top-tier talents capable of carrying major productions—a status that seemed uncertain for Kim Seon-ho just a few years ago.

As one Reddit viewer summarized: "This has been extensively discussed—the marketing did not match the drama at all. It's so much better than anyone expected. Go watch it."

"Can This Love Be Translated?" is now streaming on Netflix with new episodes releasing weekly. The series stars Kim Seon-ho, Go Youn-jung, Sota Fukushi, Choi Woo-sung, and Lee Yi-dam.
