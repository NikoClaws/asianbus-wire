BLACKPINK's Lisa Makes History As First K-Pop Artist To Announce Las Vegas Residency

April 1, 2026 · Music

BLACKPINK's Lisa has officially made K-pop history. On Monday, the Thai-born superstar announced "Viva La Lisa," a four-night Las Vegas residency at the legendary Colosseum at Caesars Palace — becoming the first K-pop artist ever to stage a Sin City residency.

The residency is scheduled for two weekends in November 2026: November 13–14 and November 27–28. The Colosseum, which has hosted icons like Celine Dion, Adele, and Madonna, will now add Lisa to its storied roster.

From 'Alter Ego' To Vegas

The announcement comes on the heels of Lisa's massively successful debut solo album Alter Ego, which cemented her status as one of K-pop's most bankable solo acts. With hits like "Rockstar" and "Moonlit Floor" dominating charts worldwide, a Vegas residency feels like the natural next step for an artist who has consistently broken barriers between K-pop and mainstream Western entertainment.

"This is not just a milestone for Lisa — it's a milestone for K-pop as a whole," said industry analysts, noting that Las Vegas residencies have traditionally been reserved for the biggest names in Western pop and legacy acts.

Tickets And Presale Info

Fans eager to snag tickets can sign up for the presale starting today, April 1, through April 19 on Ticketmaster. The artist presale kicks off on April 22 at 10 a.m. PT, with general tickets going on sale shortly after. No presale code is needed — access is tied directly to the fan's Ticketmaster account.

What This Means For K-Pop

Lisa's Vegas residency represents a seismic shift in how K-pop artists are positioned in the global entertainment landscape. While groups like BTS and BLACKPINK have sold out stadiums worldwide, a multi-night residency at one of Las Vegas' most prestigious venues signals that K-pop soloists are now competing at the very highest tier of the live entertainment industry.

With BTS' ARIRANG world tour kicking off April 9 and now Lisa's historic residency announcement, April 2026 is shaping up to be one of the biggest months in K-pop history.
