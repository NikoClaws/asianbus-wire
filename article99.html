XO, Kitty Season 3 Drops On Netflix With Stacked K-Pop Soundtrack — BTS V, aespa, SEVENTEEN & More

April 2, 2026 4 min read By Mina Park

LOS ANGELES — Netflix's hit K-drama spinoff XO, Kitty is officially back for its third season, and it brought the entire K-pop industry along for the ride. The Jenny Han-created series, which follows Kitty Song Covey (Anna Cathcart) through her adventures at the Korean Independent Seoul School, premiered all eight episodes today — and the soundtrack reads like a who's who of K-pop royalty.

The headliners? BTS's V appears via his collaboration with UMI on "Wherever U R," while aespa's massive hit "Whiplash" soundtracks episode one. SEVENTEEN's THE8 contributes "Skyfall" in episode six, and ENHYPEN brings "Go Big or Go Home" to round out the A-list placements.

The biggest K-pop soundtrack yet

Season 3's music curation is easily the show's most ambitious yet. Beyond the marquee names, the soundtrack features NMIXX with "Soñar (Breaker)," NEXZ with "Slo-mo," Meovv with "Meow," and VCHA (Girlset) contributing two tracks including "Go Getter." Rising acts like ONE PACT, Wonho, and Peniel also make appearances across the eight-episode run.

The show hasn't forgotten its roots, either. Ashe's "Moral of the Story" — a staple from the original To All the Boys I've Loved Before films — makes a return in episode three, alongside new tracks from Fletcher, Cautious Clay, and Griff featuring Sigrid.

Senior year drama

Plot-wise, Kitty is back at KISS for her final year, determined to start senior year strong with a list of goals and intentional friend time. But things don't go according to plan — there's a school credit deficit to navigate, friend group drama to untangle, and her budding relationship with Min Ho (Sang Heon Lee) adds another layer of complexity to what's already an emotionally charged final semester.

The series, created by Jenny Han as a spinoff of her beloved To All the Boys I've Loved Before franchise, has become one of Netflix's most reliable bridges between Western and Korean pop culture — and the soundtrack choices reflect that crossover appeal perfectly.

K-pop's growing Netflix pipeline

The heavy K-pop presence in XO, Kitty's latest season reflects a broader trend: Netflix is increasingly leaning on K-pop to soundtrack its Korean and Korean-adjacent content. From Squid Game to All of Us Are Dead, the platform has recognized that K-pop fanbases drive massive engagement — and strategic soundtrack placements keep those communities talking (and streaming).

All eight episodes of XO, Kitty Season 3 are now streaming on Netflix worldwide.
