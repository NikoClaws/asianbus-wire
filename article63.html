BTS Makes History: 104,000 Fans Witness Triumphant Gwanghwamun Concert As "ARIRANG" Era Begins

March 22, 2026 12 min read By Andrew Lewter

The wait is over. BTS has officially returned. In what will be remembered as one of the most significant moments in K-pop history, approximately 104,000 fans gathered at Seoul's iconic Gwanghwamun Square on Saturday, March 21, 2026, to witness the world's biggest boy band take the stage together for the first time in nearly four years.

The free concert, titled "BTS THE COMEBACK LIVE | ARIRANG," marked the group's first full performance since their hiatus announcement in June 2022. Streamed live exclusively on Netflix to millions of viewers worldwide, the event transformed central Seoul into a global spectacle that transcended the boundaries of a typical K-pop concert.

A Nation Celebrates Its Prodigal Sons

The scale of the event was nothing short of extraordinary. According to HYBE, BTS's parent company, 104,000 fans packed into Gwanghwamun Square and the surrounding streets to witness the historic performance. The Seoul Metropolitan Government deployed approximately 6,700 police officers, while an additional 8,200 security personnel from the city government and HYBE managed crowd control.

"This is beyond what we anticipated," said Park Min-soo, a Seoul city official overseeing event coordination. "We prepared for a large gathering, but the emotional energy here today is something truly unique. You can feel how much this moment means to people."

The seven members—RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook—delivered a powerful setlist featuring tracks from their new album "ARIRANG" alongside career-spanning hits that had fans singing every word. The group's signature synchronization remained impeccable, silencing any concerns about their extended time apart.

The Setlist: A Journey Through Korean Identity

Opening with the title track "Arirang," the concert immediately established its thematic focus on Korean heritage and cultural identity. The traditional folk melody, reimagined through BTS's contemporary lens, created a powerful statement about the group's artistic evolution during their time away.

"Swim," the album's lead single, received its first live performance to rapturous applause. The introspective track, which explores themes of personal navigation and finding one's way, resonated deeply with fans who had journeyed through their own challenges during the hiatus.

The production incorporated stunning visual elements that merged traditional Korean aesthetics with cutting-edge technology. Giant LED screens displayed imagery of Korean landscapes, from the serene beauty of Gyeongbokgung Palace to the dynamic energy of modern Seoul, creating a narrative arc that celebrated the nation's past while embracing its future.

"We wanted this concert to be more than just a performance," RM explained during an emotional address to the crowd. "We wanted it to be a gift—to Korea, to ARMY, and to ourselves. This music carries our hearts, our roots, and our dreams for what comes next."

Global Viewership Breaks Records

Netflix's exclusive livestream attracted unprecedented viewership numbers, though official figures have not yet been released. Industry analysts estimate that concurrent viewership likely exceeded previous records for live-streamed concerts on the platform.

Social media engagement reached extraordinary levels throughout the event. #BTSComeback trended worldwide on X (formerly Twitter) for over 18 hours, while related hashtags including #ARIRANG, #BTSIsBack, and #GwanghwamunConcert dominated global trends across multiple platforms.

The economic impact extended beyond entertainment metrics. Hotels in Seoul reported near-capacity bookings, with international fans traveling from dozens of countries to witness the event in person. Local businesses in the Gwanghwamun area reported sales increases of 300-400% compared to typical weekends.

Emotional Moments Define the Night

Perhaps the most touching moment came during the performance of "Spring Day," BTS's beloved ballad about longing and reunion. As the first notes played, the crowd raised their ARMY Bombs—the group's official lightsticks—transforming Gwanghwamun Square into an ocean of purple light.

V, known for his emotional expressiveness, was visibly moved during the song, pausing momentarily to compose himself. The moment captured the profound significance of the reunion—not just for the fans, but for the members themselves.

"We missed you so much," Jimin told the crowd, his voice thick with emotion. "Every day, we thought about this moment. Every day, we worked to become better, to give you something worth waiting for. Thank you for never giving up on us."

Jin, who was the first member to begin his mandatory military service in December 2022, reflected on the journey: "When I started my service, I didn't know what would happen. But I knew one thing—I knew we would come back. That promise kept me going, and I hope knowing we would return kept you going too."

The "ARIRANG" Album: A Commercial Phenomenon

The concert comes just one day after the release of "ARIRANG," BTS's fifth Korean-language studio album. According to Big Hit Music, the album sold 3.98 million copies on its first day of release, setting a new record for 2026.

The 14-track album represents the group's most ambitious artistic statement to date, blending traditional Korean musical elements with contemporary pop production. Critics have praised the album's cohesive vision and its thoughtful exploration of identity, belonging, and cultural pride.

"ARIRANG is not just an album—it's a manifesto," wrote music critic Kim Yeong-dae in his review for The Korea Herald. "BTS has returned not simply to reclaim their throne, but to redefine what that throne means. This is mature, confident artistry from a group that has nothing left to prove but chooses to prove everything anyway."

Looking Ahead: The World Tour

Saturday's concert served as the opening statement for what promises to be BTS's most ambitious touring schedule ever. The "ARIRANG" world tour kicks off on April 9 in Seoul, with 82 stadium shows planned across 34 cities on five continents.

The North American leg alone includes 41 dates, with shows in Los Angeles, New York, Chicago, Houston, and numerous other cities. All 2.4 million North American tickets sold out within hours of going on sale earlier this month, generating estimated revenue exceeding $500 million for the U.S. dates alone.

Industry analysts project the tour's total economic impact could exceed $5 billion globally, accounting for ticket sales, merchandise, tourism, and ancillary spending. The figures underscore BTS's unparalleled position in the global entertainment landscape.

Fan Reactions: "Worth Every Second of Waiting"

For the fans who gathered in Seoul and those who watched from around the world, Saturday's concert represented the fulfillment of years of hope and patience. The BTS ARMY, known for their unwavering dedication, had maintained active support throughout the hiatus, streaming the group's existing catalog and supporting solo releases.

"I've been a fan since 2016," said Maria Gonzalez, 31, who traveled from Mexico City for the concert. "When they announced the hiatus, I cried. When they announced the comeback, I cried. And tonight, standing here, watching them perform—I can't stop crying. This is what we waited for."

Online, the emotional outpouring was equally intense. Fans shared stories of how BTS's music had helped them through difficult periods, from personal losses to the global pandemic. The concert became not just a celebration of the group's return, but a collective moment of joy for millions who found meaning in their music.

The Documentary Awaits

For those seeking deeper insight into BTS's reunion journey, Netflix will premiere "BTS: The Return" on March 27. The documentary chronicles the making of the "ARIRANG" album, featuring intimate footage of the members during their military service farewells, their solo activities, and the emotional process of reuniting as a group.

Early reviews from advance screenings describe the film as "essential viewing for understanding not just BTS, but the emotional landscape of K-pop's most devoted fandom." The documentary reportedly includes never-before-seen footage and candid conversations about the pressures of global fame and the importance of brotherhood.

A New Chapter Begins

As fireworks illuminated the sky above Gyeongbokgung Palace at the concert's conclusion, marking the end of an unforgettable evening, the message was clear: BTS's hiatus was never an ending—it was an intermission.

The group returns older, wiser, and more creatively ambitious than ever. Their music has matured, their perspectives have deepened, and their connection with fans has, if anything, grown stronger through absence.

"We're not the same BTS who left," RM reflected at the concert's close. "But we're still your BTS. We're still seven. And we're just getting started."

For the millions of fans who never stopped believing, who kept streaming and hoping and waiting, Saturday night proved that some things are worth the wait. BTS is back, and the "ARIRANG" era has officially begun.

The "ARIRANG" album is now available on all major streaming platforms. "BTS: The Return" documentary premieres on Netflix on March 27, 2026. For world tour dates and ticket information, visit the official HYBE/Big Hit Music website.
