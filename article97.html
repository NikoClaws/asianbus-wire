BTS ARIRANG Live Viewing Trailer Drops — Tour Hits Cinemas Worldwide April 11 & 18

April 2, 2026 4 min read By Mina Park

SEOUL — BTS just gave fans who couldn't score tour tickets a reason to celebrate. BIGHIT MUSIC has released the official trailer for BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG LIVE VIEWING, announcing two cinema broadcast events that will bring the group's highly anticipated comeback tour to theaters around the world.

The first live viewing is set for April 11, broadcasting from BTS's opening night in Goyang, South Korea — the very first stop of the ARIRANG World Tour. A second screening follows on April 18, capturing the Tokyo, Japan show. Tickets are already live at the official btsliveviewing website.

'History in the making'

"World's biggest band prepares a new world tour. History in the making," reads the trailer's opening text, setting a cinematic tone that matches the scale of the tour itself. The clip showcases each member — RM, Jin, SUGA, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook — preparing for their return to the stage, with individual color themes for each member on full display.

The trailer promises performances of BTS's biggest hits alongside new anthems from their fifth studio album ARIRANG, which debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 with lead single "SWIM" topping the Hot 100.

Deluxe album with hidden track drops April 3

Adding to the excitement, BTS confirmed during a recent celebratory livestream that a deluxe version of ARIRANG will drop on April 3 — just one day away. The deluxe LP includes a previously hidden track that appears to be produced by SUGA, giving fans yet another reason to tune in before the tour kicks off.

A tour of historic proportions

The ARIRANG World Tour spans 34 cities across more than 82 concerts, running from April 2026 through March 2027. Industry analysts project revenue that could rival or exceed Taylor Swift's record-setting Eras Tour, which grossed over $2 billion. With more show announcements still to come, the final tally could climb even higher.

For fans unable to attend in person, the live viewing events offer a theatrical experience that has become a staple of K-pop touring. BTS previously set cinema records with their Permission to Dance and Yet To Come live viewing events, and ARMY is already organizing group bookings for the ARIRANG screenings.

The ARIRANG World Tour begins April 9 in Goyang, with the first cinema broadcast following two days later on April 11.
