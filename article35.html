Cha Eunwoo's Netflix Drama Pushes Forward Despite $13.4 Million Tax Scandal

March 18, 2026 By Jessica Wong 📖 8 min read

Despite being embroiled in what has become the largest tax evasion scandal in Korean celebrity history, ASTRO member and actor Cha Eunwoo's upcoming Netflix series "The WONDERfools" will proceed with its originally scheduled release. According to reports from OSEN, the superpower comic action adventure will debut on May 15, 2026, following a production presentation on May 12—though Cha Eunwoo himself will not participate in promotional activities as he is currently serving his mandatory military service.

The decision to release the series as planned comes amid widespread speculation that Netflix might delay or shelve the project due to the controversy surrounding its male lead. The ₩20.0 billion KRW (approximately $13.4 million USD) tax deduction notification Cha Eunwoo received represents an unprecedented figure for a Korean celebrity, casting a long shadow over what was meant to be one of the year's most anticipated K-drama releases.

For co-star Park Eun Bin and the rest of the cast and crew, the decision to move forward likely brings relief after months of uncertainty about the project's fate. However, questions remain about how the scandal will affect viewer reception and whether promotional efforts can effectively work around Cha Eunwoo's absence and controversy.

The WONDERfools: What We Know

"The WONDERfools" is set in 1999, during a period when apocalyptic sentiments were widespread as the new millennium approached. The story follows a group of ordinary locals who unexpectedly gain superpowers and must band together to fight villains threatening the peace of the fictional Haesung City.

The series comes with impressive creative credentials. Director Yoo In Sik, known for the critically acclaimed "Extraordinary Attorney Woo," helms the project, while Heo Da Joong, who adapted the hit film "Extreme Job," serves as writer. This pedigree had generated significant anticipation for the series before the tax scandal emerged.

Park Eun Bin leads the cast as Eun Chaeni, an ordinary woman who suddenly gains superpowers after becoming entangled in unexpected events. Cha Eunwoo plays Lee Woon Jeong, a special public servant of Haesung City who approaches a series of mysterious disappearances with suspicion. The supporting cast includes respected actors Kim Hae Sook, Choi Dae Hoon, Lim Sung Jae, and Son Hyun Joo.

The Tax Scandal Explained

The controversy stems from a tax investigation that began before Cha Eunwoo's military enlistment. The Seoul Regional Tax Office's Fourth Investigation Bureau initially targeted Fantagio, Cha Eunwoo's agency, but the investigation uncovered additional concerns involving a corporation operated by Cha Eunwoo's mother—specifically an eel restaurant identified as "Corporation A" in reports.

The resulting tax notifications were staggering in scale. Fantagio was notified of an ₩8.20 billion KRW (approximately $5.50 million USD) tax deduction, while Corporation A including Cha Eunwoo personally was hit with the ₩20.0 billion KRW figure. Combined, this represents a historic level of additional tax assessment for anyone in the Korean entertainment industry.

Reports indicate that Cha Eunwoo has hired a major law firm to respond to the situation and has filed a pre-assessment administrative review challenging the tax office's decision. The legal process is ongoing, meaning the final resolution—and the full truth about what occurred—remains uncertain.

"This is the largest amount of additional tax ever imposed on a celebrity to date. The scale of the allegations has shocked the industry." — Entertainment industry analyst on the tax case

Netflix's Calculated Gamble

Netflix's decision to proceed with "The WONDERfools" represents a calculated business decision that weighs multiple factors. Shelving or delaying the series would mean writing off the significant investment already made in production, affecting not just Netflix's balance sheet but also the careers and compensation of everyone involved in making the show.

The streaming giant also appears to be betting that viewers can separate the actor's personal controversies from the entertainment value of the content. This approach has precedent in both Korean and international entertainment, where projects have proceeded despite star scandals—sometimes successfully, sometimes not.

However, the timing creates complications. With Cha Eunwoo serving in the military, he cannot participate in promotional activities regardless of the scandal. This absence means promotional efforts will center on Park Eun Bin and the supporting cast, potentially allowing the show to be marketed in a way that de-emphasizes the controversial star.

Park Eun Bin's Position

For Park Eun Bin, who emerged as one of Korea's most acclaimed actresses following "Extraordinary Attorney Woo," the situation is particularly delicate. She signed on to "The WONDERfools" as a follow-up project that would showcase her range in a genre different from her previous work—only to find the production overshadowed by her co-star's scandal.

Park Eun Bin's team has not publicly commented on the situation, which is the typical approach when one star's controversy doesn't directly involve another. At the production presentation on May 12, she and the other attending cast members will likely face questions about Cha Eunwoo, requiring careful navigation to promote their own work without being drawn into commentary on the scandal.

Industry observers note that Park Eun Bin's star power may actually help insulate the project from the worst effects of the controversy. Her substantial fanbase and critical acclaim provide alternative drawing power that could sustain viewer interest even among those troubled by Cha Eunwoo's situation.

Industry Implications

The Cha Eunwoo case has broader implications for the Korean entertainment industry, particularly regarding the financial structures that have allowed such situations to develop. The involvement of family business entities and the massive scale of alleged tax irregularities raise questions about how celebrity finances are managed and supervised.

A recent report revealed staggering figures relating to entertainment agency tax audits, suggesting that Cha Eunwoo's case, while extreme, may be part of a larger pattern of financial irregularities in the industry. Increased scrutiny from tax authorities could reshape how agencies and celebrities structure their business affairs going forward.

For production companies and streaming platforms, the case also highlights the risks associated with depending heavily on individual stars whose personal behavior or business practices could create liability. While cancel culture operates differently in Korea than in Western markets, the financial and reputational risks remain significant.

Fan Reactions: Divided Loyalties

Cha Eunwoo's fans, known for their devoted support, find themselves in a difficult position. Some have rallied behind him, arguing that he deserves the presumption of innocence until the legal process concludes and noting that tax disputes often involve complex interpretation questions rather than clear-cut wrongdoing.

Others have expressed disappointment and confusion, struggling to reconcile the clean-cut image that made Cha Eunwoo famous with the serious allegations now surrounding him. The scale of the alleged tax issues—potentially involving family members and deliberate structures to minimize tax obligations—is difficult to dismiss as simple misunderstanding or accounting error.

International fans, who may be less familiar with Korean tax law and social expectations, have had varied reactions ranging from supportive to confused to critical. The case has sparked discussions about celebrity accountability and the expectations fans should have for the private behavior of public figures.

What Happens Next

The May 15 release of "The WONDERfools" will serve as a real-world test of whether star scandals can be overcome by quality content and alternative star power. Viewer numbers and critical reception will provide data on how Korean and international audiences respond to entertainment featuring controversial figures.

Meanwhile, Cha Eunwoo's legal challenge to the tax assessment will continue working through the administrative review process. Depending on the outcome, he could face significant financial obligations, potential legal consequences, or vindication if his challenge succeeds.

His military service, while providing a natural period of low profile, also means he cannot actively work to rehabilitate his image through new projects or public appearances. By the time he completes his service, the public's memory of the scandal will have had time to either fade or solidify, depending on how events unfold.

For now, "The WONDERfools" stands as an unusual artifact—a major production releasing under the cloud of its male lead's historic scandal, asking audiences to invest in a story while knowing that one of its central figures faces serious real-world allegations. How viewers respond may shape industry approaches to similar situations for years to come.
