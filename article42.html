ZEROBASEONE's Zhang Hao, Ricky, Gyuvin & Yujin To Debut As NDOUBLE In May

March 19, 2026 By Selena Jung

The post-ZEROBASEONE era is officially beginning to take shape. On March 18, multiple Korean media outlets reported that four members of the popular project group—Zhang Hao, Ricky, Kim Gyu Vin, and Han Yu Jin—are preparing to debut in a new permanent group named NDOUBLE (앤더블) on May 26, 2026. The news comes as ZEROBASEONE's two-and-a-half-year project contract approaches its June expiration, confirming what many fans had speculated: that some members would continue together under new formations.

NDOUBLE, which appears to combine "N" (new/next) with "Double," represents a significant development in the increasingly common practice of project group members regrouping after their temporary contracts end. The quartet will reportedly remain under WAKEONE Entertainment, the company that managed ZEROBASEONE throughout their activities.

The Members

Zhang Hao: The Chinese member who captured hearts as ZEROBASEONE's main vocalist and center, Zhang Hao finished first in the "Boys Planet" survival show that formed the group. His powerful vocals and charismatic presence made him a standout throughout ZB1's activities. Fans have long hoped he would continue in K-Pop rather than returning to the Chinese market.

Ricky: Another Chinese member, Ricky (born Ricky Wang) ranked fourth in Boys Planet and served as one of ZB1's visual members and lead dancers. His sophisticated image and dance skills, combined with his growing popularity in both Korea and China, make him a valuable asset for the new group.

Kim Gyu Vin: The tall, versatile performer ranked sixth in Boys Planet and quickly became known for his humor, charm, and all-rounder abilities. As a Korean member, Gyu Vin provides important domestic market connection and variety show appeal.

Han Yu Jin: Rounding out the quartet, Han Yu Jin ranked seventh in Boys Planet and impressed with his powerful vocals and dance skills. His energetic personality and strong performance abilities complement the other members well.

Why This Combination?

Industry observers note that the NDOUBLE lineup represents a strategic blend of strengths. The inclusion of two Chinese members (Zhang Hao and Ricky) positions the group for strong performance in the lucrative Chinese market, while the two Korean members (Gyu Vin and Yu Jin) ensure solid domestic appeal and Korean variety show compatibility.

Additionally, the four members showed particularly strong chemistry during ZEROBASEONE activities, often interacting in fan content and live broadcasts. Their established rapport should translate into natural group dynamics from debut.

The choice to form a four-member group also follows current industry trends, with smaller groups often finding it easier to achieve line distribution balance and develop distinct member identities compared to larger formations.

"The four members have expressed strong desire to continue activities together. NDOUBLE will allow them to build on the foundation established with ZEROBASEONE while developing their own distinct identity." — Industry source speaking to Korean media

What About The Other Members?

ZEROBASEONE's remaining five members—Sung Han Bin, Kim Ji Woong, Park Gun Wook, Kim Tae Rae, and Seok Matthew—have not announced specific post-ZB1 plans. Sung Han Bin, who ranked second in Boys Planet and served as the group's leader, is expected to pursue solo activities or potentially join a different group formation.

Kim Ji Woong, the eldest member, has reportedly been in discussions regarding acting opportunities, while the younger members are expected to continue in the music industry through various paths. WAKEONE has indicated it will support all members in their future endeavors, whether together or separately.

The Project Group Phenomenon

NDOUBLE's formation highlights the evolution of the project group model in K-Pop. Groups formed through survival shows typically operate under fixed-term contracts—usually two to two-and-a-half years—after which members return to their original agencies or become free agents.

This model has created a complex web of post-group activities. Previous project groups like I.O.I and Wanna One saw members scatter across numerous agencies, while more recent groups like IZ*ONE had several members continue together in new formations. The success of these successor groups has been mixed, with some achieving significant popularity while others struggled to recapture their project group momentum.

ZEROBASEONE achieved substantial success during their active period, selling millions of albums and building a dedicated fanbase called "ZEROSE." Whether NDOUBLE can maintain this momentum will be a key test of the regrouping model's viability.

May 26 Debut

The reported May 26 debut date gives NDOUBLE approximately two months after ZEROBASEONE's expected final activities in late March or April. This timeline suggests preparation has been ongoing for some time, with WAKEONE working to ensure a smooth transition from one group to the next.

The debut date also positions NDOUBLE's first release during what is traditionally an active period for K-Pop comebacks, though they will face significant competition from established acts. Their existing fanbase provides a strong foundation, but converting ZB1 fans into NDOUBLE supporters will be crucial.

Fan Reactions

ZEROSE have responded to the news with mixed emotions. Many express excitement about the continuation of their favorite members' careers together, while others feel bittersweet about the end of ZEROBASEONE as a nine-member group.

"I'm happy for them but also sad that ZB1 is really ending," wrote one fan. "At least I can still support Zhang Hao, Ricky, Gyu Vin, and Yu Jin together."

Others have begun speculating about NDOUBLE's concept and sound, with many hoping the group will have creative input in shaping their musical direction—something project group members often lack due to the pre-packaged nature of survival show groups.

Looking Ahead

NDOUBLE's success will depend on multiple factors: the quality of their debut music, their ability to develop a distinct identity separate from ZEROBASEONE, and the sustained support of fans navigating the transition. The group enters the market with significant advantages—proven chemistry, established fan recognition, and agency backing—but also faces the challenge of proving they can thrive outside the project group framework.

For Zhang Hao, Ricky, Kim Gyu Vin, and Han Yu Jin, NDOUBLE represents not an ending but a new beginning. After achieving success through the grueling survival show process and subsequent project group activities, they now have the opportunity to build something more permanent—a group identity that is truly their own.

WAKEONE Entertainment is expected to officially confirm NDOUBLE details in the coming weeks. AsianBus Wire will provide updates as the debut approaches.
