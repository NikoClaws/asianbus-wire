RIIZE Fan Gets Robbed And Assaulted By Locals During Lollapalooza Chile Concert

March 17, 2026 By Andrew Lewter

A deeply disturbing incident has shaken the K-pop community after a RIIZE fan was violently robbed and physically assaulted by local individuals during the group's performance at Lollapalooza Chile. The attack, which occurred in the festival grounds, has raised serious questions about safety measures at international K-pop events and sparked outrage among fans worldwide.

The victim, a 23-year-old Korean national who had traveled to Santiago specifically to see RIIZE perform, was targeted while waiting in the crowd area before the group took the stage. According to witness accounts and the victim's own social media posts, she was approached by multiple individuals who forcibly took her belongings and physically attacked her when she resisted.

The incident occurred on the second day of Lollapalooza Chile, one of South America's largest music festivals. RIIZE, SM Entertainment's rookie boy group, was performing as part of the festival's international lineup, drawing thousands of dedicated fans from across the region and beyond.

The Victim's Account

In a series of emotional social media posts that have since gone viral, the victim described the terrifying ordeal in detail. "I was standing in the crowd, just excited to see RIIZE, when suddenly I felt someone grab my bag," she wrote. "When I tried to hold onto it, they started hitting me. There were at least three or four of them."

The attackers allegedly stole the victim's phone, wallet, passport, and official RIIZE merchandise she had purchased. More disturbingly, she reported suffering physical injuries including bruises on her arms and face, and a cut on her hand that required medical attention.

"I couldn't even enjoy the concert after that," she continued. "I was in shock, bleeding, and scared. The security guards eventually helped me, but by then everything was gone and the people who did it had disappeared into the crowd."

"I saved money for months to make this trip. I just wanted to see my favorite group perform live. I never imagined something like this could happen. I'm devastated and traumatized." — The victim, in her social media statement

The victim was treated at a medical tent on the festival grounds and later filed a police report with Chilean authorities. She has since returned to Korea but reports ongoing emotional distress from the incident.

Festival Response And Security Concerns

Lollapalooza Chile organizers released a statement expressing regret over the incident while emphasizing their commitment to attendee safety. "We are deeply troubled to learn of this incident and are cooperating fully with local law enforcement," the statement read. "The safety of our festival guests is our top priority, and we are reviewing our security protocols in light of this event."

However, many fans and observers have criticized the festival's security measures as inadequate. Reports suggest that the area where the attack occurred had minimal security presence, and several other attendees have come forward with accounts of theft and harassment during the event.

"This isn't an isolated incident," wrote one Chilean fan on social media. "There have been problems with theft and violence at local festivals for years. International fans who come for K-pop acts are often targeted because they're perceived as easy marks—tourists carrying expensive phones and merchandise who don't know the area."

The incident has also renewed discussions about the specific vulnerabilities faced by K-pop fans at international events. Unlike dedicated K-pop concerts with their own security infrastructure, festival appearances place fans in environments where they may be unfamiliar with local conditions and where security is spread thin across large venues.

K-Pop Community Response

The global K-pop community has rallied around the victim in a remarkable display of solidarity. A crowdfunding campaign launched by fellow RIIZE fans to help cover her medical expenses and replace stolen items raised over $15,000 USD within 24 hours. Fans have also organized efforts to contact SM Entertainment and Lollapalooza organizers demanding improved security measures.

The hashtag #JusticeForBRIIZE (combining "BRIIZE," the RIIZE fandom name, with the call for justice) has trended in multiple countries, with thousands of fans sharing the victim's story and demanding accountability.

Other K-pop fandom communities have expressed solidarity as well. Fans of groups like Stray Kids, ATEEZ, and NCT—all of which have performed at international festivals—shared their own safety concerns and tips for attending events in unfamiliar locations.

"This could have been any of us," wrote one fan. "We travel across the world to see our favorite artists, often alone or in small groups. We need to look out for each other and demand better protection at these events."

SM Entertainment's Position

SM Entertainment, which manages RIIZE, has acknowledged the incident through an unofficial statement, expressing concern for the victim's wellbeing. However, the agency has stopped short of making any official public comment or addressing questions about artist safety protocols at international events.

Sources familiar with the situation suggest that SM Entertainment is privately reviewing its approach to festival appearances, potentially reconsidering future bookings at events where security concerns exist. The company has previously faced criticism for booking artists at venues with documented safety issues.

RIIZE members themselves have not publicly addressed the incident, though fans have noted that member Anton's recent social media activity included posts that some interpreted as subtle acknowledgments of the situation. However, this remains speculative.

Broader Safety Implications

The incident has highlighted ongoing concerns about safety at K-pop events worldwide. In recent years, there have been numerous reports of fans being injured, harassed, or robbed at concerts and fan events across various countries. The international nature of K-pop fandom means fans often travel long distances to unfamiliar locations, potentially increasing their vulnerability.

Industry observers have called for more systematic approaches to fan safety, including better communication between K-pop agencies and event organizers, improved security protocols specifically designed for international events, and resources for fans traveling abroad.

"K-pop is a global phenomenon now, but the infrastructure for protecting international fans hasn't kept pace," explained entertainment industry safety consultant Maria Rodriguez. "Agencies, event organizers, and local authorities need to work together to ensure that fans can enjoy events safely, regardless of where they're held."

Chilean Authorities Investigation

Chilean police have confirmed they are investigating the incident, though progress has been slow due to the chaotic nature of the festival environment and the difficulty of identifying suspects. Authorities have reviewed security camera footage from the area and are asking any witnesses to come forward.

Local advocacy groups have offered support to the victim and have criticized what they describe as insufficient police presence at major events. "This is a recurring problem," said a spokesperson for a Chilean victims' rights organization. "Festivals attract large crowds and, unfortunately, criminals who prey on vulnerable attendees. More needs to be done to prevent these incidents."

Moving Forward

As the K-pop community processes this traumatic event, many are calling for systemic changes to how international events are organized and secured. Petitions demanding improved safety measures have gathered thousands of signatures, and fan communities are compiling safety guides for those attending upcoming festivals.

For the victim, the road to recovery—both physical and emotional—continues. Her case has become a rallying point for discussions about fan safety, and she has expressed hope that sharing her story will help prevent similar incidents in the future.

"I don't want anyone else to go through what I experienced," she wrote in a follow-up post. "K-pop is supposed to bring joy. We should be able to see our favorite artists without fearing for our safety."
