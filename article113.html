BLACKPINK Announces Official Comeback Date After Rosé's Solo Success

YG Entertainment confirms summer return with new album and world tour announcement

By Ji-won Lee • April 9, 2026
6 min read

BLACKPINK is officially making their highly anticipated return. YG Entertainment confirmed the group's comeback date during a special press event on April 9, 2026, marking their first full album release since 2022.

The announcement comes on the heels of Rosé's successful solo debut, which broke records across multiple streaming platforms and topped charts in over 30 countries. Her accomplishment seems to have energized the group's own comeback preparations.

The Comeback Timeline

YG Entertainment revealed that BLACKPINK will release their new album on July 15, 2026, with a full world tour to follow. The album, titled "RUBY x DIAMOND," marks the group's first major release since their record-breaking "BORN PINK" era in 2022.

Industry analysts suggest the timing is strategic, positioning BLACKPINK to dominate the summer concert season and capitalize on strong interest from international markets.

What We Know About the Album

While YG has kept specific details under wraps, sources close to the group indicate the album will feature a mix of hip-hop influenced tracks and ballad-style songs. The group reportedly had significant creative input on the album's direction.

Rosé's Solo Success Paved the Way

Rosé's debut solo album "ROSIE" has spent three consecutive weeks in the top 5 of the Billboard 200, a rare achievement for a K-pop solo artist's debut release. The album's lead single "broken diamonds" became her most successful solo effort to date.

YG Entertainment is expected to release additional details about the album's tracklist and the tour's specific cities in the coming weeks.

About BLACKPINK

BLACKPINK is a South Korean girl group formed by YG Entertainment, consisting of Jisoo, Jennie, Rosé, and Lisa. They are one of the most successful K-pop groups internationally.
