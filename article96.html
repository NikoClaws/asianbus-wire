BTS '2.0' MV Sparks Plagiarism Debate As Fans Compare Visuals To Stray Kids

April 2, 2026 4 min read By Mina Park

SEOUL — BTS's new "2.0" music video has barely been out 24 hours and it's already at the center of K-pop's latest fandom war. The Oldboy-inspired visual, released April 1 as the second ARIRANG music video, has drawn plagiarism accusations from Stray Kids fans — and a fierce defense from ARMY that's gone viral across social media.

The controversy centers on the video's dark corridor fight scenes and gritty aesthetic, which some fans of Stray Kids (known as STAYs) argue bears too close a resemblance to Stray Kids' own music video concepts. Side-by-side comparison clips quickly racked up millions of views on X (formerly Twitter), with accusations flying in both directions.

ARMY fires back: 'Danger and Boy In Luv exist'

BTS fans were quick to shut down the plagiarism claims by pointing to the group's own extensive catalog of dark, aggressive concepts. "Danger and Boy In Luv exist," became a rallying cry, referencing BTS's 2014 music videos that featured similar gritty aesthetics years before the comparisons being cited.

ARMY also emphasized that the "2.0" video is an explicit homage to Park Chan-wook's iconic 2003 film Oldboy, not a reference to any other K-pop group. The corridor fight choreography mirrors the legendary one-take hallway sequence from the film — a cinematic touchstone that predates both groups' careers.

Billboard confirmed the Oldboy connection in its own coverage, noting that rather than fighting with a hammer like the film's protagonist, "BTS employ their epic dance skills and music to ward off their opponents."

A pattern in K-pop fan culture

Inter-fandom plagiarism debates are nothing new in K-pop. Groups frequently draw from the same cinematic, fashion, and artistic references, leading to accusations that often say more about fan rivalry than actual creative theft. Dark corridor concepts, in particular, are a staple of the genre — used by everyone from EXO to ATEEZ to NCT.

'2.0' continues ARIRANG's record-breaking run

The controversy hasn't slowed "2.0" or ARIRANG's momentum. The album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200, while lead single "SWIM" topped the Hot 100. All 14 tracks from ARIRANG simultaneously charted on Spotify's Global Daily chart, with "2.0" itself debuting at No. 50 on the Hot 100.

BTS is set to kick off their massive ARIRANG World Tour on April 9 in Goyang, South Korea. The tour spans 34 regions with more than 82 shows scheduled through 2027 — a scale that industry analysts say could rival Taylor Swift's record-breaking Eras Tour.

Neither BIGHIT MUSIC nor JYP Entertainment (Stray Kids' label) have commented on the plagiarism debate.
