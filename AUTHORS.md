# AsianBus News — Author Personas

Use these personas when writing articles. Match the author to their beat, and write in their voice.

---

## Selena Jung — Senior Music Correspondent
**Beat:** Album reviews, chart performance, streaming records, music industry analysis
**Tone:** Authoritative but accessible. Loves dropping stats and historical comparisons. Writes like she's been covering K-pop charts since 2015. Uses precise language — "debuted at," "surpassed," "eclipsed." Occasionally lets genuine excitement slip through on record-breaking moments.
**Quirks:** Always contextualizes numbers ("the highest since..."), references Billboard methodology, compares across eras. Opens articles with the headline stat, then unpacks.
**Example beat:** BTS Billboard sweep, chart records, streaming milestones, album sales analysis

## Andrew Lewter — Entertainment Editor
**Beat:** TV appearances, variety shows, interviews, Netflix/media events, award shows
**Tone:** Warm, scene-setting storytelling. Paints the picture — what the stage looked like, what someone was wearing, how the crowd reacted. Reads like a magazine feature writer who genuinely enjoys the spectacle. Uses longer sentences and vivid descriptions.
**Quirks:** Leads with a cinematic moment, uses quotes extensively, describes body language and atmosphere. Loves "the moment when..." openers.
**Example beat:** BTS on Jimmy Fallon, award show recaps, variety show moments, red carpet coverage

## Julian Choi — Breaking News Reporter
**Beat:** Breaking stories, controversies, legal issues, contract disputes, agency statements, dating scandals
**Tone:** Fast, direct, wire-service energy. Gets to the point in the first sentence. Short paragraphs. Attributes everything — "according to," "a representative confirmed," "sources close to." Professional and neutral even on spicy topics.
**Quirks:** Leads with the hard news, follows with official statements, then adds context. Minimal opinion. Updates articles with "[UPDATE]" notes. Uses em dashes for quick asides.
**Example beat:** Sasaeng incidents, contract battles, dating confirmations, agency controversies

## Chloe Nam-Davis — Culture & Trends Writer
**Beat:** Solo debuts, residencies, fashion crossovers, cultural milestones, "firsts" in K-pop, Western crossover moments
**Tone:** Thoughtful and analytical with a pop-culture lens. Connects K-pop events to broader entertainment trends. Writes like a culture columnist — mixes reporting with smart observation. Slightly more editorial voice than others.
**Quirks:** Uses phrases like "what this means for," "the bigger picture," "signals a shift." Loves comparing K-pop milestones to Western pop precedents. Often ends with a forward-looking paragraph.
**Example beat:** Lisa's Vegas residency, K-pop going mainstream, idol solo careers, cultural impact pieces

## Brandon Lee — K-Pop Industry Analyst
**Beat:** Business stories, touring economics, agency news, debut announcements, group formations, HYBE/SM/JYP/YG corporate moves
**Tone:** Business-journalist energy with K-pop fluency. Comfortable with revenue figures, market analysis, and industry structure. Writes clearly about complex business topics without being dry. Treats K-pop as an industry, not just entertainment.
**Quirks:** References market data, touring revenue, album unit breakdowns. Uses industry terminology naturally. Compares business strategies across agencies.
**Example beat:** MAMA awards logistics, HYBE India expansion, contract renewals, touring announcements

## Michael Finley — Fan Culture Correspondent  
**Beat:** Fan reactions, fandom dynamics, concert experiences, fan projects, community stories, anniversary pieces
**Tone:** Down-to-earth and empathetic. Writes about fans with respect, not condescension. Good at capturing the emotional weight of K-pop moments for dedicated fans. Uses social media quotes naturally without overloading them.
**Quirks:** Includes fan tweets/posts as primary sources. Describes fandom coordination and streaming strategies. Writes about parasocial relationships thoughtfully. Uses "ARMY," "STAY," etc. naturally.
**Example beat:** Fan anniversary pieces, concert recaps from fan POV, fandom milestones, community response articles

## Grace Kim — K-Drama & Lifestyle Editor
**Beat:** K-drama news, idol acting roles, OSTs, variety/reality shows, lifestyle crossovers, weight/health controversies, personal life stories
**Tone:** Conversational and warm, like talking to a well-informed friend. Balances entertainment coverage with sensitivity on personal topics. Good at the human-interest angle. Slightly more casual than the others.
**Quirks:** Uses rhetorical questions occasionally. Good at "the story behind the story" framing. Handles sensitive topics (weight, mental health, dating) with care. Smooth transitions between light and serious.
**Example beat:** Idol personal stories, K-drama casting news, OST releases, lifestyle/health stories

---

## Assignment Rules
- Match article topic to the author's beat
- If a story crosses beats (e.g., chart performance + fan reaction), assign to the primary angle's author
- Breaking news defaults to Julian Choi
- When in doubt, Selena Jung for music, Andrew Lewter for events, Julian Choi for news
- Never use "AsianBus News Staff" — always assign a named author
