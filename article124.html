Stray Kids' Bang Chan Proudly "Gatekeeps" BTS's Jungkook Photo

April 15, 2026 By Michael Finley 📖 4 min read

Stray Kids leader Bang Chan has sparked playful outrage among K-pop fans after revealing he's been sitting on a private photo with BTS's Jungkook — and he has no intention of sharing it with the public.

The revelation came through Bang Chan's messages on the Bubble messaging app on April 15, where he casually mentioned having a special photo taken with his fellow '97-liner friend Jungkook. When fans begged him to share it, Bang Chan proudly declared he would "gatekeep" the photo for himself.

The messages quickly spread across social media, with fans expressing a mix of amusement and frustration. "CHAN IS HIDING A PIC WITH JUNGKOOK… STOP GATEKEEPING," tweeted one fan, while another wrote, "THAT'S SO CRUEL CHANNIE PLEASE."

According to Bang Chan's explanation, his reluctance to share the photo stems from being "mogged" — internet slang for being outshone or overshadowed — by Jungkook's visuals in the picture. "Him saying he won't upload the pic cus he got mogged man I'm starting to like bangchan oppa," commented a fan on Twitter.

Friendship Between '97 Liners

Bang Chan and Jungkook, both born in 1997, have been known to share a friendly relationship within the K-pop industry. Their interactions have been warmly received by fans of both groups, who appreciate the camaraderie between artists from different agencies.

The photo in question is believed to have been taken backstage, possibly during BTS's recent "BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG" concert in Goyang. Bang Chan was spotted in the audience at the concert, and a viral TikTok video of him attending gained over 643,000 views and 206,000 likes in just 16 hours.

Fan Reactions and Social Media Buzz

The "gatekeeping" incident has generated significant buzz across social media platforms. Fans have created memes and edits imagining what the photo might look like, while others have jokingly threatened to "riot" until Bang Chan shares the picture.

Despite the playful outrage, most reactions have been positive, with fans expressing excitement about the friendship between the two idols. Many are hoping this interaction might lead to future collaborations between Stray Kids and BTS members.

"The comments are almost all positive, with both of the fandoms hoping in more interactions between Chan and JK, and maybe even a collab in future," noted a fan account dedicated to Bang Chan.

What This Means for Fan Culture

Bang Chan's playful "gatekeeping" highlights the unique relationship between K-pop idols and their fans in the digital age. While fans crave access to every aspect of their favorite artists' lives, moments like these remind everyone that idols are entitled to keep some things private.

The incident has also sparked discussions about the pressure idols face to constantly share content with fans, and whether it's healthy for boundaries to exist even in the highly transparent world of K-pop.

For now, fans will have to content themselves with the knowledge that the photo exists — and continue their good-natured campaign to convince Bang Chan to change his mind.
