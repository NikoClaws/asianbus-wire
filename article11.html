NCT's Taeyong Accused Of Misogyny And Body Shaming, Sparking Fierce Responses

March 17, 2026 By Julian Choi 📖 7 min read

NCT's long-standing leader Lee Taeyong finds himself at the center of another intense controversy after alleged screenshots surfaced online appearing to show the idol making misogynistic comments and body shaming remarks. The allegations have ignited a firestorm of responses from both his dedicated fanbase and vocal critics, reigniting debates about accountability in the K-Pop industry.

The controversy erupted late Tuesday evening when a Korean online community user posted what they claimed were screenshots from a private conversation allegedly involving Taeyong. The images, which quickly spread across social media platforms including Twitter, Weibo, and various Korean forums, appeared to show disparaging comments about women's bodies and what critics have labeled as deeply problematic views about female appearance standards.

Within hours of the initial post, hashtags related to the controversy began trending in South Korea and internationally. The phrase "Taeyong Controversy" topped Twitter's worldwide trending topics, while Korean portal sites saw the story climb to the top of real-time search rankings. The response has been swift, polarizing, and intensely emotional from all sides.

The Alleged Comments

According to the circulating screenshots, the conversation allegedly took place on a private messaging platform sometime in late 2025. The messages purportedly show someone identified as Taeyong making comments rating women's appearances, criticizing body types that don't conform to certain standards, and using language that many have described as dehumanizing toward women.

One particularly controversial excerpt allegedly states opinions about women's weight that sparked immediate outrage from body positivity advocates and feminist groups in South Korea. Another passage supposedly contains comments about female idols from other groups, comparing their physical appearances in what critics call a degrading manner.

It's important to note that neither SM Entertainment nor Taeyong himself have confirmed the authenticity of these screenshots. Questions have been raised about potential manipulation or fabrication, with some fans pointing to inconsistencies they claim prove the images are doctored.

NCTzens Rally To Defend Their Leader

NCTzens, NCT's devoted fanbase, have mobilized rapidly in defense of Taeyong. Many fans have launched their own investigations into the screenshots, creating detailed threads analyzing font types, timestamp formats, and other technical elements they believe prove the images are fake.

"We've seen this playbook before with Taeyong. Every time he's about to have a major comeback or achievement, these 'revelations' conveniently appear. The timing is always suspicious, and the 'evidence' never holds up to scrutiny." — @ProtectTaeyong, fan account with 450K followers

Supporters have pointed to Taeyong's history of dealing with false accusations, including the well-documented bullying allegations from his trainee days that he later received a court ruling in his favor for. They argue this latest controversy follows a similar pattern of unverified claims being spread to damage his reputation.

Fan-organized streaming parties and support hashtags have been running continuously since the controversy broke, with #WeTrustTaeyong and #TaeyongIsInnocent trends attempting to counter the negative coverage. Donation drives to fund potential legal action against the original poster have already raised significant sums.

Critics Demand Accountability

On the opposing side, critics argue that the K-Pop industry has a history of dismissing legitimate concerns about idol behavior and that fans reflexively defending artists regardless of evidence is part of a broader problematic pattern.

Korean feminist groups have called for a thorough investigation, stating that the alleged comments, if authentic, represent harmful attitudes that contribute to the objectification and mistreatment of women. Several prominent Korean influencers and activists have shared the screenshots with critical commentary, urging followers not to dismiss the allegations without proper examination.

"Whether these specific screenshots are real or not, we need to have a conversation about how male idols talk about women when they think no one is listening. The industry has protected problematic behavior for too long." — Kim Yuna, women's rights activist

Some former fans have announced they will no longer support Taeyong until the situation is clarified, sharing emotional posts about their disappointment and conflict. The hashtag #TaeyongApologize has also gained traction, though at significantly lower volumes than the supportive tags.

SM Entertainment's Response

SM Entertainment, NCT's management agency, released a brief statement approximately 18 hours after the controversy began. The statement, which many have criticized as inadequate, read: "We are aware of the circulating claims and are currently reviewing the situation. We ask that people refrain from spreading unverified information."

The statement did not directly address whether the screenshots were authentic or fabricated, nor did it indicate any timeline for a more comprehensive response. Industry observers note this measured approach is typical of major K-Pop agencies dealing with scandals, though critics argue it leaves the public in an information vacuum that allows speculation to flourish.

Legal experts suggest that if SM Entertainment believes the screenshots are fabricated, they could pursue defamation charges against the original poster and those spreading the content. However, no such action has been announced as of publication.

A History of Controversy

This is not the first time Taeyong has faced serious allegations. In 2017, he was accused of being a school bully during his middle school years, allegations that led to years of legal battles. In 2020, the Seoul Western District Court ruled that the initial accuser had spread false information, resulting in a criminal conviction for defamation against the accuser.

Supporters argue this history proves Taeyong is a target for malicious attacks, while critics contend that his past controversies suggest a pattern of concerning behavior. The polarized nature of these discussions reflects broader tensions within K-Pop fandom culture around accountability and the presumption of innocence.

Impact on NCT's Activities

The timing of the controversy is particularly significant as NCT is currently preparing for a major comeback scheduled for April. Industry insiders report that SM Entertainment is closely monitoring public sentiment to determine whether any adjustments to promotional plans are necessary.

Taeyong's scheduled appearance at an upcoming fashion event has been quietly removed from the brand's promotional materials, though it remains unclear whether this represents a cancellation or simply a precautionary measure. The brand has not responded to requests for comment.

NCT's other members have remained silent on the controversy, maintaining their regular social media activity without acknowledging the situation—a common approach in K-Pop when individual members face scandals.

The Broader Conversation

Beyond the specifics of this case, the controversy has reignited discussions about several issues within the K-Pop industry: the treatment of women, the culture of idol worship that can shield problematic behavior, the weaponization of social media for both attacks and defense, and the difficulty of establishing truth in an era of easily manipulated digital evidence.

Mental health advocates have also raised concerns about the impact of such intense public scrutiny on all parties involved, regardless of the ultimate truth of the allegations. The stress of being at the center of a viral controversy, whether as accused or accuser, can have severe psychological consequences.

As the K-Pop industry continues its global expansion, these controversies increasingly play out on an international stage, with fans and critics from dozens of countries participating in real-time debates. The resolution of this particular situation—whatever form it takes—will likely set precedents for how similar controversies are handled in the future.
