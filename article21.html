Lee Dong Hwi Cuts Ties With Troubled Best Friend WINNER Mino After Drug Scandal

March 17, 2026 By Julian Choi 📖 7 min read

In a development that has shocked fans of both celebrities, actor Lee Dong Hwi has reportedly severed all ties with his longtime best friend Song Mino, better known as WINNER's Mino, following the rapper's highly publicized drug scandal. The two had been inseparable for nearly a decade, making this public fallout particularly devastating for fans who had long celebrated their brotherhood.

According to multiple sources close to both celebrities, Lee Dong Hwi made the difficult decision to distance himself from Mino after the WINNER member was charged with habitual marijuana use earlier this year. The "Reply 1988" star reportedly feels betrayed by his friend's choices and is concerned about the potential damage to his own carefully cultivated public image.

"Dong Hwi was absolutely devastated when he learned the full extent of the situation," an industry insider revealed to local media. "He had no idea what Mino was involved in, and he felt blindsided. For someone who has worked so hard to build a reputation as a reliable, professional actor, this association became too dangerous to maintain."

A Friendship That Defined An Era

Lee Dong Hwi and Mino's friendship became the stuff of K-entertainment legend over the years. The two met in 2014 through mutual acquaintances in the entertainment industry and quickly became inseparable. Their bromance was frequently documented on variety shows, social media, and in countless paparazzi photos showing them hanging out together.

The pair were so close that they were often referred to as "soulmates" by fans, who created dedicated social media accounts to celebrate their friendship. They vacationed together, appeared on each other's content, and were regularly spotted at the same restaurants and clubs in Gangnam and Itaewon.

Mino even mentioned Lee Dong Hwi in multiple interviews as someone who helped him through difficult times in his career, particularly during WINNER's extended hiatus period. "Dong Hwi hyung is like a real brother to me," Mino said in a 2021 interview. "He's someone I can call at 3 AM when I'm struggling, and he'll always pick up."

"Their friendship was one of the most genuine celebrity friendships we'd seen in K-entertainment. To see it end like this is heartbreaking for fans who watched them grow together over the years." — K-entertainment analyst Park Soo Jin

The Drug Scandal That Changed Everything

Mino's troubles began when he was investigated by the Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency's drug crime investigation unit in late 2025. The investigation revealed that the WINNER rapper had allegedly been using marijuana on multiple occasions over an extended period, with some reports suggesting the habit dated back several years.

The scandal sent shockwaves through the K-pop industry, which maintains an extremely strict zero-tolerance policy toward drug use. In South Korea, marijuana is classified as a narcotic and carries severe legal penalties, including imprisonment. For celebrities, drug charges typically result in immediate career destruction and social ostracism.

YG Entertainment, which manages WINNER, initially released a brief statement saying they would cooperate fully with the investigation. However, as more details emerged, the agency announced an indefinite hiatus for group activities, and several brands quickly terminated their endorsement deals with Mino.

The rapper himself has remained largely silent since the scandal broke, releasing only a brief handwritten apology through his agency in which he expressed remorse for "disappointing fans and causing concern." He did not specifically address the drug allegations in his statement.

Lee Dong Hwi's Careful Distancing

Sources indicate that Lee Dong Hwi began distancing himself from Mino almost immediately after the investigation became public knowledge. The actor reportedly stopped responding to Mino's messages and declined several attempts by the rapper to meet in person.

"Dong Hwi feels terrible about the situation, but he also has to think about his own career," a source close to the actor explained. "He's worked incredibly hard to get where he is, and he can't risk being associated with a drug scandal. In Korea, these things can destroy careers by association."

The actor's agency, Key East Entertainment, has not officially commented on the friendship status but has been reportedly advising Lee Dong Hwi to maintain his distance until the situation is fully resolved. This is a common strategy in the Korean entertainment industry, where guilt by association can have severe consequences.

Fans of Lee Dong Hwi have largely supported his decision, understanding the precarious nature of celebrity careers in South Korea. However, some have criticized him for "abandoning" his friend during his darkest hour, arguing that true friendship should transcend professional concerns.

The Broader Impact On Their Social Circle

The fallout from Mino's scandal has reportedly affected a wider circle of celebrity friendships. Several other stars who were known to be close to the WINNER member have similarly distanced themselves, creating a chilling effect throughout the industry.

P.O from Block B, another close friend of Mino's, has noticeably stopped mentioning the rapper in public appearances. Similarly, actor Kang Ha Neul, who had previously spoken warmly about their friendship, has remained silent on the matter.

Industry observers note that this type of social distancing is unfortunately common in the Korean entertainment industry when scandals break. The collectivist nature of Korean society means that association with scandal can taint even innocent parties, making distancing a matter of professional survival rather than personal choice.

What This Means For WINNER

The scandal has raised serious questions about WINNER's future as a group. The YG Entertainment boy group, which debuted in 2014 through the survival show "WIN: Who Is Next," has already been operating with reduced membership following the departure of Nam Taehyun in 2016.

With Mino on indefinite hiatus and facing potential legal consequences, the remaining members—Seungyoon, Jinwoo, and Seunghoon—face an uncertain path forward. Industry analysts speculate that the group may effectively disband, though no official announcement has been made.

YG Entertainment has been notably quiet about WINNER's future plans, leading many to believe that the company is waiting to see how Mino's legal situation resolves before making any decisions. In the meantime, the remaining members have been focusing on individual activities.

A Cautionary Tale For Celebrity Friendships

The dissolution of Lee Dong Hwi and Mino's friendship serves as a sobering reminder of the fragile nature of celebrity relationships in the Korean entertainment industry. What appeared to be an unbreakable bond has been severed by circumstances beyond anyone's control, illustrating how quickly fortunes can change in the world of K-entertainment.

For fans who had invested emotionally in the two celebrities' friendship, the situation is particularly painful. Social media has been flooded with nostalgic posts remembering happier times, while others express anger at both parties—Mino for his alleged actions and Lee Dong Hwi for his perceived abandonment.

As of press time, neither Lee Dong Hwi nor Mino has publicly addressed the status of their friendship. Whether this distance is permanent or merely a temporary measure remains to be seen, but for now, one of K-entertainment's most beloved friendships appears to have reached its end.
