SM Entertainment Issues Urgent Warning After SMTR25 Trainees Face Dangerous Sasaeng Incidents

The agency reveals trainees have experienced unauthorized entry into their residence, physical harassment, and individuals forcibly entering their taxis — all before they've even debuted.

SM Entertainment has released a serious official statement addressing escalating safety concerns involving their pre-debut trainees in the SMTR25 program. The announcement, made on March 25, 2026, describes a disturbing pattern of invasive behavior that has left the young trainees in significant distress.

According to the statement posted on the official SMTR25 social media accounts, trainees have recently experienced alarming incidents including unauthorized entry into their dormitory, excessive and unwanted physical contact from individuals following them, and most disturbingly, people forcibly entering taxis alongside the trainees.

Pre-Debut Nightmare

What makes these incidents particularly concerning is that the SMTR25 trainees have not yet officially debuted. The program, launched in early 2025, was designed to introduce promising trainees to the public before their official group debut later this year. The 15 trainees currently participating in the "Reply High School" variety show have been building a fanbase through social media and live performances at the SMTOWN LIVE 2025 tour.

However, this visibility has apparently attracted individuals engaging in extreme "sasaeng" behavior — a term referring to obsessive fans who invade celebrities' privacy and personal space. The fact that these trainees are experiencing such harassment before even debuting has shocked many in the K-pop community.

Incidents Reported by SM Entertainment:

• Unauthorized entry into the trainees' dormitory residence

• Excessive physical contact and touching

• Individuals forcibly boarding taxis with trainees

• Shouting trainees' names in their residential building

• Trespassing into restricted areas

• Creating disturbances affecting trainees and nearby residents

SM Entertainment's Response

In their statement, SM Entertainment acknowledged that warnings and additional security measures have already been implemented, but the problematic behavior has continued unabated. The agency made clear that they will now pursue "strong legal action without leniency" if the invasive behavior persists.

"We would like to express our sincere gratitude to all fans who continue to show deep support and affection for SMTR25. We are issuing this statement to address a series of ongoing incidents involving serious invasions of SMTR25 members' privacy."

— SMTR25 Official Statement

The company emphasized that protecting the safety and privacy of their trainees remains their "top priority" and that they have been working to create a secure environment for the young artists to train and develop.

Who Are SMTR25?

SMTR25 represents SM Entertainment's next generation of male artists. The program currently features talented trainees including Hanbi, Songha, Nicholas, Daniel, Kassho, Justin, Hyunjun, Haruta, Hamin, Kachin, and Tata. Several of these trainees previously participated in NCT Universe: LASTART and gained recognition during SMTOWN LIVE 2025.

The trainees range in age from their late teens to early twenties, with nationalities spanning Korean, Japanese, and Korean-American. They have been building anticipation for their debut through the "Reply High School" variety show, which divides the trainees into themed classes representing different decades of K-pop history.

A Growing Industry Problem

Sasaeng behavior has long been a serious issue in the K-pop industry, with numerous agencies having to take legal action against obsessive individuals who cross boundaries. However, the targeting of pre-debut trainees represents a concerning escalation. These young artists are still developing their careers and may be more vulnerable to the psychological impact of such invasive behavior.

Industry observers note that the rise of social media and pre-debut content has created earlier and more intense connections between aspiring idols and their potential fanbases. While this can build valuable support, it also exposes trainees to unwanted attention before they have the established support systems that debuted artists typically have.

Fan Community Response

The statement has sparked widespread discussion among K-pop fans, with many expressing support for SM Entertainment's decision to take a firm stance. Fans of the SMTR25 trainees have been sharing the agency's statement and calling on others to respect the trainees' boundaries.

Many have pointed out that true support means respecting artists' personal space and privacy, regardless of how much one admires them. The conversation has also reignited broader discussions about fan culture in K-pop and the need for healthier boundaries between artists and their audiences.

Looking Ahead

SM Entertainment has confirmed that at least one boy group featuring SMTR25 members will debut in 2026. The agency has announced partnerships with Warner Music Japan, Universal Music Group Thailand (through True), and Tencent Music Entertainment for Chinese promotions, suggesting ambitious international expansion plans for the new group.

For now, the focus remains on ensuring the trainees can continue their development in a safe environment. SM Entertainment's statement serves as both a warning to those engaging in problematic behavior and a reassurance to fans that the company takes the wellbeing of their artists seriously.

The K-pop industry will be watching to see how effectively these measures protect the young trainees as they prepare for what should be an exciting debut — one that deserves to be celebrated without the shadow of harassment and invasion of privacy.
