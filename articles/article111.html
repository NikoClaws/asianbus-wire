BTS' 'Arirang' Makes History with Second Week at Billboard 200 #1

April 6, 2026 · AsianBus Wire

BTS has done it again. The K-pop supergroup's fifth studio album Arirang has become the first K-pop act in history to top the U.S. Billboard 200 albums chart for two consecutive weeks.

Billboard confirmed Sunday (local time) that "Arirang" held the top spot for a second straight week, beating out Ye's "Bully" and Melanie Martinez's "Hades" in a stacked chart. The album earned 187,000 equivalent album units in the week ending April 2 — down 71 percent from its explosive opening week of 641,000 units, but still enough to rule the chart.

BTS' "Arirang" becomes the first K-pop album ever to spend two consecutive weeks at #1 on the Billboard 200.

Of those 187,000 units, 114,000 came from physical and digital album sales, while streaming equivalent albums (SEA) accounted for 65,000. Released March 20, "Arirang" explores BTS's identity as a Korean group and the universal emotions the members have encountered throughout their journey.

The achievement adds to what has been a monumental year for the group. Last week, lead single "Swim" also debuted at #1 on the Billboard Hot 100, making BTS the first group in chart history to debut both an album and a single at the top of their respective charts in the same week.

"Arirang" is BTS's seventh album to debut at #1 on the Billboard 200, following "Love Yourself: Tear," "Love Yourself: Answer," "Map of the Soul: Persona," "Map of the Soul: 7," "Be," and the anthology album "Proof."
