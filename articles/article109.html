Stray Kids Face International Backlash Over State Luncheon Appearance

April 5, 2026 · AsianBus Wire

On April 3, 2026, the K-pop world was set ablaze not by a new music video or a world tour announcement, but by a series of photographs from a state-organized event in Seoul.

Stray Kids found themselves at the center of a heated international controversy after attending a prestigious state luncheon at the Blue House, organized to celebrate the 140th anniversary of diplomatic relations between South Korea and France. The event drew together high-ranking government officials and select cultural icons — with Stray Kids as the only K-pop group invited.

Social media was quickly flooded with images of members Hyunjin and Felix smiling alongside French President Emmanuel Macron and First Lady Brigitte Macron. While some fans initially celebrated the group's prestige and role as cultural ambassadors, the mood shifted rapidly.

"Seeing them using our Kids to clear their disgusting reputation is a big 'no' to me and shouldn't be celebrated at any cost." — fan reaction on social media

French "STAYs" (the group's fanbase) began voicing deep discomfort over the appearance, citing the current French political climate and what they describe as "regressive politics" regarding Islamophobia and women's rights under the Macron administration. For these fans, the presence of Stray Kids served to legitimize a political agenda they strongly oppose.

Others directly referenced the First Lady's past comments regarding feminism and women's rights protests in France, adding fuel to the growing backlash. The incident has reignited debates about how K-pop groups navigate geopolitical complexities as their global influence continues to grow.

Stray Kids and their label have not publicly commented on the controversy. The group is currently preparing for upcoming release activities, with their next scheduled project details still under wraps.
