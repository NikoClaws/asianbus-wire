BTS 'ARIRANG' Officially Surpasses 1 Billion Streams on Spotify

April 5, 2026 · AsianBus Wire

BTS has achieved what no K-pop group — and no Asian act — has ever done before. Their album ARIRANG has officially surpassed 1 billion streams on Spotify, marking a historic milestone for Korean pop music on the global stage.

The achievement, confirmed on April 5, 2026, places BTS among an elite group of global artists — but as the first K-pop group and first Asian act ever to hit this mark. In just over two weeks since release, the album has become the 6th fastest album to reach 1 billion streams overall, sitting behind only Taylor Swift and Bad Bunny albums in that historic company.

ARIRANG debuted simultaneously at #1 on the Billboard 200, #1 on the Billboard Hot 100, and #1 on Spotify's Global chart — a historic triple crown that no Korean act had ever achieved before. The album dropped on March 20, 2026, after the group spent nearly four years on hiatus due to mandatory military service.

The record-breaking album is now propelling the group into their next chapter. The BTS World Tour ARIRANG is set to kick off April 11 in Goyang, South Korea, with additional dates expected to be announced globally.

ARMY worldwide continues to celebrate what fans are calling "a moment decades in the making" — BTS not only returning from military service stronger than ever, but rewriting the record books in the process.
