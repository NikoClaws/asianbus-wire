SEVENTEEN's Vernon and The8 Announce Groundbreaking Sub-Unit: Album Set for June 2026

March 22, 2026 10 min read By Brandon Lee

CARATs have a new summer destination to anticipate. Pledis Entertainment has officially confirmed that SEVENTEEN members Vernon and The8 will debut as a new sub-unit, with their album scheduled for release in June 2026. The announcement marks SEVENTEEN's sixth official sub-unit and one of the most unexpected pairings in the group's decade-long history.

The confirmation came on March 12, 2026, sending fans into a frenzy of excitement. According to Pledis, both artists are deeply involved in the production of the upcoming album, promising a release that reflects their distinct artistic visions and the unique chemistry between them.

An Unexpected But Perfect Pairing

At first glance, Vernon and The8 might seem like an unconventional combination. Vernon, a Korean-American rapper known for his cool demeanor and hip-hop-influenced style, contrasts with The8, a Chinese member celebrated for his elegant contemporary dance background and artistic sensibilities.

Yet those who follow SEVENTEEN closely understand why this pairing makes perfect sense. Both members share a deep interest in art, fashion, and self-expression beyond music. Their individual aesthetic choices—Vernon's street style meets high fashion approach and The8's avant-garde minimalism—suggest a visually striking collaboration.

"Vernon and The8 have discussed working together for years," a source close to the group revealed. "They have a creative wavelength that might not be immediately obvious, but once you see what they're creating together, it makes complete sense."

Allure Korea Feature Teases Concept

Ahead of the official announcement, Vernon and The8 graced the cover of Allure Korea's April 2026 issue, offering fans their first glimpse of the sub-unit's visual direction. The pictorial showcased both members in bold, artistic styling that merged streetwear elements with high fashion sensibilities.

The accompanying interview provided insight into the sub-unit's creative process. "We wanted to create something that couldn't exist without both of us," The8 explained. "Our styles are different, but that's what makes it interesting. We're not trying to meet in the middle—we're creating something new from our differences."

Vernon echoed the sentiment: "The8-hyung and I have always respected each other's artistic vision. This project gave us the chance to really explore that. The album isn't about compromise—it's about collision, in the best way."

The photoshoot's natural, freckled makeup and deliberately disheveled styling suggested a raw, authentic concept that diverges from typical K-pop polish—fitting for two members known for their artistic independence.

Creative Control at the Forefront

What distinguishes this sub-unit from many K-pop projects is the level of creative control granted to Vernon and The8. According to Pledis Entertainment, both members are involved in every aspect of the album's production, from songwriting and composition to visual concept and choreography design.

This approach aligns with SEVENTEEN's broader reputation as a "self-producing" idol group. The group has long been celebrated for members' involvement in creating their music, and the Vernon-The8 sub-unit appears to take that philosophy to new heights.

The8 has previously released solo work in Chinese, including the EP "STARDUST," which showcased his artistic range. Notably, Vernon contributed to that project, suggesting the two have already established a productive collaborative dynamic.

"Working on The8-hyung's Chinese EP was eye-opening," Vernon shared in a previous interview. "I learned so much about different production approaches. Now, bringing that experience into our own project feels natural."

SEVENTEEN's Sub-Unit Tradition

The Vernon-The8 unit becomes SEVENTEEN's sixth official sub-unit, joining a lineage that has produced some of the group's most beloved music. Previous units include the hip-hop unit (S.Coups, Wonwoo, Mingyu, Vernon), vocal unit (Woozi, Jeonghan, Joshua, DK, Seungkwan), and performance unit (Hoshi, Jun, The8, Dino).

More recently, BooSeokSoon (BSS)—comprising DK, Seungkwan, and Hoshi—released their debut mini album "Serenade" in January 2026 to commercial success. The album debuted at number three on the Circle Album Chart, demonstrating strong appetite for SEVENTEEN sub-unit content.

Additionally, DK x Seungkwan released their unit debut earlier in 2026, further expanding the range of sub-unit combinations. Each project has showcased different facets of the group's versatility, and expectations are high for what Vernon and The8 will bring.

Musical Direction: What to Expect

While specific details about the upcoming album remain under wraps, industry observers have speculated about potential musical directions based on both members' artistic histories and stated preferences.

Vernon's solo work and featured tracks have leaned into alternative hip-hop and R&B-influenced production. His lyrics often explore introspective themes with a laid-back delivery that contrasts with more aggressive rap styles common in K-pop.

The8, meanwhile, has demonstrated versatility spanning contemporary R&B, Chinese traditional music fusion, and experimental pop. His Chinese releases have received praise for their mature, artistic approach that prioritizes atmosphere over conventional hooks.

"I imagine something genre-defying," speculated music critic Hwang Ji-young. "Neither Vernon nor The8 is interested in following trends. They're both artists who want to create something that feels true to them. The result could be unlike anything in K-pop right now."

CARAT Response: Excitement and Anticipation

The announcement has generated tremendous excitement within CARAT, SEVENTEEN's dedicated fanbase. Social media exploded with reactions ranging from surprise at the unexpected pairing to enthusiastic predictions about the musical direction.

"Vernon and The8?? I didn't know I needed this but now I can't think about anything else," one fan posted. "Their aesthetics are so different but complementary. The visuals alone are going to be incredible."

Fan communities have begun analyzing past interactions between the two members, compiling evidence of their artistic chemistry. Clips of them discussing art, fashion, and music have resurfaced, reframed as foreshadowing of this collaboration.

The June release date positions the sub-unit debut as a summer highlight, arriving during a typically competitive period in K-pop. The timing suggests confidence in the project's appeal and quality.

What This Means for SEVENTEEN

The sub-unit announcement comes during a busy period for SEVENTEEN. Following DK and Seungkwan's unit debut and BSS's comeback, the group has maintained a steady stream of content even as some members prepare for military service obligations in the coming years.

Sub-units serve multiple purposes within K-pop's ecosystem. They allow artists to explore creative directions that might not fit the full group's concept, keep the fandom engaged during gaps between full-group releases, and demonstrate the individual talents that might otherwise be overshadowed in a 13-member formation.

For Vernon and The8 specifically, the sub-unit offers an opportunity to showcase their artistry to audiences who might not have fully appreciated their contributions within SEVENTEEN's larger context.

As June approaches, anticipation will only build. If the Allure Korea preview is any indication, Vernon and The8 are preparing something that transcends typical K-pop releases—an artistic statement from two members who have always marched to their own beat.

More details about the Vernon x The8 sub-unit album will be announced by Pledis Entertainment in the coming weeks. Stay tuned for updates on release date, concept, and promotional activities.
