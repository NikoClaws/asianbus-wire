BLACKPINK Jisoo's 'Boyfriend on Demand' Claims #1 Global Spot On Netflix

March 20, 2026 8 min read By AsianBus News Staff

BLACKPINK's Jisoo has achieved her biggest acting milestone yet. Her Netflix romantic comedy "Boyfriend on Demand" has claimed the #1 spot on the platform's Global Non-English Series chart for the second consecutive week, amassing over 25.6 million hours watched in its first three days alone and reaching the Top 10 in 34 countries.

The 10-episode series, which premiered on March 6, 2026, follows Mi-rae (played by Jisoo), an exhausted webtoon producer who finds romance through a virtual dating subscription service. Co-starring veteran K-drama actor Seo In-guk, the show has captured audiences with its escapist premise and romantic chemistry—even as debate continues about Jisoo's acting abilities.

Record-Breaking Numbers

According to Netflix's official data, "Boyfriend on Demand" has dominated viewership metrics since its release:

  • First 3 Days: 25.6 million hours watched, 2.6 million views
  • Week 1: Debuted at #4 on Global Top 10, reached Top 10 in 34 countries
  • Week 2: Climbed to #1 globally, accumulated additional millions of viewing hours

The series has performed particularly well in Southeast Asia, Japan, and Latin America—regions where BLACKPINK enjoys enormous popularity. However, it's also broken into Western markets, charting in the United States, United Kingdom, and several European countries.

The Virtual Romance Premise

Directed by Kim Jung-sik and written by Namgung Do-young, "Boyfriend on Demand" offers a contemporary take on romantic fantasy. Mi-rae, worn down by the harsh realities of dating, subscribes to a cutting-edge virtual reality dating service that promises the boyfriend of her dreams.

The twist? Her virtual perfect match begins to blur the lines between digital fantasy and reality—especially when she encounters Kyeong-nam (Seo In-guk), a "frosty" co-worker who seems eerily similar to her virtual boyfriend.

"Imagine coming home from a long day of work to immerse yourself in a virtual reality world with the boyfriend of your dreams. That's the (virtual) reality that Mi-rae finds herself living," The Hollywood Reporter wrote in their review.

The supporting cast includes Yoo In-na and Ha Young, adding depth to Mi-rae's workplace and personal dynamics.

The Acting Debate Continues

Despite commercial success, "Boyfriend on Demand" has reignited the perennial debate about idol actors—specifically whether K-pop stars can deliver compelling dramatic performances.

Critics have been divided. While some praise Jisoo's growth since her 2022 debut in "Snowdrop," others argue she lacks the range expected of a leading actress.

"Jisoo's screen presence is undeniable, and her chemistry with Seo In-guk carries the lighter moments beautifully. But the more emotionally demanding scenes reveal limitations," wrote one Korean critic.

Supporters counter that the romantic comedy genre plays to Jisoo's strengths—charm, charisma, and visual appeal—without demanding the dramatic heavy lifting that might expose weaknesses.

BLINK Response

BLINKs have rallied around the series, organizing streaming parties and social media campaigns to boost its numbers. Many fans have celebrated the achievement as validation of Jisoo's multi-talented profile.

"#1 globally. Two weeks in a row. But please, tell me more about how Jisoo can't act. The numbers speak for themselves."

"Proud of Seo Mi-rae. Proud of Jisoo. This is her era."

Fan accounts have also highlighted Jisoo's rigorous preparation for the role, including dialect coaching and consultation with webtoon industry professionals to authentically portray Mi-rae's career struggles.

Seo In-guk's Return to Rom-Com

For Seo In-guk, "Boyfriend on Demand" represents a return to the romantic comedy genre that launched his acting career with hits like "Reply 1997" and "The Smile Has Left Your Eyes." His performance as the initially cold Kyeong-nam has drawn praise from critics and viewers alike.

In interviews, Seo described his chemistry with Jisoo as "surprisingly natural," adding that her musical background informed her sense of timing and emotional pacing.

Netflix's K-Drama Strategy

"Boyfriend on Demand" is part of Netflix's aggressive expansion into Korean content, which has become a cornerstone of the platform's global strategy. Following the success of "Squid Game," "The Glory," and other Korean originals, Netflix has invested billions in Korean productions.

The casting of BLACKPINK's Jisoo represents a strategic play to capture K-pop fandom—a demographic known for passionate engagement and global reach.

What's Next for Jisoo

Industry insiders report that multiple offers have flooded Jisoo's agency following "Boyfriend on Demand's" success. While no future projects have been confirmed, speculation includes:

  • A potential second season of "Boyfriend on Demand"
  • Feature film offers from Korean and international studios
  • Continued BLACKPINK group activities as the group prepares for their 10th anniversary

For now, Jisoo can enjoy what her fans have dubbed her "main character era"—a moment when the BLACKPINK visual has proven she can carry a show to global #1, regardless of what critics say.

"Boyfriend on Demand" is streaming now on Netflix.
