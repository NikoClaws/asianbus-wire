McDonald's Launches 'KPop Demon Hunters' Meals — First-Ever Dueling Collab Features Saja Boys & HUNTR/X

By Soo-yeon Kim April 3, 2026

The "KPop Demon Hunters" global takeover just hit the drive-through. McDonald's has officially launched its collaboration with Netflix's Oscar-winning animated film, introducing two dueling meals, a brand-new McFlurry flavor, photocards, and exclusive fan content at participating locations nationwide.

The partnership — which the fast-food giant is calling a "Battle for the Fans" — marks McDonald's first-ever dueling adult meals, letting customers pick a side between the film's heroic girl group HUNTR/X and their demon-powered rivals, the Saja Boys.

Pick A Side: The Menu Breakdown

The Saja Boys Breakfast Meal leans into the boy band's fiery energy with a Spicy Saja McMuffin featuring a new Spicy Saja sauce, a hash brown, and a small "Soda Pop" soft drink — a nod to the film's chart-topping soundtrack hit of the same name.

The HUNTR/X Meal takes the dinner shift with Ramyeon McShaker Fries, a 10-piece Chicken McNuggets, and two limited-edition dipping sauces: Hunter Sauce and a purple Demon Sauce. The meal comes with a medium drink and packaging covered in exclusive artwork from the film.

Rounding out the collab is the Derpy McFlurry, inspired by the film's beloved comic-relief character. Details on the exact flavor are being kept under wraps, but early reports suggest a cookies-and-cream base with pink and purple swirl.

Photocards And Fan Content

In a move that feels ripped straight from K-pop album packaging, each meal comes with collectible photocards featuring HUNTR/X and Saja Boys characters. There are reportedly 12 cards total — six per faction — and fans are already organizing trading threads online.

The animated cast also appears in a new commercial, with Rumi (voiced by EJAE) singing the iconic McDonald's jingle at the end. The ad has already racked up millions of views on YouTube since dropping alongside the meal launch on March 31.

The 'KPop Demon Hunters' Phenomenon Keeps Growing

The McDonald's deal is just the latest chapter in the film's unprecedented cultural moment. Since premiering on Netflix, "KPop Demon Hunters" has swept two Academy Awards (Best Animated Feature and Best Original Song for "Golden"), topped the Billboard Hot 100, and spawned a confirmed sequel already in development.

Industry watchers are already comparing the collab's potential to the infamous McDonald's Szechuan Sauce craze sparked by "Rick and Morty" — except this time, the franchise is prepared with two full meal options and nationwide availability from day one.

The meals are available now at participating McDonald's locations for a limited time. A sequel film is currently in production at Netflix.
