BTS "ARIRANG" Sells 4 Million Copies In 3 Days — New Record In K-Pop History

March 23, 2026 8 min read By Selena Jung

BTS has done it again—and this time, they've done it bigger than ever before. The global supergroup's fifth studio album "ARIRANG" has sold over 4 million copies in just three days, setting a new record in K-Pop history and cementing the group's status as the undisputed kings of the industry.

According to data released by Hanteo Chart on March 23, BTS topped both the World Chart and Album Chart for the third week of March 2026. The achievement is particularly remarkable given that the album was only released on Friday, March 20—meaning the 4 million figure was achieved in a mere 72-hour window.

Dominating The Charts

The numbers are staggering. On the weekly World Chart, BTS earned first place with a World Index of 23,226.79 points, scoring high in both album and digital sales. To put the margin of their victory in perspective, second-place IVE achieved a World Index of 10,587.83 points—less than half of BTS's total. Choi Yena rounded out the top three with 9,960.42 points.

On the weekly Album Chart, "ARIRANG" recorded an album index of 6,372,629.00 points based on 4,006,610 copies sold in the third week of March. This represents not just a first-place finish, but a career-high in album sales for the group—a remarkable feat for artists who have been breaking records since their debut.

The gap between BTS and other artists on the Album Chart was even more pronounced. Second place went to All(H)Ours' fifth mini-album "NO DOUBT" with an album index of 97,189.20 points (80,991 copies sold). Third place was claimed by CORTIS's debut mini-album "COLOR OUTSIDE THE LINES" with 66,703.27 points (48,968 copies sold).

A Historic Return

"ARIRANG" marks BTS's first group album since their members completed their mandatory military service. The long-awaited reunion has been the subject of intense anticipation, with fans worldwide counting down the days until all seven members could perform together again.

The album's concept represents a bold artistic direction for the group. Drawing heavily on Korean traditional music and cultural themes, "ARIRANG" is named after the iconic Korean folk song that has been passed down through generations. The title track reimagines this classic melody through BTS's contemporary lens, blending traditional Korean instruments with modern production.

The album features 11 tracks in total, including the controversial "Aliens" by RM that has sparked debate about the group's use of English, as well as collaborative tracks that showcase each member's individual growth during their time apart.

Global Domination Continues

The album sales success is complemented by streaming numbers and global chart performance. "ARIRANG" has topped iTunes charts in over 100 countries, while the title track has been dominating Spotify's Global Top 50 since its release.

Perhaps most impressively, the Netflix livestream of BTS's Gwanghwamun comeback concert ranked number one in 77 countries, according to FlixPatrol data. This demonstrates that BTS's appeal extends far beyond album purchases—they remain a cultural phenomenon with the ability to command global attention.

The group's return has also reignited conversations about K-Pop's global impact. While some questioned whether BTS could maintain their dominance after years away from group activities, these sales figures provide a definitive answer.

Breaking Their Own Records

BTS has a history of breaking their own records with each release, but the 4 million copies in 3 days represents a significant leap even by their standards. Their previous first-week sales record was set by "Map of the Soul: 7" in 2020, which sold approximately 4.2 million copies in its first week.

"ARIRANG" has essentially matched that weekly total in less than half the time, suggesting final first-week numbers could reach unprecedented heights when the full data is compiled.

Industry analysts note that the extended hiatus may have actually intensified demand. "There's been a significant build-up of anticipation," one K-Pop market analyst explained. "Fans who might have spread their purchases across multiple releases instead concentrated their buying power on this single album. Add in new fans who discovered BTS during their hiatus through their solo work and the documentary, and you have perfect conditions for record-breaking sales."

The ARMY Effect

Central to BTS's success is their devoted fanbase, ARMY, who have organized purchasing campaigns to maximize chart impact. Social media has been flooded with fans sharing their album collections, bulk purchase receipts, and streaming parties aimed at pushing the group to new heights.

This organized fan effort has drawn both praise and criticism. Supporters argue it demonstrates genuine passion and community, while critics question whether bulk buying accurately reflects genuine demand. Regardless of one's position on fan purchasing practices, the numbers speak to BTS's unmatched ability to mobilize their fanbase.

ARMY's efforts extended beyond purchases. Fan-organized events in cities worldwide celebrated the album release, with viewing parties for the Gwanghwamun concert and album listening sessions bringing fans together in person after years of pandemic-era virtual connections.

Industry Impact

BTS's return has implications beyond their own career. HYBE, their parent company, has seen significant stock movement tied to the comeback. While shares fell following concerns about concert attendance, the album sales figures may provide a counterbalancing positive narrative.

The K-Pop industry as a whole benefits from BTS's success, which brings mainstream attention to Korean music and creates a rising tide effect for other artists. When BTS makes international headlines, it increases visibility for the entire genre.

Competitors are watching closely, too. Other major groups planning 2026 comebacks will be setting their benchmarks against these numbers, even as they recognize that BTS occupies a unique position in the market that isn't easily replicated.

What's Next

BTS is scheduled to embark on a world tour later this year, their first since before military service obligations began taking members away from group activities. If album sales are any indication, ticket demand will be astronomical.

The group is also expected to continue promotional activities for "ARIRANG," including music show performances and variety show appearances in Korea. International promotions may follow, though specific plans have not been announced.

For now, ARMY and the wider K-Pop community are celebrating what can only be described as a triumphant return. Four million copies in three days isn't just a number—it's a statement that BTS's reign is far from over.

BTS's "ARIRANG" is available now on all major streaming platforms and for physical purchase worldwide.
