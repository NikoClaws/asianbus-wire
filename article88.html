BTS 'SWIM' Debuts At No. 1 On Billboard Global 200 As ARIRANG Claims Historic Top 9

April 1, 2026 10 min read By Selena Jung

BTS has officially made Billboard chart history — again. The group's comeback single "SWIM" debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard Global 200, marking the South Korean septet's record-extending eighth chart-topper on the ranking. But the real story isn't just the No. 1 — it's what happened behind it: all nine tracks from their new album ARIRANG swept the top nine positions on the chart, tying Taylor Swift's all-time record.

Let that sink in for a moment. Nine songs. Nine consecutive chart positions. From No. 1 to No. 9, every single slot belongs to BTS. The last time anyone pulled this off was Swift herself, when songs from The Tortured Poets Department launched at Nos. 1 through 9 in May 2024. Before that, it had never been done at all.

For a group that spent the better part of four years away from music — scattered across military bases along the Korean peninsula — the achievement isn't just impressive. It's borderline absurd. And it raises a question that the music industry has been debating for years but can no longer avoid: Is BTS the biggest musical act of the 21st century?

The Full Chart Breakdown

According to Luminate data for the tracking week ending March 26 — the first full week following ARIRANG's March 20 release — BTS didn't just dominate the Global 200. They colonized it.

"SWIM" led the charge with 108.8 million streams and 221,000 units sold, numbers that would be remarkable for any artist's lead single but are particularly striking given BTS's extended absence. Behind it, "Body to Body" settled in at No. 2 with 65 million streams and 35,000 in sales, followed by "Hooligan" at No. 3 (50.2 million streams), "FYA" at No. 4 (48.2 million), and "Normal" rounding out the top five with 45.8 million streams.

The dominance continued through the back half of the top 10: "Aliens" at No. 6 (44.2 million streams), "Like Animals" at No. 7 (43.3 million), "2.0" at No. 8 (42.8 million), and "Merry Go Round" closing out the sweep at No. 9 with 40.7 million streams and 17,000 in sales.

What's particularly noteworthy is the consistency. The gap between the No. 2 and No. 9 tracks — roughly 24 million streams — is relatively narrow, suggesting that fans aren't just latching onto the lead single. They're consuming the entire album as a cohesive body of work, exactly as BTS intended it to be heard.

The Global Excl. U.S. Chart: Complete Monopoly

If the Global 200 numbers were impressive, the Billboard Global Excl. U.S. chart tells an even more dramatic story. On that chart — which strips out American data to measure purely international performance — BTS became the first act in chart history to monopolize the entire top 10 and top 13 simultaneously.

The achievement surpasses Taylor Swift's nine of the top 10 with The Life of a Showgirl in October 2025 and Bad Bunny's top-three lockdown earlier this year. It's a testament to BTS's genuinely global appeal — not just in the traditionally K-pop-friendly markets of East and Southeast Asia, but across Europe, Latin America, the Middle East, and beyond.

"People sometimes forget that BTS was building an international fanbase long before the Western music industry paid attention to K-pop," noted chart analyst Bryan Rolli. "They had massive fanbases in Brazil, India, Indonesia, and Turkey before they ever cracked the Billboard Hot 100. Those fans didn't go anywhere during the hiatus. They were just loading the spring."

The international numbers also underscore a subtle but important point about ARIRANG's appeal: despite being BTS's most Korean-leaning album in years — named after the country's unofficial national anthem, with heavier use of Korean lyrics than their recent English-language singles — the album's global performance hasn't suffered one bit. If anything, the cultural specificity seems to have enhanced its appeal.

How SWIM Conquered The World In Seven Days

"SWIM" itself deserves a closer look. The song's 108.8 million first-week streams represent BTS's biggest streaming debut for any single, surpassing the first-week numbers for previous hits like "Dynamite," "Butter," and "Permission to Dance." The track also moved 221,000 units in sales — a massive number in an era where single-track purchases have largely been replaced by streaming.

Musically, "SWIM" represents a departure from the bright, radio-friendly pop that characterized BTS's previous English-language singles. Built around a shimmering synth-pop foundation with production contributions from Kevin Parker of Tame Impala, the song layers RM and Suga's introspective Korean verses over a chorus that floats between languages. It's the kind of track that rewards repeated listening — which, judging by the streaming numbers, is exactly what fans have been doing.

The music video, which dropped simultaneously with the album, has already surpassed 300 million views on YouTube, making it one of the fastest K-pop videos to reach that milestone. The visual, directed by Lumpens — BTS's longtime creative collaborators — features the seven members in and around water in various states of submersion, a metaphor for diving back into their collective identity after years apart.

V's TikTok Challenge Adds Fuel To The Fire

Just when the streaming numbers seemed to be settling into a (still-massive) cruising altitude, member V decided to throw gasoline on the fire. On March 26, Kim Taehyung posted a short clip to his TikTok account (@tete_kimv) that would end up pushing "SWIM" streams to another level entirely.

The clip was disarmingly simple: V, captioning his post "#TikTokDifficult..It's hard," proceeded to launch himself across a bed in an exaggerated swimming motion, flailing his arms with the complete lack of self-consciousness that has made him one of K-pop's most beloved personalities. Within hours, fans were recreating the "SWIM challenge" worldwide.

The challenge has since been attempted by millions — from ARMY members flopping onto mattresses in their dorm rooms to professional swimmers doing the bit in Olympic-sized pools. Several other K-pop idols have joined in, and at least three NBA players posted their own versions. The organic virality of the challenge has pushed "SWIM" streams even higher heading into week two.

"This is what separates BTS from basically everyone else," one social media strategist noted on Twitter. "Their marketing isn't marketing. V didn't plan this. He was just being V. And that authenticity is worth more than any ad campaign."

ARMY's Organized Streaming Machine

Of course, viral TikTok challenges are one thing. ARMY's coordinated streaming operation is something else entirely. Within minutes of ARIRANG's release, fan-organized streaming guides were circulating in over 30 languages. Dedicated streaming accounts posted hourly updates on the album's performance across Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, and regional platforms like Melon and Genie in South Korea.

The operation ran — and continues to run — with the kind of logistical precision that would make a military commander jealous. (The irony, given BTS's recent military service, is not lost on anyone.) Time-zone-based streaming shifts ensured that ARIRANG maintained consistent play counts around the clock. Asian ARMY would stream during their waking hours, then hand off to European fans, who passed the baton to the Americas.

It's tempting to dismiss this as artificial inflation, but the reality is more nuanced. ARMY's streaming efforts amplify genuine interest — they don't create it from nothing. The fact that casual listeners are also driving the album's numbers is evidenced by the strong showing across tracks that aren't typically the focus of organized streaming campaigns.

Records Set This Week: A Summary

For those keeping score at home — and ARMY always keeps score — here's the full tally of records BTS set or tied with ARIRANG's first tracking week:

BTS now holds a record-extending eighth No. 1 on both the Global 200 and Global Excl. U.S. charts. The top-nine sweep on the Global 200 ties Taylor Swift's all-time record. The top-13 monopoly on the Global Excl. U.S. chart is a first for any artist, ever. ARIRANG simultaneously debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 albums chart. And "SWIM" alone logged 108.8 million streams in a single tracking week.

These aren't incremental improvements on existing records. Several of them represent entirely new territory that no artist — in any genre, from any country — has ever reached before.

What The Numbers Mean For K-Pop

BTS's chart dominance has implications that extend well beyond their own career. The ARIRANG era is, in a sense, a proof of concept for the global viability of Korean-language music at the very highest commercial level. While BTS has always been a K-pop act, their recent pre-hiatus releases leaned heavily on English-language singles to crack Western charts. ARIRANG's success, with its Korean-forward approach, suggests that the market has evolved.

Other K-pop acts will be watching these numbers closely. If a deeply Korean album can sweep the global charts, it changes the calculus for every label deciding how much English to include in their artists' releases. The old conventional wisdom — that you need English-language tracks to compete internationally — may finally be dead.

"ARIRANG proves that authenticity sells," said one K-pop industry executive who spoke on background. "The global audience is more sophisticated than we gave them credit for. They don't need music translated for them. They need music that feels real."

With V's TikTok challenge showing no signs of slowing down, ARMY's streaming operations running at full capacity, and the ARIRANG world tour set to kick off in Seoul in May, BTS's grip on the global charts could extend well into the summer. The comeback era, it seems, is just getting started.

BTS's 82-date world tour begins May 2026 in Seoul. ARIRANG is available on all major streaming platforms.
