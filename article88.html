BTS 'SWIM' Debuts At No. 1 On Billboard Global 200 As ARIRANG Claims Historic Top 9

The reunited septet ties Taylor Swift's record with nine consecutive top-10 entries and becomes the first act ever to monopolize the entire top 13 on the Global Excl. U.S. chart.

BTS promotional image for ARIRANG. Photo: BIGHIT MUSIC

BTS has officially made Billboard chart history — again. The group's comeback single "SWIM" debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard Global 200, marking the South Korean septet's record-extending eighth chart-topper on the ranking. But the real story is what happened behind it: all nine tracks from their new album ARIRANG swept the top nine positions on the chart.

The achievement ties Taylor Swift's record for the most top-10 entries in a single week and the most titles occupying consecutive positions from No. 1 down, set when songs from The Tortured Poets Department launched at Nos. 1 through 9 in May 2024.

The Full Chart Breakdown

According to Luminate data for the tracking week ending March 26 — the first full week following ARIRANG's March 20 release — here's how BTS dominated the Global 200:

  • No. 1 "SWIM" — 108.8M streams / 221K sold
  • No. 2 "Body to Body" — 65M streams / 35K sold
  • No. 3 "Hooligan" — 50.2M streams / 21K sold
  • No. 4 "FYA" — 48.2M streams / 20K sold
  • No. 5 "Normal" — 45.8M streams / 21K sold
  • No. 6 "Aliens" — 44.2M streams / 19K sold
  • No. 7 "Like Animals" — 43.3M streams / 20K sold
  • No. 8 "2.0" — 42.8M streams / 18K sold
  • No. 9 "Merry Go Round" — 40.7M streams / 17K sold

Even Bigger Numbers Outside The U.S.

On the Billboard Global Excl. U.S. chart, BTS went even further — becoming the first act in chart history to monopolize the entire top 10 and top 13 simultaneously. The achievement surpasses Taylor Swift's nine of the top 10 with The Life of a Showgirl in October 2025 and Bad Bunny's top three lockdown earlier this year.

BTS Billboard Records Set This Week:

• 8th No. 1 on both Global 200 and Global Excl. U.S. (record-extending)
• Top 9 sweep on Global 200 (ties Taylor Swift's record)
• Top 13 monopoly on Global Excl. U.S. (first act ever)
• ARIRANG simultaneously debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 albums chart
• "SWIM" alone: 108.8 million streams in one week

V's TikTok Keeps The Momentum Going

Adding fuel to the fire, member V (Kim Taehyung) sparked a viral TikTok challenge over the weekend with a playful clip promoting "SWIM." In the chaotic video, posted to his official TikTok account (@tete_kimv) on March 26, V captioned the clip "#TikTokDifficult..It's hard" as he dove across a bed in an imitation of swimming. The clip has since been recreated by millions of fans worldwide, with "SWIM challenge" trending globally on the platform.

The fan-driven challenge has pushed "SWIM" streams even higher heading into week two, with ARMY mobilizing streaming parties across time zones to maintain the song's chart position.

ARIRANG's First Full Week By The Numbers

ARIRANG represents BTS's first studio album of entirely new material since 2020's "BE," and the group's first release since all seven members completed their mandatory military service. BIGHIT MUSIC announced the album alongside an 82-date world tour in January 2026, with the album finally dropping on March 20.

The combined streaming and sales numbers paint a picture of a comeback that has exceeded even the most bullish industry expectations. "SWIM" alone racked up 108.8 million official global streams in its first tracking week — a massive number that puts it among the biggest debut weeks for any song in 2026.

With V's TikTok challenge showing no signs of slowing down and ARMY's organized streaming efforts continuing at full force, BTS's grip on the global charts could extend well beyond the initial tracking period. The group's world tour kicks off in Seoul in May, promising to keep the ARIRANG era at the center of the K-pop conversation for months to come.
