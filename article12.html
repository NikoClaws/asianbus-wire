ILLIT Wonhee's Visuals Maliciously Mocked In Viral Tweet Linked To NewJeans Fans

March 17, 2026 By Chloe Nam-Davis

The ongoing tensions between HYBE's artist fandoms have reached a disturbing new low after ILLIT member Wonhee became the target of viral mockery on social media, with evidence suggesting the harassment originated from accounts associated with NewJeans' fanbase. The incident has reignited fierce debates about cyberbullying, fandom toxicity, and the responsibility of agencies to protect their artists.

The controversy began when a tweet featuring manipulated images of Wonhee, accompanied by mocking commentary about her appearance, began circulating widely on social media platform X (formerly Twitter). The tweet, which has since been removed for violating platform guidelines, garnered tens of thousands of engagements before its deletion, with many users sharing and amplifying its cruel content.

Digital forensics conducted by concerned fans revealed that the original poster and many of the most engaged accounts spreading the content were connected to NewJeans fan communities, identified through their posting history, following patterns, and previous interactions. This connection has intensified the already fraught relationship between ILLIT and NewJeans supporters.

The Nature of the Attack

The viral tweet reportedly contained side-by-side comparisons designed to mock Wonhee's facial features, with captions using derogatory language to describe her appearance. Additional posts in the thread allegedly compared her unfavorably to other female idols and used editing techniques to exaggerate certain features for comedic ridicule.

Screenshots preserved before the deletion show comments calling Wonhee various degrading names and suggesting she was "unqualified" to be an idol based on her looks. The posts also made references to the ongoing HYBE-ADOR conflict, positioning the mockery as a form of retaliation for perceived wrongs against NewJeans.

What made the incident particularly harmful was the coordinated nature of the spread. Multiple accounts appeared to share the content simultaneously, using specific hashtags designed to maximize visibility. Some posts were translated into multiple languages to ensure international reach.

ILLITzens Respond With Protection Campaigns

ILLIT's fanbase, known as ILLITzens, responded swiftly to the attack with organized counter-campaigns. Fans flooded social media with supportive messages, positive images of Wonhee, and the hashtag #WonheeIsBeautiful, which trended in multiple countries within hours of the incident.

"Wonhee is a 19-year-old girl who has done nothing wrong. She didn't ask to debut under HYBE. She didn't ask to be caught up in company politics. She's just trying to live her dream, and these people are trying to destroy her confidence for something completely out of her control." — @ILLITProtect, fan protection account

Fan sites dedicated to Wonhee released statements condemning the harassment and urging BELIFT LAB, ILLIT's management company, to take legal action. Several fans reported the original accounts to both platform moderators and Korean cyber crime authorities, with some claiming to have filed formal police reports.

The protective response extended beyond ILLIT's immediate fandom, with fans of other groups expressing solidarity and condemning the attack. Many pointed out that regardless of inter-fandom disputes, targeting a young woman's appearance crosses fundamental ethical lines.

The HYBE-NewJeans Conflict Context

To understand the severity of this incident, context about the broader conflict is essential. ILLIT debuted in March 2024 under BELIFT LAB, a HYBE subsidiary. Almost immediately, comparisons to NewJeans emerged, with some accusing HYBE of deliberately creating ILLIT as a "replacement" for NewJeans amid the company's escalating dispute with ADOR and its former CEO Min Hee-jin.

The situation intensified dramatically throughout 2024 and 2025, with legal battles, public accusations, and eventually NewJeans' acrimonious departure from HYBE. Throughout this period, some NewJeans supporters viewed ILLIT as a symbol of everything wrong with HYBE's handling of their favorite group.

This hostility has manifested in various forms, from mass disliking of ILLIT's content to harassment campaigns against individual members. Wonhee has been a particularly frequent target, though all five ILLIT members have faced some degree of antagonism linked to the conflict.

NewJeans Fans Push Back

It's important to note that many NewJeans supporters, known as Bunnies, have distanced themselves from the harassment and condemned the viral tweet. Prominent fan accounts posted statements emphasizing that the attackers do not represent the broader fandom.

"Real Bunnies support NewJeans by streaming their music, buying their albums, and showing love. We don't tear down other artists. Anyone engaging in this kind of behavior is not one of us and should not claim to act in our name." — Statement from major Bunnie fan coalition

However, others have pointed out that the NewJeans fandom has struggled to police its more extreme members, with harassment of ILLIT being a recurring problem that has never been adequately addressed. Critics argue that the community's general tolerance of anti-ILLIT sentiment, even when stopping short of direct attacks, creates an environment where more extreme behavior can flourish.

BELIFT LAB's Response

BELIFT LAB released a statement approximately 24 hours after the incident gained widespread attention. The statement was notably more forceful than typical K-Pop agency responses to fan conflicts:

"We are aware of the malicious posts targeting our artist Wonhee and have already begun taking legal action. We have collected evidence and submitted reports to relevant authorities. We will pursue all perpetrators to the fullest extent of the law, regardless of their location or fandom affiliation. Our artists' wellbeing and dignity are non-negotiable."

The agency also confirmed they had been in contact with Wonhee and other ILLIT members to provide support. While not disclosing details, they acknowledged the incident had affected the members emotionally and stated that appropriate measures were being taken to protect their mental health.

Industry Reactions

The incident has drawn commentary from various industry observers and fellow artists. Several K-Pop idols liked or shared posts condemning the harassment, though none have made direct public statements—a common approach given the professional risks of inserting oneself into fandom conflicts.

Mental health advocates in the Korean entertainment industry have used the incident to call for stronger protections for young artists. "These are teenagers and young adults being subjected to mass psychological abuse," said one expert who works with idol trainees. "The industry needs systematic approaches to protecting artists from this kind of targeted harassment."

Platform responsibility has also been questioned, with critics noting that the viral tweet remained active for hours despite clearly violating terms of service. Some have called for social media companies to implement faster response systems for coordinated harassment campaigns targeting public figures.

Wonhee's Silence

Throughout the controversy, Wonhee has maintained silence on her personal social media accounts. Her last post, shared the day before the incident, was a cheerful behind-the-scenes photo from a recent schedule—a stark contrast to the storm that would follow.

Fans have expressed concern about the psychological impact on the young idol, noting that she has seemed less active on platforms where fans can observe her mood. Some have urged fellow supporters to give her space while continuing to flood her tags with positive messages she might see.

ILLIT's group activities appear to be continuing as scheduled, with the members recently completing a magazine photoshoot and preparing for their next comeback. Whether the incident will affect Wonhee's public appearances or the group's promotional strategies remains to be seen.

A Larger Pattern

This incident is unfortunately not an isolated case but part of a larger pattern of female idols being subjected to appearance-based harassment. The K-Pop industry's emphasis on visual perfection creates an environment where such attacks are particularly damaging, as they strike at the very qualities idols are often judged by.

As the industry continues to grapple with toxic fandom behavior, many are calling for a fundamental reassessment of how conflicts between artist communities are allowed to escalate. Without meaningful consequences for perpetrators and better protection systems for targets, incidents like this will continue to traumatize young artists caught in the crossfire of disputes they never asked to be part of.
